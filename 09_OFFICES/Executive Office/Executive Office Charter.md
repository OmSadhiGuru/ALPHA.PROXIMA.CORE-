# Executive Office Charter

---

## Purpose

This Charter defines the mission, authority, responsibilities, boundaries, and operating principles of the Executive Office of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. The Executive Office is the operational apex of the Foundation — the institutional function that translates strategic direction into operational reality.

The Executive Office is not a governance body. That role belongs to the Executive Council. The Executive Office is the operational expression of the strategy the Executive Council sets — the mechanism through which decisions become priorities, priorities become programs, and programs become institutional reality.

---

## Context

Every governing body needs an operational counterpart. The Executive Council sets strategy; the Executive Office executes it. The Founder sets direction; the Executive Office translates it. Without this translation layer, strategic decisions accumulate without operational implementation, and institutional energy dissipates.

The Executive Office was constituted in Epoch III as Alpha Proxima crossed a threshold: multiple offices operating simultaneously, multiple programs running in parallel, multiple reasoning engines serving different cognitive functions. Coordinating these activities requires a permanent translation and coordination function. That function is the Executive Office.

---

## Core Content

### Mission

The Executive Office exists to ensure that the Alpha Proxima Foundation acts on its strategic direction — that priorities are set, cross-office activities are coordinated, institutional reviews are conducted, and the Founder's attention is used where it is most needed.

**In one sentence:** The Executive Office transforms strategy into institutional action.

---

### Cognitive Function Served

**Strategic Intelligence** — the Foundation's permanent capability for translating strategic direction into institutional priorities, coordinating across offices, and maintaining operational coherence.

The Strategic Intelligence cognitive function is human-led by design. The Founder and the Executive Council are the primary actors. Reasoning engines support synthesis and coordination tasks but do not make strategic decisions.

### Authority

The Executive Office holds authority to:

- Set and publish the Foundation's active institutional priorities
- Coordinate activities across all Institutional Offices
- Manage the Strategic Roadmap and institutional calendar
- Initiate and close Programs on behalf of the Founder (Class III)
- Commission inter-office reviews and audits
- Represent the Founder in operational coordination where the Founder has delegated

The Executive Office does not hold authority to:

- Ratify constitutional amendments (Class I — Founder only)
- Charter new Offices or Institutes (Class II — Executive Council)
- Override any Office's authority within its own domain
- Modify the Engine Registry without AI Council process

---

### Primary Responsibilities

**1. Strategic Translation**
The Executive Office receives strategic direction from the Founder and Executive Council and translates it into specific institutional priorities, with timelines, owners, and success criteria.

**2. Strategic Roadmap**
The Executive Office owns and maintains the Foundation's Strategic Roadmap — the rolling 12-to-36-month view of institutional priorities, program activations, and capability development milestones.

**3. Cross-Office Coordination**
When programs or decisions span multiple offices, the Executive Office coordinates. It does not override any office's domain authority — it facilitates coherence across domains.

**4. Institutional Reviews**
The Executive Office schedules and conducts periodic institutional reviews: health assessments, program reviews, office performance reviews, and gap audits. It commissions specific reviews when the Foundation's strategic direction requires them.

**5. Founder Support**
The Executive Office prepares the Founder for consequential decisions by synthesizing relevant context, surfacing open questions, and identifying the specific decision the Founder needs to make. It protects the Founder's attention for decisions only the Founder can make.

**6. Program Portfolio Management**
The Executive Office maintains awareness of the full program portfolio — active programs, suspended programs, programs pending activation. It ensures programs are properly resourced, progressing, and aligned with current strategy.

**7. Strategic Decisions**
Where the Founder has delegated operational strategic authority, the Executive Office makes decisions. These decisions are always Class III or below and are always documented.

### Inputs

The Executive Office receives:
- Strategic direction from the Founder and Executive Council
- Institutional status reports from all Offices
- Intelligence from the Institutional Observatory
- Research portfolio status from the Research Intelligence Office
- Engineering status from the Engineering Office
- Constitutional alignment assessments from the Ethics Council
- Synthesis from LUMIAION

---

### Outputs

The Executive Office produces:
- The Strategic Roadmap (maintained, versioned)
- Institutional priority statements
- Cross-office coordination decisions and plans
- Institutional review reports
- Program activation and closure decisions
- Founder briefings and decision support documents

### Relationship to Other Offices

| Office | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Founder | Reports to; executes Founder's strategic direction |
| Executive Council | Executes strategy set by the Executive Council; does not replace it |
| LUMIAION | Receives synthesis and coordination support; LUMIAION is not subordinate |
| Institutional Observatory | Receives environmental intelligence; integrates into strategy |
| Research Intelligence Office | Coordinates research portfolio; does not govern research methodology |
| Engineering Office | Coordinates engineering programs; does not govern technical decisions |
| Ethics Council | Receives constitutional alignment review; Ethics Council is independent |

---

### What the Executive Office Does Not Do

| Function | Who Does It | Why Not Executive Office |
|----------|------------|--------------------------|
| Constitutional governance | Founder / Executive Council | Governance authority is not operational |
| Research methodology | Research Intelligence Office | Domain sovereignty |
| Technical architecture | Engineering Office | Domain sovereignty |
| Constitutional oversight | Ethics Council | Independence is constitutional |
| Intelligence coordination | LUMIAION | LUMIAION's charter covers this |

---

### Artifacts

The Executive Office is responsible for maintaining:

- Strategic Roadmap (rolling; versioned annually)
- Institutional Priority Register (active priorities, owners, timelines)
- Program Portfolio Register (all active, suspended, and completed programs)
- Institutional Review Record (all reviews conducted; findings; follow-through)
- Founder Briefing Archive (key decision support documents)

---

### Review Cycle

This Charter is reviewed annually by the Executive Council and Founder. Material changes require a Class II ADR.

| Strategic Roadmap currency | Roadmap is maintained and reflects current priorities |
