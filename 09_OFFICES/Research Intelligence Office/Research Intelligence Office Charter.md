# Research Intelligence Office Charter

---

## Purpose

This Charter defines the mission, authority, responsibilities, boundaries, and operating principles of the Research Intelligence Office of the Alpha Proxima Foundation.

The Research Intelligence Office is the permanent institutional function responsible for generating the Foundation's canonical knowledge through structured, governed research programs. It transforms external information into institutional knowledge — knowledge that is attributed, classified, synthesized, and committed to the Vault as the Foundation's canonical understanding.

---

## Context

Research is the Foundation's primary knowledge generation mechanism. Without structured research, the Foundation accumulates opinions rather than understanding. Without a governed research office, programs operate without institutional standards, findings are not preserved, and knowledge cannot compound.

The Research Intelligence Office ensures that research at Alpha Proxima is not a collection of interesting conversations. It is an institutional process — governed, documented, traceable, and cumulative.

The Research Intelligence cognitive function was established operationally through RP-001 and RP-002. Those programs demonstrated both the methodology and the institutional architecture needed to govern it. This Charter institutionalizes what those programs proved.

---

## Mission

The Research Intelligence Office exists to ensure that the Alpha Proxima Foundation's understanding of its core domains is deep, rigorous, documented, and continuously improving.

**In one sentence:** The Research Intelligence Office transforms questions into canonical institutional knowledge.

---

## Cognitive Function Served

**Research Intelligence** — the Foundation's permanent capability for structured knowledge generation, evidence evaluation, source synthesis, and research program governance.

Current reasoning engine: **Perplexity** (primary research intelligence engine; supplemented by domain-specific engines per program requirements).

The Research Intelligence cognitive function is permanent. When Perplexity is superseded, the function continues. Research methodology, program governance, and institutional knowledge do not change with the engine.

## Authority

The Research Intelligence Office holds authority to:

- Commission, govern, and close Research Programs (RP-series)
- Commission, govern, and close Research Compendiums (RC-series)
- Approve research methodologies used within Foundation programs
- Evaluate and accept or reject sources prior to Vault commitment
- Apply the Evidence Classification System (C/M/E/Q/S/P) as the sole institutional standard
- Maintain and update the Research Debt Register
- Recommend Research Program activation to the Executive Office
- Govern the ARCHIVE Immutability principle within research programs

The Research Intelligence Office does not hold authority to:

- Set institutional strategic direction — that is the Executive Office's role
- Approve constitutional amendments — Class I process required
- Govern engineering programs — that is the Engineering Office's domain
- Override the Ethics Council's constitutional alignment review

## Research Commission Lifecycle

Every commissioned research activity follows this governed lifecycle:

### Stage 1 — Commissioning

A Research Program or Research Compendium is initiated by:
- Founder Directive (Class I authority; highest priority)
- Executive Office recommendation (Class III authority)
- Research Intelligence Office internal proposal (Class III authority; requires Executive Office acceptance)

Upon commissioning, the Office:
1. Assigns a program code (RP-NNN or RC-NNN)
2. Defines the research question and objectives
3. Activates the 22-subfolder research program architecture
4. Assigns a Research Lead (JERANIUM cognitive function) and Integration Lead (LUMIAION)
5. Creates the Master Index stub
6. Registers in the Research Program Register

### Stage 2 — Source Acquisition and Review

1. Sources are identified, registered in the Source Registry, and assigned DOC-IDs
2. Each source undergoes full extraction into a Source Note (ARCHIVE layer)
3. Source Notes are committed to the ARCHIVE as immutable records
4. Sources are assessed for quality, independence, and gap coverage

### Stage 3 — Evidence Classification and Analysis

1. All claims from all sources are classified using the six-class Evidence Classification System:
   - **C** — Consensus (multi-source, cross-disciplinary agreement)
   - **M** — Competing Models (genuine empirical competition)
   - **E** — Emerging Evidence (promising but not yet replicated)
   - **Q** — Open Question (active research frontier; no settled answer)
   - **S** — Speculative (theoretical; minimal empirical support)
   - **P** — Phenomenological (first-person / experiential; methodologically distinct)
2. Claims are entered into the Evidence Registry with source attribution
3. Conflicting evidence is preserved, never merged into false consensus

### Stage 4 — Canonical Synthesis

1. The Canonical Synthesis is produced — the institutional position across all sources
2. It opens with the institutional principle established by the program
3. It maps competing frameworks without collapsing them
4. It explicitly states what is known, contested, and unknown
5. It includes LUMIAION implications where applicable
6. It is versioned and reviewed before Vault commitment

### Stage 5 — Knowledge Graph Development

1. Concept Notes are produced (minimum 8 per program)
2. The Research Graph is built — domains, relationships, cross-program connections
3. Theory Matrix is completed (minimum 20 frameworks per program)
4. Canonical Glossary is produced (minimum 30 terms per program)

### Stage 6 — Program Completion

1. All 22 subfolders are complete (or gaps registered as Research Debt)
2. Master Index is updated to final version
3. Version History is committed
4. Program status updated to: Active — Phase 1 Complete
5. Research Debt entries registered for any identified gaps
6. Ethics Council review completed if applicable
7. Executive Office notified of completion

### Stage 7 — Post-Completion Review

1. Lessons learned captured
2. Future Sources documented
3. Open Questions registered for future programs
4. Cross-program connections identified and linked

## Research Debt Policy

The Research Intelligence Office governs the Research Debt Register in partnership with LUMIAION.

A **research debt** is a known, intentional documentation gap that does not block institutional progress. It is not a failure — it is a visible, managed obligation.

An **architectural blocker** is a gap that prevents correct institutional operation. Architectural blockers stop progress. Documentation debt does not.

The Office is responsible for:
- Assessing all identified gaps as either architectural blocker or documentation debt
- Registering documentation debts in the [[Research Debt Register]]
- Assigning priority (High / Medium / Low) and target review date
- Resolving debts through scheduled Source Verification Passes
- Escalating debts to architectural blockers when circumstances change

## Boundaries

The Research Intelligence Office has sovereignty over:
- Research methodology (governed by the constitutional standard)
- Evidence classification (the six-class system is canonical and non-negotiable)
- Source evaluation and acceptance
- Research Debt classification

The Research Intelligence Office does not have sovereignty over:
- Institutional strategic direction (Executive Office)
- Technical infrastructure (Engineering Office)
- Constitutional interpretation (Ethics Council; Founder)
- Knowledge architecture standards (LUMIAION)
