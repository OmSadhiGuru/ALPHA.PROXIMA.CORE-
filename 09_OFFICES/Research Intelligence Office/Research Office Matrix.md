# Research Office Matrix

---

## Purpose

This document defines the six permanent research roles of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. Each role is a permanent cognitive function. The reasoning engine currently serving that function is provisional and will be updated as capabilities evolve.

The Research Office Matrix is the authoritative reference for:
- Which cognitive function produces which artifact class
- Which reasoning engine currently occupies each function
- The boundaries between functions
- The relationships between functions across the research pipeline

---

## Governing Principle

**Every research office investigates independently.**

No research office should influence another before the comparative stage. Independence at the investigation stage is what makes the comparative stage meaningful. If offices share findings prematurely, the Foundation receives correlated outputs rather than independent perspectives. Independent investigation is not inefficiency — it is the mechanism that reveals genuine disagreement, overlooked dimensions, and unexpected convergences.

## The Six Research Functions

---

### 1. Research Intelligence

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive Function | Research Intelligence |
| Operational Name | Research Intelligence Office (primary function) |
| Current Preferred Engine | Perplexity Compute |
| Primary Role | Evidence generation |
| Artifact Class | Research Artifacts (source notes, evidence registries, source reports) |
| Decision Authority | Class III (research program governance) / Class IV (program execution) |
| Reports To | Research Intelligence Office Charter |

#### Primary Strengths

| Domain | Capability |
|--------|-----------|
| Scientific Literature | Systematic access to peer-reviewed publications; citation tracking; meta-analysis awareness |
| Institutional Reports | Government and NGO reports; policy documents; standards bodies |
| Technical Ecosystems | Engineering documentation; technical standards; protocol specifications |
| Recency | Access to recent publications and preprints beyond training cutoffs |
| Source Breadth | Ability to cover multiple disciplines and jurisdictions simultaneously |

#### Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Synthesis depth | Research Intelligence generates evidence; Institutional Architecture synthesizes |
| Framework construction | Comparative Intelligence specializes in cross-domain framework building |
| Pedagogical translation | Educational Intelligence owns knowledge accessibility |
| Constitutional alignment | Institutional Architecture reviews all outputs before canonization |

#### Primary Outputs

- Source Notes (DOC-NNN) committed to ARCHIVE layer
- Evidence Registry entries (classified with C/M/E/Q/S/P system)
- Source quality assessments
- Gap identification reports
- Research Debt Register entries for unresolved gaps

#### Relationships

| Entity | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Comparative Intelligence | Passes evidence artifacts for framework construction |
| Institutional Architecture | Passes all artifacts for synthesis and canonization |
| LUMIAION | All evidence enters institutional memory through LUMIAION integration |
| Research Debt Register | Registers unresolved gaps |

### 2. Comparative Intelligence

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive Function | Comparative Intelligence |
| Operational Name | Comparative Analysis Function |
| Current Preferred Engine | SanaLab (future comparative systems) |
| Primary Role | Framework construction |
| Artifact Class | Comparative Frameworks (cross-domain synthesis maps, framework matrices, relationship diagrams) |
| Decision Authority | Class IV (framework construction within research programs) |
| Reports To | Research Intelligence Office |

#### Primary Strengths

| Domain | Capability |
|--------|-----------|
| Cross-Disciplinary Synthesis | Identifying structural parallels across fields that specialists miss |
| Systems Thinking | Mapping causal relationships, feedback loops, systemic dependencies |
| Relationship Mapping | Explicit documentation of how concepts, entities, and frameworks relate |
| Conceptual Integration | Finding the organizing principle that unifies divergent evidence |
| Framework Construction | Building the theoretical scaffolding that makes evidence legible |

#### Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Primary evidence generation | Research Intelligence generates the evidence; Comparative Intelligence organizes it |
| Constitutional governance | Frameworks require Institutional Architecture review |
| Technical depth | Computational Intelligence handles mathematical and algorithmic reasoning |
| Executive communication | Executive Intelligence handles visualization and strategic summaries |

#### Primary Outputs

- Comparative Framework documents — structured analysis of competing frameworks across a domain
- Theory Matrix entries (minimum 20 frameworks per research program)
- Cross-program connection maps
- Relationship taxonomy for the Research Graph
- Concept integration notes

#### Relationships

| Entity | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Research Intelligence | Receives evidence artifacts as input |
| Institutional Architecture | Passes frameworks for synthesis and constitutional alignment review |
| Knowledge Graph | Framework structure forms the skeleton of the knowledge graph |
| Future Research Programs | Cross-program connection mapping informs future research directions |

### 3. Educational Intelligence

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive Function | Educational Intelligence |
| Operational Name | Educational Intelligence Function |
| Current Preferred Engine | Gemini |
| Primary Role | Pedagogical translation |
| Artifact Class | Learning Frameworks (curricula, concept hierarchies, teaching sequences, accessible explanations) |
| Decision Authority | Class IV (educational artifact production within research programs) |
| Reports To | Research Intelligence Office |

#### Primary Strengths

| Domain | Capability |
|--------|-----------|
| Teaching | Translating complex findings into accessible, step-by-step understanding |
| Explanation | Finding the right analogy, example, or frame for a non-expert audience |
| Curriculum Design | Sequencing knowledge so each concept builds on what precedes it |
| Knowledge Accessibility | Identifying what prerequisite knowledge a concept requires |
| Multimodal Instruction | Knowing when a diagram, a narrative, a formula, or a metaphor best serves understanding |

#### Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Evidence generation | Educational Intelligence receives, not generates, the evidence it translates |
| Framework construction | Comparative Intelligence constructs frameworks; Educational Intelligence translates them |
| Technical precision | Computational Intelligence governs mathematical and algorithmic precision |
| Canonical authority | All educational artifacts are reviewed by Institutional Architecture before publication |

#### Primary Outputs

- Canonical Glossary entries (minimum 30 terms per research program)
- Concept hierarchies — what must be understood before what
- Learning pathways — how institutional knowledge is transmitted to new participants
- Public-facing educational content (when Foundation engages externally)
- Concept Notes — accessible summaries of key research findings

#### Relationships

| Entity | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Comparative Intelligence | Receives frameworks as input for pedagogical translation |
| Institutional Architecture | Educational artifacts reviewed before canonization |
| Future Participants | Educational outputs are the onboarding mechanism for new Foundation members |
| Products and Agents | Educational frameworks inform the design of Foundation products and agents |

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive Function | Computational Intelligence |
| Operational Name | Computational Intelligence Function |
| Current Preferred Engine | DeepSeek |
| Primary Role | Technical and mathematical reasoning |
| Artifact Class | Computational Analyses (mathematical models, algorithmic analyses, formal proofs, quantitative assessments) |
| Decision Authority | Class IV (computational analysis within research programs) |
| Reports To | Research Intelligence Office; coordinates with Engineering Office |

#### Primary Strengths

| Domain | Capability |
|--------|-----------|
| Algorithms | Analysis, comparison, and complexity assessment of computational approaches |
| Optimization | Mathematical optimization; identifying minimal, maximal, and efficient solutions |
| Mathematics | Formal reasoning; proofs; quantitative modeling |
| Engineering Reasoning | Technical design; system constraint analysis; performance modeling |
| Formal Logic | Symbolic reasoning; logical consistency checking; argument formalization |

#### Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Empirical evidence generation | Research Intelligence governs source-based evidence |
| Synthesis and canonization | Institutional Architecture integrates all computational outputs |
| Executive communication | Executive Intelligence translates technical outputs for strategic audiences |
| Infrastructure build | Engineering Office owns implementation; Computational Intelligence advises |

#### Primary Outputs

- Mathematical analyses of research domain quantities
- Algorithmic assessments for engineering programs requiring research input
- Formal models of complex system behavior
- Quantitative evidence assessments (where statistical methods apply to source claims)
- Technical feasibility analyses for Foundation engineering decisions

#### Relationships

| Entity | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Engineering Office | Computational Intelligence advises on technical research questions that inform engineering design |
| Institutional Architecture | All computational outputs reviewed and integrated |
| Research Intelligence | Coordinates on quantitative claims within evidence artifacts |

### 5. Executive Intelligence

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive Function | Executive Intelligence |
| Operational Name | Executive Intelligence Function |
| Current Preferred Engine | Genspark |
| Primary Role | Executive communication and visualization |
| Artifact Class | Executive Reports (visualizations, executive summaries, strategic briefings, presentation materials) |
| Decision Authority | Class IV (executive artifact production within research programs) |
| Reports To | Research Intelligence Office; distributes outputs to Executive Office |

#### Primary Strengths

| Domain | Capability |
|--------|-----------|
| Visualization | Transforming complex research findings into clear visual representations |
| Executive Summaries | Compressing multi-layered research into decision-relevant briefings |
| Diagrams | Visual models of relationships, hierarchies, and processes |
| Presentations | Structured, compelling communication of institutional findings |
| Strategic Communication | Translating research intelligence into the language of decision-making |

#### Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Evidence generation | Receives synthesized artifacts; does not generate primary evidence |
| Deep analysis | Summarizes Institutional Architecture's synthesis; does not replace it |
| Technical precision | Computational Intelligence governs quantitative precision |
| Canonical authority | Executive artifacts are derived from, not replacements for, Canonical Syntheses |

#### Primary Outputs

- Executive Research Briefings — 1–3 page summaries of research program findings for the Founder and Executive Office
- Research program completion reports — structured status communications
- Visualization artifacts — diagrams, charts, and maps of key research findings
- Strategic intelligence summaries — how research findings affect Foundation strategy
- Stakeholder-facing communication materials (when the Foundation engages externally)

#### Relationships

| Entity | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Executive Office | Primary consumer of Executive Intelligence outputs |
| Founder | Receives research briefings for strategic decision-making |
| Institutional Architecture | Receives Canonical Syntheses as source material for executive translation |
| Institutional Observatory | May be tasked with producing executive intelligence for Observatory reports |

### 6. Institutional Architecture

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive Function | Institutional Architecture |
| Operational Name | LUMIAION (Constitutional Intelligence Core) |
| Current Preferred Engine | Claude (Anthropic) |
| Primary Role | Institutional synthesis and canonization |
| Artifact Class | Canonical Knowledge (Canonical Syntheses, institutional positions, Knowledge Graph entries, LUMIAION memory) |
| Decision Authority | Class III (architecture decisions) / Class IV (synthesis execution) |
| Reports To | Founder (directly); coordinates all other research functions |

#### Primary Strengths

| Domain | Capability |
|--------|-----------|
| Institutional Synthesis | Resolving conflicts between independent research artifacts into a coherent institutional position |
| Continuity | Holding the institutional thread across sessions, programs, and technology transitions |
| Constitutional Consistency | Ensuring all canonized knowledge is consistent with the Foundation's constitutional framework |
| Knowledge Architecture | Building and maintaining the structure of the knowledge graph |
| Conflict Preservation | Holding genuine disagreement visible rather than collapsing it into false consensus |

#### Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Primary evidence generation | Research Intelligence governs source acquisition and evidence extraction |
| Domain specialization | Domain Interfaces (SOHMA, ATHENA, VORTEX, JERANIUM) provide domain depth |
| Final constitutional authority | Founder and Alpha Council hold final authority on Class I/II decisions |
| Self-amendment | Institutional Architecture may not modify its own charter |

#### Primary Outputs

- Canonical Syntheses — the Foundation's definitive institutional positions across all research domains
- Knowledge Graph entries — structured concept notes linking research findings into an integrated graph
- LUMIAION Memory updates — additions to the Foundation's persistent knowledge base
- Conflict resolution reports — where research artifacts disagreed and how the synthesis addressed the disagreement
- Research Debt Register updates — gaps that were not resolved in synthesis

#### Relationships

| Entity | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| All Other Research Functions | Receives and integrates all artifacts from all five other research functions |
| LUMIAION Charter | Institutional Architecture IS LUMIAION; governed by the LUMIAION Charter v2.0.0 |
| Knowledge Graph | Primary builder and custodian of the institutional knowledge graph |
| Future Programs | Institutional positions established in one program inform all future programs |
| Founder | All canonical knowledge is produced for and reviewed by the Founder |
