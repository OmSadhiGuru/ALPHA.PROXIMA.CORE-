# ABOUT

### Headline
**OSG exists for one reason: to give inner work a structure.**

### Story
For most of my life, growth came in flashes — a book that rearranged something, a retreat that opened a door, a hard season that taught what comfort couldn't. But the flashes faded. What I lacked wasn't insight. It was *architecture* — a way to hold what I was learning so it compounded instead of scattering.

OSG is the system I built to solve that, refined into something I could give to others.

It draws on two sources I refuse to confuse. The first is **research** — what psychology and neuroscience can actually show about attention, emotion, memory, and behavior change. The second is **contemplative practice** — the depth that wisdom traditions have carried for millennia, and the metaphysical framework I've developed through my own path. Both matter. But one is evidence and one is a lens, and you deserve to always know which is which.

### What OSG believes
- **Insight without structure evaporates.** Systems are how change lasts. **[Evidence-informed]**
- **You are the sovereign of your own inner life.** A good teacher hands you tools and gets out of the way.
- **Honesty about uncertainty is a feature.** We label what's proven and what's a framework — always.
- **Depth and rigor are not opposites.** You can be both scientifically honest and spiritually serious.

### Boundaries (trust section)
OSG is education and coaching, not therapy or medical care. We work with healthy adults seeking growth. If you're facing a mental health crisis, we'll say so and point you to the right professional support. Knowing our limits is part of taking you seriously.

# FAQ

**Is this therapy?**
No. OSG is education and coaching for healthy adults seeking growth. We're not a substitute for mental health treatment, and if you need that, we'll help you find it. Knowing the difference is part of how we work.

**What makes OSG different from other personal-growth programs?**
Two things. First, it's a *system*, not a collection of techniques — one integrated architecture you build once and run for life. Second, we never blur the line between science and belief: everything is labeled **[Evidence-based]** or **[Contemplative framework]**, so you always know what you're getting.

**Do I need to be "spiritual" to benefit?**
No. The evidence-based core stands on its own. The contemplative framework is offered as an optional depth — clearly labeled — and you can engage with as much or as little of it as fits you.

**Do I need experience with meditation?**
No. The course builds from the ground up. If you already practice, you'll gain structure and progression.

**How much time does the course take?**
Plan for about 2–3 hours per week: teaching, practice, and one live call. The practices themselves are short and daily.

**What if I fall behind?**
You get lifetime access to all materials and can move at your own pace after the live cohort. The community and maintenance plan are built precisely to handle real life.

**Is the live cohort or self-paced better?**
The live cohort adds accountability, group calls, and real-time guidance. Self-paced offers flexibility. Coaching clients get both plus personal sessions.
