## 1. Executive Course Overview

**Course mission.** To help each learner build a reliable, discerning relationship with their own inner wisdom — so they stop outsourcing their most important questions and learn to consult, trust, and act on their own inner guidance.

**Transformation promise.**
> In 8 weeks, you move from second-guessing yourself and searching outside for answers → to a calm, discerning relationship with your own inner wisdom that you can consult for the rest of your life.

**Before → After:**

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Seeking answers from experts, gurus, endless content | Consulting your own inner guidance first |
| Confusing anxiety, impulse, and intuition | Discerning genuine inner wisdom from noise |
| Second-guessing every decision | A repeatable practice for inner clarity |
| "I don't trust myself" | A grounded, tested inner authority |
| External authority | Inner authority, held with humility |

**Ideal student.** A reflective adult (≈30–55) who is capable and self-aware but chronically defers to external authority, over-researches decisions, or doubts their own knowing. Has likely done some inner work (therapy, meditation, reading) and now wants to *trust themselves* with structure and discernment — not naïve "follow your gut" advice.

**Explicitly not for:** those seeking a quick fix; those wanting an external authority to tell them what to do (the course does the opposite); anyone in acute mental-health crisis (the course states this and refers out — OSG is education, not therapy).

**Prerequisites.** None required. Openness to reflection and daily practice (~15 min/day). Beginners welcome; prior meditation experience deepens but is not needed.

**Expected outcomes.** By completion, the learner can:
1. Quiet mental noise enough to notice inner signals. `[E][F]`
2. Observe their own thoughts and reactions without being run by them (the Inner Witness). `[E][F]`
3. Distinguish genuine intuition/wisdom from fear, impulse, and conditioning. `[E][F]`
4. Recognize and work with the inner resistance that blocks self-trust. `[R][F]`
5. Use a set of inner-guidance practices (stillness, inquiry, journaling, contemplation) to consult their inner wisdom deliberately. `[F]`
6. Align inner guidance with their examined values, so trust is grounded, not impulsive. `[E][R]`
7. Sustain a lifelong "inner guru" practice via a personal maintenance plan.

**Estimated duration.** 8 weeks (Module 0 Orientation + 6 core weekly modules + Module 7 Integration & Graduation).

**Weekly commitment.** ~2–3 hours: lesson video(s) + workbook + daily practice (~15 min) + one live/community touchpoint.

## 2. Complete Course Roadmap

Following the OLS Course Architecture (§4): Orientation → Weekly Modules → Lessons → Reflection → Practice → Community → Progress Review → Graduation → Next Path.

```
MODULE 0 — ORIENTATION: "Meeting the Inner Guru"
    ↓  baseline self-assessment · how to learn here · the promise & the boundary
MODULE 1 — THE NOISE AND THE SIGNAL        (quieting to perceive)
MODULE 2 — THE INNER WITNESS               (self-observation)
    ↓  ── PROGRESS REVIEW #1 (mid-early) ──
MODULE 3 — INTUITION & DISCERNMENT         (signal vs. noise)
MODULE 4 — SHADOW & RESISTANCE             (what blocks self-trust)
    ↓  ── PROGRESS REVIEW #2 (midpoint) ──
MODULE 5 — THE PRACTICES OF INNER GUIDANCE (accessing wisdom)
MODULE 6 — VALUES & INNER AUTHORITY        (grounding trust)
    ↓  ── PROGRESS REVIEW #3 (pre-graduation) ──
MODULE 7 — INTEGRATION & GRADUATION: "Living from the Inner Guru"
    ↓  maintenance plan · before/after · celebration · testimonial
NEXT PATH → Coaching (Inner Architecture Container) · Community tier · Advanced course
```

**Reflection points:** embedded in every lesson (OLS 12-part blueprint §5–6) + a Module-level reflection each week + three Progress Reviews (self-assessment vs. the transformation promise).

**Community milestones:** Week 0 introduction post (within 48h); weekly discussion + challenge; a mid-course "turning point" share (Week 4); graduation celebration + testimonial (Week 8).

**Graduation:** completion of Module 7, the before/after Inner Guru Map, and the personal maintenance plan.

**Next learning path:** the Inner Architecture Coaching Container (personal guidance), the paid community tier, or an advanced OSG course.

## 3. Weekly Module Blueprint (Modules 0–7)

*Each module states: learning objective · core transformation · key concepts · skills developed · reflection outcomes · practical outcomes · required assets · estimated completion time.*

---

### MODULE 0 — Orientation: "Meeting the Inner Guru"

- **Learning objective:** Orient to the course, install the first small win, and take an honest baseline of the current relationship with inner guidance.
- **Core transformation:** From "I hope this works" → "I know how to work this, and I've already begun."
- **Key concepts:** The Inner Guru (defined) `[F]`; the Transformation Path; the evidence/framework promise; learner sovereignty.
- **Skills developed:** How to reflect, journal, and practice the OSG way.
- **Reflection outcomes:** A named starting point — where self-trust stands today.
- **Practical outcomes:** Completed baseline Inner Guru Map; first 5-minute stillness practice done.
- **Required assets:** Welcome video; Orientation workbook section; baseline self-assessment; first guided stillness audio; community intro prompt.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~60–75 min.

---

### MODULE 1 — The Noise and the Signal

- **Learning objective:** Quiet mental noise enough to begin noticing subtler inner signals.
- **Core transformation:** From a mind too loud to hear itself → to moments of genuine inner quiet.
- **Key concepts:** Mental noise vs. inner signal `[F]`; the attention system and why stillness is trainable `[E]`; the "clearing" before the "listening."
- **Skills developed:** Basic attention/stillness practice; noticing the texture of inner states.
- **Reflection outcomes:** Awareness of one's habitual noise patterns.
- **Practical outcomes:** A daily 10-minute stillness practice installed.
- **Required assets:** 2 lesson videos + practice video; stillness audio (10 min); workbook pages; journal prompts; community prompt.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~2.5 hrs across the week.

---

### MODULE 2 — The Inner Witness

- **Learning objective:** Observe one's own thoughts and reactions without being swept by them.
- **Core transformation:** From being *inside* every thought → to watching thought from a steadier vantage.
- **Key concepts:** The Inner Witness `[F]`; metacognition and its trainability `[E]`; the pause between stimulus and response `[E]`.
- **Skills developed:** Witnessing practice; real-time self-observation; labeling inner states. `[E — affect labeling]`
- **Reflection outcomes:** Recognizing the difference between "I am angry" and "I notice anger."
- **Practical outcomes:** A daily witnessing practice; one real-world pause captured.
- **Required assets:** 2 lesson videos + practice video; witnessing audio; workbook pages; journal prompts; community prompt; Progress Review #1.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~2.5 hrs.

---

### MODULE 3 — Intuition & Discernment

- **Learning objective:** Distinguish genuine intuition and inner wisdom from fear, impulse, and conditioning.
- **Core transformation:** From "I can't tell my gut from my anxiety" → to a working discernment between them.
- **Key concepts:** What intuition is and isn't — the evidence and its limits `[E]`; the felt difference between wisdom, fear, and impulse `[F]`; discernment as a skill, not a gift.
- **Skills developed:** A discernment protocol (test an inner prompt before acting); somatic noticing.
- **Reflection outcomes:** A personal map of how fear vs. wisdom *feels* different for this learner.
- **Practical outcomes:** Discernment protocol applied to one real decision.
- **Required assets:** 2 lesson videos + practice video; discernment worksheet; case study; workbook pages; journal prompts; community prompt.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~2.5–3 hrs.

---

### MODULE 4 — Shadow & Resistance

- **Learning objective:** Recognize and work with the inner resistance and disowned material that blocks self-trust.
- **Core transformation:** From being unconsciously run by inner blocks → to meeting them consciously.
- **Key concepts:** Resistance and self-sabotage patterns `[R]`; the "shadow" as a working framework `[F]`; self-trust as courage, not certainty.
- **Skills developed:** Resistance-mapping; compassionate self-inquiry; meeting difficult material without being overwhelmed.
- **Reflection outcomes:** Identification of the learner's core self-trust block.
- **Practical outcomes:** One resistance pattern named and worked with.
- **Required assets:** 2 lesson videos + practice video; shadow/resistance worksheet; contemplation audio; workbook pages; journal prompts; community prompt; Progress Review #2. **Boundary note:** explicit "this is not therapy; refer out" callout.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~3 hrs.

---

### MODULE 5 — The Practices of Inner Guidance

- **Learning objective:** Use a set of practices to deliberately consult inner wisdom.
- **Core transformation:** From hoping for insight → to a repeatable method for accessing it.
- **Key concepts:** The four inner-guidance practices — stillness, written inquiry, contemplative sitting, symbolic/imaginal inquiry `[F]`; journaling as a thinking tool `[E]`; asking a real question and listening.
- **Skills developed:** Written dialogue with the inner guru; contemplative inquiry; symbolic reflection.
- **Reflection outcomes:** Discovery of which guidance practice fits this learner best.
- **Practical outcomes:** A real question taken through the full inner-guidance process.
- **Required assets:** 2–3 lesson videos + practice video; guided inquiry audio; inner-dialogue worksheet; templates; workbook pages; journal prompts; community prompt.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~3 hrs.

---

### MODULE 6 — Values & Inner Authority

- **Learning objective:** Ground inner guidance in examined values so self-trust is discerning, not impulsive.
- **Core transformation:** From "trust yourself" as a slogan → to inner authority grounded in what the learner most values.
- **Key concepts:** Values clarification `[E]`; inner authority vs. mere self-will `[F]`; the union of intuition + values + discernment.
- **Skills developed:** Values-based decision practice; aligning a guidance prompt with core values before acting.
- **Reflection outcomes:** A clarified, examined set of core values.
- **Practical outcomes:** A real decision made through the full integrated method (stillness → witness → discern → consult → values-check → act).
- **Required assets:** 2 lesson videos + practice video; values worksheet; integration template; workbook pages; journal prompts; community prompt; Progress Review #3.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~2.5–3 hrs.

---

### MODULE 7 — Integration & Graduation: "Living from the Inner Guru"

- **Learning objective:** Assemble the practices into one sustainable lifelong system and mark the transformation.
- **Core transformation:** From "I took a course" → "I have an ongoing relationship with my inner guru."
- **Key concepts:** The personal inner-guidance rhythm (daily/weekly/quarterly); maintenance and relapse-recovery; the inner guru as lifelong relationship `[F]`; habit design `[E]`.
- **Skills developed:** Designing and documenting a personal maintenance plan; quarterly self-review.
- **Reflection outcomes:** The completed "after" Inner Guru Map next to the "before."
- **Practical outcomes:** A written, personal maintenance plan the learner will run after the course.
- **Required assets:** 2 lesson videos; maintenance-plan template; before/after map; graduation video; testimonial prompt; Next Path overview.
- **Estimated completion time:** ~2–2.5 hrs.

## 6. Journal Blueprint

A dedicated learner journal (separate from workbook worksheets; this is the daily reflective companion).

**Structure:**
- **Daily prompts (8 weeks):** one short prompt per day tied to the current module's theme (e.g., Module 1 week: "What did the quiet show me today?"). ~15 rotating prompts per module theme.
- **Weekly prompts:** one deeper end-of-week reflection per module (the lesson's primary journal question).
- **Milestone reflections:** at Weeks 2, 4, 6 (aligned to the three Progress Reviews) — "How has my relationship to my own knowing changed?"
- **End-of-course reflection:** a structured multi-part final entry: Who was I? What changed? What will I carry? What is my inner guru to me now?
- **Free pages:** unstructured space for inner-dialogue overflow (esp. Module 5).

**Design spec:** available as printable PDF and digital; daily prompts pre-dated/sequenced; the daily practice is intentionally short (≤10 min) to protect consistency `[E — habit formation]`.

## 8. Instructor Preparation

What the instructor must prepare before production.

**Skills required:**
- Personal fluency in all four inner-guidance practices (must practice what is taught).
- Ability to hold contemplative space on camera; guided-meditation delivery.
- Skilled storytelling; warm, humble, precise tone (OLS §12).
- Facility with the evidence/framework distinction — able to cite research honestly and label frameworks cleanly.
- Boundary discipline: recognizing when to refer out (Module 4 especially).

**Demonstrations to prepare:**
- Live stillness, witnessing, discernment, and inner-dialogue demos.
- A real (appropriately shared) discernment of fear vs. wisdom.
- A worked example of the full integrated decision method (Modules 6–7).

**Stories to prepare (1–2 per module):**
- M0: a personal story of over-relying on external authority.
- M1: a "too much noise to hear myself" story.
- M2: a moment of witnessing that changed a reaction.
- M3: an intuition that was right; one that was fear in disguise.
- M4: a self-trust block and meeting it (vulnerable but bounded).
- M5: a real question answered through inner guidance.
- M6: a values-grounded decision under pressure.
- M7: the ongoing relationship with one's own inner guru.

**Visuals needed:** the Transformation Path diagram; the "noise vs. signal" model; the fear/impulse/wisdom discernment map; the four-practices diagram; the integrated decision-method flow; the daily/weekly/quarterly rhythm.

**Props:** journal + workbook (shown on camera); a simple stillness setup (cushion/chair) for practice demos; timer.

**Research references to compile (for `[E]` claims):** attention/focus training; metacognition; affect labeling; intuition research (strengths & limits); self-compassion; values-based living; habit formation. Each `[E]` claim in scripts must cite a compiled reference; each `[F]` claim must be labeled as framework. *(Reference list assembled during scripting; sourcing standard per OSG citation practice.)*
