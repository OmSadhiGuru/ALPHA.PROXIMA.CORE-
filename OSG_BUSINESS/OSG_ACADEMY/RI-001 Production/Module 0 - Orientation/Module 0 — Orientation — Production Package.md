# Module 0 — Orientation · Production Package
## Awaken the Inner Guru (RI-001)

*Production-ready. English master (FR transcreation follows per Production Blueprint §11). Labeling: `[E]` evidence · `[R]` reflective · `[F]` framework. Lessons: L0.1 Welcome & How This Works · L0.2 Meeting Your Inner Guru.*

---

## 1. Module Objective

Orient the learner to the course and method, install the first small win within 24 hours, and capture an honest baseline of their current relationship with inner guidance.

## 2. Student Transformation

**From:** "I've enrolled, I hope this works, I'm not sure where to start."
**To:** "I understand how this works, I've taken my honest starting point, and I've already done my first practice."

## 3. Complete Lesson Scripts

> Delivery notes: warm, unhurried, direct. Speak to one person, not an audience. Pauses marked `(pause)`. On-screen cues in **[BRACKETS]**. Word-for-word, teleprompter-ready.

### LESSON 0.1 — Welcome & How This Works *(~12–15 min)*

**[ON SCREEN: title — "Welcome"]**

Hello, and welcome to *Awaken the Inner Guru*.

If you're here, something in you is tired of looking outside yourself for answers you suspect you already carry. You've read the books. You've asked the smart people. You've searched. And still, in the quiet, there's a voice you don't quite trust yet. (pause) This course is about learning to trust it — not blindly, but with discernment.

Let me be clear about what this course is, and what it isn't.

This course will not give you answers. (pause) I know that might sound strange coming from a teacher. But if I hand you answers, I've made you dependent on me — and that's the opposite of what we're doing here. Instead, I'm going to help you build a *capacity*: the ability to reflect, to observe your own mind, to tell the difference between fear and wisdom, and to consult your own inner guidance when it matters. That capacity stays with you for life. Answers don't.

**[ON SCREEN: "This course builds capacity, not dependency."]**

Here's how it works.

We have eight weeks together, plus an orientation week — this one — and a ninety-day integration after we finish. Each week has one theme and a small number of short lessons. Every week you'll *learn* something, *reflect* on it, *practice* it, *observe* what happens in your real life, *share* in the community, and *review* what shifted. That rhythm repeats every week. Once you learn it, you carry it.

**[ON SCREEN: the weekly rhythm — Learn · Reflect · Practice · Observe · Share · Integrate · Review]**

Plan for about two to three hours a week, and about fifteen minutes a day of practice. That daily fifteen minutes matters more than the videos. (pause) Let me say that again: the practice matters more than the videos. This is not a course you *watch*. It's a course you *do*.

One promise I make to you, and I'll keep it in every lesson. I will always tell you the difference between two kinds of things. Some of what I teach is supported by research — psychology, neuroscience, how attention and memory actually work. When it is, I'll say so. **[ON SCREEN: "[E] Evidence-based"]** And some of what I teach comes from contemplative traditions and from my own experience — practices and frameworks that are meaningful and useful, but that are not scientific claims. When that's what I'm offering, I'll say that too. **[ON SCREEN: "[F] A framework, a lens — not a fact"]** You will always know which is which. That honesty is the ground we stand on.

A boundary, because it matters. This course is education, not therapy. It is not treatment for anxiety, depression, trauma, or any mental-health condition. If you're in real distress, please reach out to a qualified professional — and if anything here brings up more than you can hold, step back and get support. Taking your inner life seriously includes knowing when you need a human being beside you. `[boundary]`

**[ON SCREEN: "Education, not therapy. In crisis? Please reach a professional."]**

So here's what I want you to do, and it's only one thing. Don't try to do the whole course today. Just go to the next lesson — *Meeting Your Inner Guru* — and do the short baseline exercise and the first five-minute practice. One step. That's how this whole course works: one step at a time. (pause)

I'm genuinely glad you're here. Let's begin.

**[ON SCREEN: "Next: Meeting Your Inner Guru →"]**

### LESSON 0.2 — Meeting Your Inner Guru *(~25–30 min)*

**[ON SCREEN: title — "Meeting Your Inner Guru"]**

Let's start with the phrase itself: *the inner guru*. (pause)

I want to be careful here, because words like this can be misused. When I say "inner guru," I do not mean a mystical voice that is always right, or a magic intuition that never fails. That's not it. `[F]` What I mean is simpler and more grounded: the part of you that already carries wisdom — from your experience, your values, your honest perception — that usually gets drowned out by noise, fear, and the habit of looking outside yourself. The inner guru is not infallible. It's *neglected*. This course is about learning to hear it, and then to test what it says with discernment. `[F]`

**[ON SCREEN: "The inner guru: your own neglected wisdom — heard, then tested."]**

Now, why don't we hear it? (pause) Partly because we were trained not to. Most of us learned early that the answers are *out there* — in the expert, the authority, the book, the algorithm. And there's nothing wrong with learning from others. But somewhere along the way, many capable people stop consulting themselves entirely. They can tell you what every expert thinks and not what *they* think. Does that sound familiar? (pause)

There's also a real mechanism here. Your attention is limited, and when your mind is loud — busy, anxious, scrolling — the subtler signals simply don't get through. This isn't a character flaw. It's how attention works, and the good news is that attention is trainable. `[E]` We'll train it. That's most of Week 1.

**[ON SCREEN: "Attention is trainable. [E]"]**

So before we build anything, we take an honest measurement. In a moment I'm going to ask you to open your workbook to the Baseline pages and fill out something we call the Inner Guru Map. (pause) It's not a test. There are no right answers. It's a snapshot of where you stand today, so that in eight weeks you can look back and see — in your own words — exactly what changed. Most people are surprised. We rarely notice our own growth unless we've marked the starting line.

**[ON SCREEN: "Open your workbook: the Inner Guru Map (Baseline)."]**

The map asks you a few honest questions. Where do you currently go for answers when something matters? How much do you trust your own judgment, right now, on a scale you define? When was the last time you knew something inside and overrode it — and what happened? And: what is one real question, alive in your life right now, that you'd love your inner guru to help you with by the end of these eight weeks? (pause) Write that question down. We'll come back to it.

**[Instructor completes or references the map briefly on screen.]**

Now, the first practice. (pause) Everything in this course is built on one root skill — the ability to become quiet enough, inside, to actually perceive what's there. So we start there, today, with five minutes of stillness. Not to empty your mind — you can't, and trying is the wrong goal. `[F]` Just to sit, and let the noise settle a little, the way silt settles in still water. That's it. Five minutes.

You'll find the guided audio linked right below this lesson. When we finish here, put it on, sit comfortably, and let it lead you. Then come back and log it — one line in your practice log. That's your first small win, and I want you to have it *today*, while the motivation is fresh. `[E — early wins build follow-through]`

**[ON SCREEN: "Today: (1) Inner Guru Map · (2) 5-min stillness · (3) log it."]**

Let me tell you what to expect over our eight weeks, briefly, so you can see the shape of the path. We'll quiet the noise, so you can hear the signal. We'll learn to watch your own mind instead of being swept by it. We'll learn to tell the difference between fear, impulse, and genuine wisdom — because they feel different once you know how to look. We'll meet the inner resistance that makes self-trust hard. We'll learn actual practices for consulting your inner guidance. We'll ground all of it in your real values, so your trust is discerning, not reckless. And then we'll practice using it under real pressure — and build a way to keep it going for the rest of your life. (pause) That's the journey. It ends with you needing me less, not more. That's the point.

**[ON SCREEN: the 8-week arc, one line each]**

For now — just three things. The map. The five minutes. The log. Go do them, and I'll see you in Week 1.

**[ON SCREEN: "Your turn. Map → Stillness → Log. See you in Week 1."]**

If it helps, silently notice — "in" as you breathe in… (…) and "out" as you breathe out. (……)

Your mind will wander. (…) That's completely normal — that's what minds do. When you notice you've drifted into thinking, you don't need to be annoyed. (…) Just gently come back to the breath. In… (…) and out. (……)

Let the noise of the day be there in the background if it wants to. (…) You don't have to fight it. You're just sitting here, letting the water grow still. (………)

In… (…) and out. (………)

Notice, underneath the busyness, that there's a part of you that is simply *aware*. (…) Aware of the breath. Aware of sitting here. (…) You don't have to do anything with that. Just notice it's here. (……)

Rest here for a few more breaths, in your own time. (…………)

And now, gently, let your attention widen. (…) Notice the sounds around you. (…) Feel the weight of your body where it's supported. (…) And when you're ready — in no hurry — let your eyes open. (…)

## 12. Instructor Notes

- **Tone:** warm, grounded, unhurried. You are a guide, not a guru — model the humility the course teaches.
- **Say the promise plainly** (evidence vs. framework) in 0.1 and honor it every time you make a claim on camera.
- **Do not oversell the "inner guru."** Underclaim. It's neglected wisdom to be *tested*, never an infallible voice. This protects trust and the `[E]`/`[F]` line.
- **Hold the boundary** clearly (not therapy). Deliver it with care, not as fine print.
- **Story to prepare (0.1):** a brief, honest personal moment of over-relying on external authority. Keep it short and real.
- **Story to prepare (0.2):** a time you knew something inside and overrode it — and the cost. Vulnerable but bounded.
- **Drive the ONE next step.** End both lessons pointing to a single action. Do not overwhelm.
- **Meditation delivery:** slower than feels natural; honor the silences; let it breathe. Record in a quiet, soft-acoustic space.
- **FR note:** this English master will be transcreated to French per Production Blueprint §11; keep phrasing clean and idiom-light to ease transcreation.
