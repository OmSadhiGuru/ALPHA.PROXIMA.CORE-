# Community Onboarding Flow
### Turning a new member into an active participant

*A community only creates retention if new members become active in their first week. This flow is designed to get every new member to post once within 48 hours — the single strongest predictor of long-term participation.* **[Evidence-based — online community engagement research]**

---

## Structure (recommended channels/spaces)

Keep it small at launch. Too many channels kills a young community.

| Space | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Start Here** | Rules, orientation, the one first action |
| **Introductions** | Every new member's first post lands here |
| **The Practice** | Daily/weekly practice check-ins |
| **Ask & Support** | Questions, stuck points, troubleshooting |
| **Wins & Reflections** | Celebrate shifts, share insights |
| **Live Calls** | Schedules, links, replays |
| *(later)* **Cohort channels** | Per-cohort spaces as you grow |

---

## The onboarding flow (first 7 days)

### Step 1 — The invite (arrives with course onboarding email)
The community link is delivered in the onboarding email (file 05, C/D) with a *specific first action*, never just "join the community."

### Step 2 — Start Here (first thing they see)
A single pinned post:

> **Welcome. Do these three things, in order:**
> 1. Read the two ground rules below.
> 2. Post your intro in **Introductions** using the prompt.
> 3. Say hi to one person who posted before you.
>
> That's it. You're in.

**Two ground rules (keep it to two):**
1. **Confidentiality.** What's shared here stays here.
2. **Support, don't fix.** We hold space and share experience; we don't diagnose or give unsolicited advice. (OSG is not therapy — if someone's in crisis, we point them to professional help.)

### Step 3 — The intro prompt (engineered for an easy first post)
> **Introduce yourself in 3 lines:**
> - Where you are right now (one sentence).
> - What brought you to OSG.
> - One thing you're hoping to build.
>
> Then reply to one person above you. 🙏

*Why this works:* it's small, structured, and vulnerable-but-safe — the easiest possible first post, and it immediately creates a reciprocal interaction. **[Evidence-based — reciprocity & early-interaction effects]**

### Step 4 — The 48-hour welcome (host action)
Within 48 hours, the host (or a community lead) personally replies to every intro. Even one warm sentence. This is the highest-leverage retention action in the entire community and must not be skipped in the launch phase.

### Step 5 — First practice check-in (day 3–4)
A prompt in **The Practice**:
> **First check-in:** Did you try [the Module 1 attention practice]? One word for how it went. No wrong answers.

Low-friction, keeps momentum, normalizes imperfect practice.

### Step 6 — First win surfaced (day 5–7)
Host seeds **Wins & Reflections** with a prompt:
> **Tiny wins count.** Share one small thing you've noticed since starting — even "I paused before reacting once." Especially that.

## Weekly rhythm (once running)

| Day | Ritual |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Module drop + "what's your focus this week?" |
| Wednesday | Live call |
| Friday | Wins & reflections prompt |
| Sunday | Gentle week-ahead intention prompt |

Predictable rhythm > constant novelty. A community with reliable weekly rituals outperforms one that depends on spontaneous activity.

---

## Host operating rules for launch phase

1. **Reply to every intro personally** (first 90 days minimum).
2. **Seed every space** with the first post — never leave an empty channel.
3. **Celebrate publicly, correct privately.**
4. **Model the vulnerability you want** — post your own imperfect practice check-ins.
5. **Protect the boundary** — redirect anything that needs professional help, kindly and clearly.

---

## The one metric that matters at launch

**% of new members who post within 48 hours.** Track it weekly. If it drops, the intro flow (Steps 2–4) needs attention before anything else. Everything else in community health follows from that first post.
