# Awaken the Inner Guru Recording Start Guide

## Purpose

Convert the RI-001 blueprint into a practical recording plan.

## What The Course Says

`Awaken the Inner Guru` is about building self-trust through structured reflection and practice.

The promise is:

> Move from second-guessing yourself and seeking answers outside to a calm, discerning relationship with your own inner wisdom.

The course should always preserve this constraint:

> The course does not provide answers. It builds the learner's capacity to reflect, observe, discern, integrate, and judge for themselves.

## Recording Subject

The subject is:

**Self-trust and inner discernment through structured reflection, stillness, witnessing, intuition, resistance work, values, and real-life application.**

Do not frame it as:

- instant intuition
- mystical certainty
- external guru teaching
- therapy
- advice-giving
- motivational content

Frame it as:

- capacity-building
- learner sovereignty
- discernment
- practice
- reflection
- grounded inner authority

## Recommended Recording Order

Start with French if following the committed bilingual model.

1. Record the short orientation/welcome first.
2. Record Week 0 and Week 1 before recording the rest of the course.
3. Use Week 0 and Week 1 as the pilot production standard.
4. Review sound, tone, pacing, captions, and learner clarity.
5. Continue only after the first production pass feels repeatable.

## Recording Subjects By Week

| Week | Theme | What You Are Teaching |
|------|-------|-----------------------|
| Week 0 | Meeting the Inner Guru | Orientation, baseline self-trust, how to use the course |
| Week 1 | The Noise and the Signal | Quieting mental noise so inner signal can be noticed |
| Week 2 | The Inner Witness | Observing thoughts and reactions without being run by them |
| Week 3 | Intuition and Discernment | Distinguishing intuition from fear, impulse, and conditioning |
| Week 4 | Shadow and Resistance | Recognizing resistance that blocks self-trust |
| Week 5 | Practices of Inner Guidance | Using stillness, inquiry, contemplation, and journaling to consult inner wisdom |
| Week 6 | Values and Inner Authority | Grounding guidance in examined values |
| Week 7 | Discernment in Action | Applying the full method under real-life pressure |
| Week 8 | Living from the Inner Guru | Building a sustainable lifelong practice |

## First Videos To Record

### 1. L0.1 - Welcome & How This Works

Goal: explain the course structure and how to work with it.

Core message:

- This is not passive content.
- The learner must practice.
- The course builds capacity, not dependency.
- The learner is responsible for their own reflection and application.

Folder:

```text
OSG_LAUNCH/10_ACADEMY/AIG/02_RECORDING/FR/M0_Orientation/
```

### 2. L0.2 - Meeting Your Inner Guru

Goal: define the Inner Guru as a framework and create the baseline.

Core message:

- The inner guru is not a supernatural authority.
- It is the learner's capacity for inner wisdom, discernment, reflection, and grounded knowing.
- The learner starts by honestly mapping their current relationship with self-trust.

Folder:

```text
OSG_LAUNCH/10_ACADEMY/AIG/02_RECORDING/FR/M0_Orientation/
```

### 3. L1.1 - Why You Can't Hear Yourself

Goal: teach mental noise vs. inner signal.

Core message:

- Many people cannot hear themselves because the mind is too loud.
- Stillness is the first condition for discernment.
- The learner begins by noticing their own noise patterns.

Folder:

```text
OSG_LAUNCH/10_ACADEMY/AIG/02_RECORDING/FR/M1_Noise_and_Signal/
```

### 4. L1.2 - The Practice of Stillness

Goal: install the first daily practice.

Core message:

- Stillness is a practice, not a personality trait.
- The learner does not need to be good at it.
- The first win is simply showing up and noticing.

Folder:

```text
OSG_LAUNCH/10_ACADEMY/AIG/02_RECORDING/FR/M1_Noise_and_Signal/
```

## Minimal Recording Checklist

Before recording each lesson:

- [ ] Lesson has one learning objective.
- [ ] Lesson follows the OSG 12-part lesson structure.
- [ ] Evidence-based claims are marked `[E]`.
- [ ] Contemplative/framework claims are marked `[F]`.
- [ ] Reflective practice is marked `[R]` where relevant.
- [ ] The lesson gives the learner something to do.
- [ ] The lesson does not give the learner an answer.
- [ ] Therapy boundary is included where needed.
- [ ] File name includes course, module, lesson, language, asset type, and version.
