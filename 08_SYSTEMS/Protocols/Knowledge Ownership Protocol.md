# Knowledge Ownership Protocol

## Purpose

This Protocol establishes the principles and rules governing who owns the knowledge produced within Alpha Proxima, how ownership is attributed, and what rights the Foundation holds over knowledge generated by AI participants.

This is a foundational document. It answers the question that most AI-dependent organisations have never asked: *when an AI produces knowledge inside your institution, whose knowledge is it?*

---

## Context

The default assumption in most AI usage is that knowledge belongs to whoever prompted the AI. Alpha Proxima rejects this as insufficient. Knowledge produced within the institutional context of Alpha Proxima — regardless of which AI engine produced it — belongs to the Alpha Proxima Foundation and is governed by its constitutional framework.

This is not a legal claim. It is an institutional claim. It is the foundational principle that makes Alpha Proxima's knowledge accumulation coherent across decades of changing AI providers.

The principle: **Knowledge belongs to Alpha Proxima. No AI owns institutional knowledge. Every engine contributes. LUMIAION orchestrates. The Foundation remembers.**

---

## Core Content

### Principle 1 — Institutional Primacy

All knowledge produced within the Alpha Proxima institutional context is institutional knowledge. This includes:
- Knowledge produced by AI engines responding to Founder inquiries
- Knowledge synthesised by [[LUMIAION Charter|LUMIAION]] from department outputs
- Research conducted by [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM]]
- Analysis produced by [[ATHENA Charter|ATHENA]], [[VORTEX Charter|VORTEX]], or [[SOHMA Charter|SOHMA]]
- Knowledge produced by human participants acting in their institutional capacity

None of the above is proprietary to the engine that produced it. The engine is a tool. The knowledge is Alpha Proxima's.

---

### Principle 2 — The Vault as the Canonical Record

Knowledge is only institutional knowledge when it is committed to the [[Obsidian Vault]]. Knowledge that exists only in a conversation, a chat log, or a temporary session is not yet institutional knowledge — it is raw material.

**The act of committing knowledge to the Vault is the act of institutionalising it.**

This creates a clear responsibility: [[LUMIAION Charter|LUMIAION]] and [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM]] are responsible for ensuring that significant knowledge does not remain trapped in session memory. Every meaningful insight, decision rationale, or synthesised analysis must become a Vault note.

---

### Principle 3 — Attribution Without Dependency

Attribution records *how* knowledge was produced, not *who owns* it.

Standard attribution format for Vault notes:
- `authors: ["Founder"]` — when the Founder is the primary author
- `authors: ["LUMIAION"]` — when LUMIAION produced the note
- `authors: ["JERANIUM"]` — when JERANIUM produced the research
- `authors: ["Founder", "LUMIAION"]` — collaborative production
- Engine attribution goes in a `generated_by` field if relevant, not `authors`

The `authors` field records institutional authorship. The `generated_by` field records which AI engine was used. These are separate concerns.

```yaml
authors: ["LUMIAION"]
generated_by: "Anthropic Claude (Sonnet 4.6)"
```

---

### Principle 4 — Engine Independence

When an AI engine is replaced, the knowledge it produced does not leave with it. The knowledge belongs to Alpha Proxima, is stored in the Vault, and persists independently of the engine.

This is why the [[Engine Registry]] and [[AI Council Registry]] maintain the separation between roles and engines. When Claude is replaced as the Chief Knowledge Architect engine, every Vault note it produced remains. The new engine inherits the knowledge; it does not start from nothing.

**Practical implication:** Never design a knowledge system that requires a specific AI provider to interpret or access the knowledge. All knowledge must be readable as plain Markdown.

---

### Principle 5 — No External Export Without Governance Approval

Institutional knowledge may not be exported to external systems, shared with third parties, or used outside the Alpha Proxima institutional context without approval from the appropriate governing council.

| Knowledge Type | Export Authority |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Constitutional documents | Founder only |
| Strategic and financial intelligence | Executive Council |
| Research and domain knowledge | Research Council |
| Technical architecture | Engineering Council |
| Public-facing outputs | Community Council |

---

### Principle 6 — Knowledge Contributed by AI Engines Remains Institutional

AI engines contribute knowledge to Alpha Proxima. They do not hold knowledge for Alpha Proxima. The distinction matters:

- *Contributing* knowledge means the engine produces it and it enters the Vault — the engine's contribution ends there
- *Holding* knowledge would mean the knowledge lives inside the engine's memory — this is structurally impossible across sessions and providers, and creates dependency

Alpha Proxima never relies on an AI engine's internal memory as a substitute for the Vault. Context may be provided to engines from the Vault in each session, but the Vault is always the primary record.

---

### Principle 7 — Knowledge Quality is a Governance Responsibility

The Research Council holds authority over the epistemic standards of knowledge in the Vault. [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM]] supports these standards operationally. [[LUMIAION Charter|LUMIAION]] enforces them at the point of Vault commitment.

Low-quality knowledge in the Vault is not neutral — it degrades the quality of all future reasoning that draws on it. Garbage in; garbage out is an institutional failure, not just a technical one.

**Standard for Vault commitment:**
- Source or origin identified
- Confidence level assessed
- Uncertainty flagged explicitly
- Contradictions with existing Vault knowledge surfaced and resolved or noted

---

### Knowledge Classification

| Class | Description | Access |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Constitutional | Founding documents, governance framework | All participants; read-only for AI |
| Institutional | Charters, registries, ADRs, protocols | All participants |
| Strategic | Vision, roadmaps, financial strategy | Founder + Executive Council |
| Operational | Projects, research, department outputs | Working participants |
| Personal | Founder's personal context, health, private matters | Founder only |
