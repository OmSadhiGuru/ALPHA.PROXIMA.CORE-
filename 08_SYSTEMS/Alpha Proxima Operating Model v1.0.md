# Alpha Proxima Operating Model v1.0

---

## Purpose

This document is the official operational description of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. It describes how the Foundation operates as a coherent institution — how decisions flow, how knowledge flows, how information flows, and how the Foundation evolves while preserving its identity.

This is not a governance document. Governance is established in the constitutional books. This is an operational map — a description of how the institution actually functions day to day and across time.

Where the Constitution says *what* the Foundation is, and the Governance Framework says *who* holds authority, this document says *how* the institution works.

---

## Mission

The Alpha Proxima Foundation builds open architectures for AI orchestration, knowledge systems, digital memory, and human augmentation — designed to endure across multiple generations of technology while remaining grounded in human values and governed by human judgment.

---

## Institutional Philosophy

The Foundation operates on twelve founding principles documented in [[Founding Principles of Alpha Proxima]]. The operating philosophy can be expressed in five operational commitments:

1. **Architecture before implementation.** Every new capability is designed before it is built.
2. **Knowledge before action.** Every significant decision is grounded in institutional knowledge.
3. **Capture before closure.** Every session's insights are committed to the Vault before the session ends.
4. **Function before engine.** Every cognitive capability is defined by its responsibility, not by the AI that currently serves it.
5. **Coherence before scale.** The Foundation verifies internal coherence before expanding.

## Artifact Lifecycle

Every institutional artifact follows this lifecycle:

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Creation | Artifact is produced (research, engineering, governance) | Producing Office |
| Review | Artifact is reviewed for quality and constitutional alignment | Relevant Council or peer review |
| Ratification | Artifact is accepted into canonical institutional record | Founder or delegated authority |
| Commitment | Artifact is committed to the Vault | LUMIAION / Chief Knowledge Architect |
| Versioning | Artifact receives version number; all changes are tracked | Producing Office |
| Archive | When superseded, artifact is archived — not deleted | LUMIAION |

## Governance Model

The Foundation's governance operates across four decision classes:

| Class | Type | Primary Authority | Process |
|-------|------|------------------|---------|
| I | Constitutional | Founder | Supermajority + 14-day deliberation + ADR |
| II | Institutional | Executive Council | Majority + ADR + specialist concurrence |
| III | Operational | Specialist Office or Delegate | ADR + relevant authority review |
| IV | Tactical | Individual actor or Office | Logged; reviewable |

**Ethics Council:** Independent of all class levels. May review and recommend against any action at any class level if it presents a constitutional risk. Reports directly to the Founder.

## Operational Principles

**1. One system of record per artifact type.**
Knowledge artifacts live in the Vault. Engineering artifacts live in GitHub. Neither duplicates the other's function.

**2. No invisible decisions.**
Every Class I–III decision has an ADR. Tactical decisions are logged. Nothing of consequence is decided without a traceable record.

**3. Research precedes canonical knowledge.**
Before a claim becomes institutional knowledge, it has passed through the evidence classification system and been documented in a Research Program artifact.

**4. Every office has one domain. One domain has one office.**
No two offices share sovereign authority over the same domain. When domains overlap, the boundary is negotiated and documented — not left ambiguous.

**5. LUMIAION coordinates; offices own.**
LUMIAION's role is coordination and synthesis, not authority. Each Office owns its domain. LUMIAION ensures they operate as a coherent whole.

**6. Silence is a valid state.**
The Observatory not reporting is not a failure. A program not producing output in a week is not a failure. Institutional silence is meaningful when it reflects a genuine absence of activity requiring attention.

**7. Architecture gates expansion.**
New programs, new offices, and new capabilities are added only when the existing architecture can coherently accommodate them. Coherence before scale.

## Institutional Evolution

Alpha Proxima is designed to evolve. Epochs mark architectural thresholds — moments when the Foundation's operating model must update to accommodate what the institution has become.

| Epoch | Threshold | Characteristic |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Epoch I | Foundation | Constitutional structure established; first research programs initiated |
| Epoch II | Stewardship | Methodology standardized; documentation debt distinguished from architectural blockers |
| Epoch III | Coherence | Cognitive architecture institutionalized; multiple offices operating; engine abstraction formalized |
| Epoch IV (future) | Sovereignty | Memory sovereignty achieved; Foundation operates on private infrastructure |
| Epoch V (future) | Scale | Foundation opens to community; external affiliates; institutional growth |

Each Epoch transition is documented as a Founder Directive and resolved through a formal Constitutional Refactoring Initiative, producing at minimum: an updated Operating Model, any new constitutional instruments, and a Constitutional Impact Report.
