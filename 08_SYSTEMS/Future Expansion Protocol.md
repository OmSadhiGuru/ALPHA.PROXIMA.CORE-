# Future Expansion Protocol

## Purpose

This Protocol defines how Alpha Proxima Foundation expands — how new departments are added, how new councils are constituted, how new AI engines are integrated, and how the institution scales without losing constitutional coherence.

Growth without governance is entropy. This Protocol ensures that every expansion is intentional, documented, and constitutionally grounded.

---

## Context

Institutions that plan for growth before they need it grow coherently. Institutions that plan for growth after they need it import chaos. Alpha Proxima was designed to be expanded — the trailing dash in `ALPHA.PROXIMA.CORE-` is a permanent statement of incompleteness.

But expansion carries risk. Every new element added to a system increases its complexity. Every new participant introduces new assumptions. Every new technology creates new dependencies. This Protocol manages those risks through clear expansion criteria, process, and documentation requirements.

---

## Core Content

### Expansion Principles

**Principle 1 — Expansion Must Be Earned**
A new department, council, or system earns its place by demonstrating that the current architecture cannot adequately serve a domain. Adding complexity for its own sake is a failure mode. The bar is: *does the absence of this element create a genuine gap in institutional capability?*

**Principle 2 — Roles Before Engines**
When adding AI capability, define the role first. What is the permanent institutional responsibility? What are the boundaries? What are the success metrics? Only after the role is defined does engine selection begin.

**Principle 3 — Integration Before Addition**
Before adding a new department, verify that the gap cannot be addressed by expanding an existing department's scope. Fragmentation is a governance cost. Four deep departments serve better than eight shallow ones.

**Principle 4 — Every Expansion Creates Documentation**
A new entity that is not documented in the Vault does not exist institutionally. Charter, registry entry, relationship map update, and architecture update are mandatory for every expansion.

**Principle 5 — Constitutional Alignment First**
Every expansion is reviewed by the Ethics Council for constitutional alignment before ratification. The Ethics Council asks: does this expansion advance or compromise the principles in [[Book I - The Constitution]]?

### Department Expansion Process

When a new intelligence department is proposed:

1. **Concept Note** — Submit [[Concept Note Template|Concept Note]] to AI Council identifying:
   - The domain gap the new department addresses
   - Why existing departments cannot cover this domain
   - Proposed mission, scope, and boundaries
   - Proposed relationships to existing departments
   - Proposed engine and Chief Architect role (if new role needed)

2. **Ethics Council Review** — Ethics Council reviews for constitutional alignment (7 days)

3. **AI Council Deliberation** — Minimum 14-day review period; all existing department charters reviewed for boundary conflicts

4. **Executive Council Ratification** — Simple majority vote; Class II decision; ADR produced

5. **Charter Production** — New department charter produced using the existing charter format (see [[SOHMA Charter]], [[ATHENA Charter]], etc. as models)

6. **Registry Update** — [[Institutional Registry]], [[AI Council Registry]], and [[Institutional Relationship Map]] updated

7. **Architecture Update** — [[Foundational Architecture]] updated to reflect new department

---

### Council Expansion Process

When a new governing council is proposed:

1. **Concept Note** — Submitted to Executive Council
2. **Ethics Council Review** — Constitutional alignment check; minimum 14 days
3. **All existing councils notified** — 21-day deliberation period
4. **Executive Council Ratification** — Supermajority required for new council creation
5. **Book II Amendment** — [[Book II - Governance Framework]] updated via Class II ADR
6. **Charter Production** — Council charter produced and ratified

### Planned Future Expansions

The following expansions are anticipated but not yet initiated. Each requires a formal Concept Note before work begins.

---

#### Department: PROMETHEUS (Technology and Innovation)
**Anticipated domain:** Emerging technology, AI developments, innovation strategy, technology forecasting  
**Current coverage gap:** No existing department holds responsibility for tracking and evaluating emerging technologies that may affect Alpha Proxima's architecture  
**Trigger for initiating:** When the volume of technology-evaluation work exceeds JERANIUM's capacity to handle it alongside its core research function  
**Proposed engine:** To be evaluated at time of initiation

---

#### Department: HERMES (Communication and Education)
**Anticipated domain:** External communication, teaching, knowledge translation, community education  
**Current coverage gap:** No department owns the translation of institutional knowledge into public-facing or community-facing form  
**Trigger for initiating:** When Alpha Proxima develops a community or publication function  
**Proposed engine:** To be evaluated at time of initiation

---

#### Department: APOLLO (Creative Intelligence)
**Anticipated domain:** Creative work, artistic production, aesthetic intelligence, narrative  
**Current coverage gap:** The current architecture is strong on analysis and weak on creation in the aesthetic sense  
**Trigger for initiating:** When the Founder's creative projects require systematic intelligence support  
**Proposed engine:** To be evaluated at time of initiation

---

#### Council: Ethics Council (Priority)
**Status:** Required; not yet formally constituted  
**Trigger for initiating:** Immediately — the Ethics Council is referenced in [[Book II - Governance Framework]] and should be constituted before any Class I decisions are made  
**Composition:** 3–5 independent humans; no AI voting members

---

#### Council: Community Council
**Status:** Planned; timing TBD  
**Trigger for initiating:** When Alpha Proxima develops an external community, publication, or partnership function

---

#### Infrastructure: Vector Database (Phase 2)
**Status:** Planned  
**Trigger for initiating:** When the knowledge base in the Vault reaches a scale where full-text search is insufficient for retrieval  
**Proposed technology:** Open-source vector database (Chroma, Weaviate, or equivalent) deployed locally or on private infrastructure  
**Chief Memory Architect role:** Activated at this milestone

---

#### Infrastructure: Home Server (Phase 3)
**Status:** Planned  
**Trigger for initiating:** When Alpha Proxima is ready to operate core AI functions on private infrastructure  
**Design goal:** Full independence from external AI providers for core memory and reasoning functions

### What Expansion Never Changes

Regardless of how many departments, councils, engines, or systems are added, these elements remain constant:

- [[Book I - The Constitution]] as the supreme governing instrument
- The decision class taxonomy (Class I–IV)
- The principle that roles are permanent and engines are replaceable
- The principle that knowledge belongs to Alpha Proxima
- LUMIAION's role as Constitutional Intelligence Core
- The Vault as the canonical knowledge store
- The Founder's supreme authority

These are the fixed points around which everything else expands.
