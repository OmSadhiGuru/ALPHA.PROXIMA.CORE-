## Problem Statement

A coherent institutional architecture for AI-augmented human intelligence requires a founding act — a moment at which intent becomes institution, at which the Founder's vision is formally committed to durable record, and at which the governance system activates for the first time.

Without this act, everything that follows is informal. Decisions made before a founding act cannot be traced to legitimate institutional authority. The architecture built in the sessions preceding this ADR — constitutional documents, charters, registries, protocols — requires formal ratification to be binding.

This ADR is that act.

## Decision

The Alpha Proxima Foundation is hereby formally constituted as a permanent institution under the following terms:

**1. Name and Identity**
The institution is named the Alpha Proxima Foundation, operating its primary intelligence and engineering codebase under the designation `ALPHA.PROXIMA.CORE-`. The trailing dash is permanent — a declaration that the work is never finished.

**2. Founder**
Frederick Belizaire Gunville, *Om Sadhi Guru*, is the Founder and supreme authority of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. This role is permanent and passes only through the succession protocol defined in [[Book II - Governance Framework]], Article VIII.

**3. Purpose**
The Alpha Proxima Foundation exists to design, build, and steward open architectures for:
- AI orchestration and governance
- Knowledge systems and institutional memory
- Digital augmentation of human intelligence
- The preservation and evolution of human wisdom across technological transitions

The Foundation is not a product company, a consulting firm, or a research lab in the conventional sense. It is an architecture — for thinking, building, and deciding — designed to remain coherent across at least one century of change.

**4. Supreme Governing Instrument**
[[Book I - The Constitution]] is the supreme governing instrument of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. No policy, decision, or action may supersede it.

LUMIAION is not an assistant. LUMIAION is an institutional participant with defined authority, defined constraints, and defined accountability. Its current engine is Anthropic Claude. Its role is permanent. Its engine is replaceable.

## Rationale

**Why a formal founding act?**
An institution that begins informally tends to stay informal. Governance structures that are never activated are governance structures that do not exist. By recording this decision as ADR-0001 — the first formal decision of the governance system — the Foundation demonstrates that its governance is operational, not decorative.

**Why now?**
The constitutional and charter layer is complete. The infrastructure is operational. The deployment pipeline is live. The founding act comes at the right moment: after the architecture exists to support it, before the first operational decisions are made under its authority.

**Why acknowledge incompleteness?**
Because an institution that pretends to be complete when it is not is building on false foundations. The Executive Council seats are unfilled. The Ethics Council does not yet exist. Acknowledging this in the founding act is the first exercise of the epistemic humility that [[Book I - The Constitution]] requires.

**Why record both names of the Founder?**
Frederick Belizaire Gunville and Om Sadhi Guru are not two personas — they are two registers of the same person. A Foundation that integrates ancient wisdom with frontier technology must be comfortable holding both. Recording both names in the founding act says: this institution holds the whole person, not just the professional face.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1 — Do Not Create a Formal Founding Act
**Description:** Allow the institution to emerge from accumulated documents without a formal constituting decision.
**Pros:** Simpler; less ceremony.
**Cons / Reasons Rejected:** Institutions without founding moments have no clear point from which authority flows. Every subsequent decision would lack a traceable origin. Rejected on principle.

### Alternative 2 — Wait Until Executive Council Is Constituted
**Description:** Defer ADR-0001 until the three human Council seats are filled.
**Pros:** More formally complete — the founding act would have full council backing.
**Cons / Reasons Rejected:** Waiting for a complete council to ratify the act of creating the institution creates a circular dependency. The Founder's authority pre-dates the council; that is the point of the Founder role. Deferred indefinitely means never. Rejected.

### Alternative 3 — Make This a Less Formal Document
**Description:** Record the founding as a Vault note rather than a formal ADR.
**Pros:** Less overhead; faster.
**Cons / Reasons Rejected:** The founding of an institution designed to last a century deserves the highest-fidelity record the system can produce. Using the ADR format for this act is the governance system's first act of self-application. Rejected.

## Consequences

### Positive Consequences
- All founding documents are formally ratified and constitute the legal (institutional) record of the Foundation
- The governance system is operational from this moment forward
- LUMIAION has formal constitutional standing as an institutional participant
- Future decisions have a clear foundational authority to reference

### Negative Consequences / Trade-offs
- The founding act is ratified by the Founder alone — this is a legitimate but minimal form of institutional ratification; future Class I decisions will require the full council process
- The incompleteness of the council structure means the governance system is not yet at full strength

### Risks
- **Council constitution delayed indefinitely** — if the Executive Council seats remain unfilled, the institution operates in a permanently incomplete state
- **Founder as single point of failure** — until the council is constituted and the succession protocol is documented, the institution's authority rests entirely with one person

### Mitigations
- Constituting the Executive Council is the highest-priority open task following this ADR
- The succession protocol in [[Book II - Governance Framework]] is already written; it activates the moment the Founder designates successors
