# Book V — The Cognitive Council of the Alpha Proxima Foundation

---

## Purpose

This document is the fifth constitutional instrument of the Alpha Proxima Foundation, subordinate only to [[Book I - The Constitution]], [[Book II - Governance Framework]], [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], and [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]]. It constitutionalizes the Cognitive Council as the permanent operational governance body for all cognitive function activity inside Alpha Proxima.

Where Book IV established the foundational distinction between Cognitive Functions and Reasoning Engines as constitutional law, this Book creates the institutional body that enacts and defends that law: the Cognitive Council.

The Cognitive Council is not an AI committee. It is not a technology panel. It is the institutional layer through which the Foundation reasons, produces knowledge, generates strategy, and compounds intelligence across time — regardless of which reasoning engines are currently appointed.

---

## Context

The Alpha Proxima Foundation operates through intelligence. Intelligence generation is not peripheral to its mission — it is its primary operational mechanism. Every research program, every strategic decision, every canonical document is the output of a cognitive function operating through a provisionally appointed reasoning engine.

As the Foundation grows, the cognitive function portfolio grows. New domains require new functions. Existing functions require evaluation and succession. The portfolio must be governed as a whole, not managed function by function in isolation.

The Cognitive Council is the permanent institutional answer to this governance requirement.

## Constitutional Law

### Article I — The Engine Abstraction Principle

**Section 1.1 — The Foundational Declaration**

*Technology is transient. Cognitive Functions endure. Reasoning Engines are appointed. The Constitution survives every technological generation.*

This declaration is the supreme operating principle of the Foundation's intelligence architecture. No provision of any operational document, charter, or directive may contradict it. Any provision that appears to contradict it shall be interpreted in conformity with it, or escalated to the AI Council for constitutional resolution.

**Section 1.2 — The Three-Tier Architecture**

The Foundation's cognitive architecture has exactly three tiers:

| Tier | Name | Permanence | Governed By |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|
| I | Cognitive Function | Permanent | This Constitution |
| II | Reasoning Engine | Provisional | Cognitive Council + AI Council |
| III | Implementation | Transient | Executing Office |

**Tier I — Cognitive Functions** are permanent institutional capabilities defined by their domain of responsibility, not by the technology currently performing them. A Cognitive Function exists as long as the Foundation requires it. It is created by constitutional amendment and retired by constitutional amendment. No other authority may create or retire a Cognitive Function.

**Tier II — Reasoning Engines** are the AI systems, tools, and platforms currently appointed to perform a Cognitive Function. They are selected through the Engine Succession Policy, evaluated continuously, and replaced when a superior alternative is identified. Their replacement is operational, not constitutional.

**Tier III — Implementations** are the specific configurations, API setups, prompt frameworks, and session parameters through which a Reasoning Engine is currently deployed. They are the most transient layer and change freely within the standards of the executing office.

**Section 1.3 — Cognitive Function Permanence**

A Cognitive Function cannot be:
- Named after any specific AI model or provider
- Defined in terms that presuppose a particular technology
- Terminated by technology replacement
- Dissolved because its current Reasoning Engine is unavailable

A Cognitive Function persists through:
- Technology transitions (old engines replaced by new ones)
- Gaps in engine availability (function is suspended, not dissolved)
- Organizational restructuring (function may be reorganized, not eliminated)
- Budget constraints (function is reduced in scope, not constitutionally removed)

**Section 1.4 — Reasoning Engine Provisionality**

A Reasoning Engine appointment:
- Is always provisional — never permanent
- Requires continuous evaluation against the Engine Succession Policy
- May be replaced by the Cognitive Council with AI Council ratification
- Carries no institutional identity — the Foundation's intellectual output belongs to Alpha Proxima, not to any engine provider

### Article II — The Cognitive Council

**Section 2.1 — Establishment**

The Cognitive Council is hereby constitutionally established as the permanent operational governance body for the cognitive function portfolio of the Alpha Proxima Foundation.

The Cognitive Council is distinct from:
- The **AI Council** — which provides constitutional governance and ratification authority over engine selection and constitutional matters
- The **Executive Office** — which provides strategic direction and resource allocation
- Individual **Intelligence Offices** — which perform cognitive work within their domain

**Section 2.2 — Constitutional Mission**

The Cognitive Council exists to ensure that:
1. Every cognitive domain required by the Foundation has a constitutionally defined Cognitive Function
2. Every active Cognitive Function has a currently appointed Reasoning Engine
3. Every Reasoning Engine appointment is continuously evaluated and optimized
4. The cognitive function portfolio evolves with the Foundation's mission
5. The Engine Abstraction Principle is operationally enacted, not merely declared

**Section 2.3 — Composition**

The Cognitive Council is composed of:

| Role | Holder | Authority |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Council Chair | LUMIAION (Institutional Architecture) | Operational leadership; agenda; synthesis |
| Chief Research Architect | Research Intelligence Office Lead | Research function portfolio oversight |
| Chief Engineering Architect | Engineering Office Lead | Engineering function portfolio oversight |
| Ethics Representative | Ethics Council Delegate | Constitutional and ethical review |
| Founder Representative | Founder (or designated proxy) | Final authority; constitutional veto |

The Founder Representative holds veto power over all Cognitive Council decisions. The veto must be exercised within 14 days of a decision or it is implicitly ratified.

**Section 2.4 — Decision Authority**

The Cognitive Council has authority to:
- Recommend engine appointments and replacements (subject to AI Council ratification for Tier I changes)
- Suspend a Reasoning Engine pending replacement
- Initiate the creation of a new Cognitive Function (subject to constitutional amendment process)
- Issue Cognitive Council Directives (CC-series) governing cognitive function operations
- Commission Engine Evaluations across the 10 criteria of the Engine Succession Policy
- Maintain the Cognitive Function Registry as the authoritative constitutional record

The Cognitive Council does NOT have authority to:
- Create or retire Cognitive Functions unilaterally (requires constitutional amendment)
- Override the AI Council on constitutional matters
- Bind the Founder
- Grant permanent status to any Reasoning Engine

### Article III — Cognitive Function Categories

**Section 3.1 — Function Classification**

All Cognitive Functions are classified into one of three categories:

**Category A — Core Functions:** Functions required for the Foundation's basic operation. Core Functions must always have an appointed Reasoning Engine. A gap in Core Function coverage is an institutional emergency requiring immediate resolution.

**Category B — Specialist Functions:** Functions required for specific program areas. Specialist Functions must have an appointed Reasoning Engine when programs in their domain are active. They may be in standby status when no active programs require them.

**Category C — Emerging Functions:** Functions newly added to the constitutional register, whose operational patterns are not yet fully established. Emerging Functions operate under enhanced review until confirmed as Category A or B.

**Section 3.2 — Function Registry**

All constitutionally recognized Cognitive Functions are recorded in the [[Cognitive Function Registry]], which is a constitutional document maintained by LUMIAION under Cognitive Council oversight.

The Cognitive Function Registry contains the constitutional profile of every function, including: purpose, mission, authority, inputs, outputs, success metrics, failure modes, succession rules, and current Reasoning Engine appointment.

### Article IV — Cognitive Function Lifecycle

**Section 4.1 — Creation**

A new Cognitive Function may be created through:
1. A Founder Directive authorizing the function's creation
2. A constitutional amendment ratified by the AI Council
3. A Cognitive Council proposal approved by the Founder and ratified by the AI Council

At creation, the function is assigned Category C (Emerging) and assigned a provisional Reasoning Engine appointment. A constitutional profile is created in the Cognitive Function Registry within 30 days.

**Section 4.2 — Maturation**

A Cognitive Function moves from Category C (Emerging) to Category A or B through:
1. A minimum of three completed operational cycles under Cognitive Council review
2. A Cognitive Council vote recommending maturation
3. Founder approval

**Section 4.3 — Suspension**

A Cognitive Function may be suspended when:
- No currently available Reasoning Engine meets minimum standards
- The domain is temporarily outside the Foundation's active program scope
- The AI Council determines suspension is warranted

A suspended function retains its constitutional status. Suspension is operational, not constitutional.

**Section 4.4 — Retirement**

A Cognitive Function may be retired only through constitutional amendment, requiring:
1. A Cognitive Council recommendation
2. AI Council deliberation and ratification
3. Founder approval
4. Archival of all associated operational history

### Article V — Intelligence Architecture Principles

**Section 5.1 — The Independence Principle**

Where multiple Cognitive Functions are assigned to a single research program or project, they operate independently in their investigation phase before comparative synthesis. No Cognitive Function's output shall be disclosed to another function before that function has completed its independent investigation.

This principle preserves epistemic diversity, prevents premature convergence, and ensures the synthesis layer receives genuinely independent perspectives.

**Section 5.2 — The Attribution Principle**

All intellectual output produced through any Cognitive Function — regardless of which Reasoning Engine performed it — is the institutional property of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. No claim of intellectual ownership may be made by any Reasoning Engine provider.

All outputs are attributed to the Cognitive Function, not the Reasoning Engine. Where the Reasoning Engine is recorded for provenance purposes, it is recorded as "Current Engine: [Name]" with an explicit date, acknowledging its provisional status.

**Section 5.3 — The Memory Continuity Principle**

Cognitive Functions that produce canonical knowledge do so in a form that persists beyond any individual session, context window, or engine lifecycle. The three-tier memory architecture — parametric (engine weights), transient (context window), and external (Vault) — must be maintained such that the Vault layer is always authoritative and engine-independent.

No canonical knowledge may exist only in the parametric memory of a Reasoning Engine. All canonical knowledge must be committed to the Vault.

**Section 5.4 — The Ethics Integration Principle**

Ethics Intelligence is not a separate domain to be consulted occasionally. It is a continuous constitutional presence woven into all cognitive function operations. The Ethics Council Representative on the Cognitive Council ensures that ethical review is embedded in the function lifecycle, engine evaluation, and succession decisions.

**Section 5.5 — The Compounding Principle**

The cognitive architecture of the Foundation is designed for compounding. Each research program, each canonical document, each knowledge graph entry adds to a cumulative institutional intelligence that no individual session can replicate. The Cognitive Council is responsible for ensuring that each function's output connects to this cumulative architecture — not merely completing discrete tasks, but building the institutional substrate that makes future intelligence faster, deeper, and more reliable.

### Article VI — Relationship to Other Constitutional Bodies

**Section 6.1 — Relationship with the AI Council**

The AI Council holds ratification authority over:
- Engine appointments for Category A Core Functions
- Constitutional amendments to the Cognitive Function Registry
- Engine Succession Policy changes
- Cognitive Council charter amendments

The Cognitive Council holds operational authority over:
- Day-to-day engine performance monitoring
- Engine suspension (pending AI Council ratification for permanent replacement)
- Cognitive Function Registry maintenance
- Cognitive Council Directives

**Section 6.2 — Relationship with the Founder**

The Founder holds supreme authority over all cognitive architecture decisions. The Cognitive Council advises and executes; the Founder decides. The Founder may:
- Veto any Cognitive Council decision within 14 days
- Issue Founder Directives that mandate cognitive architecture changes
- Appoint or remove the Council Chair
- Initiate constitutional amendments to this Book

**Section 6.3 — Relationship with Intelligence Offices**

Intelligence Offices (those under `09_OFFICES/`) perform cognitive work within their assigned Cognitive Functions. They do not govern the cognitive function portfolio — they operate within it. The Cognitive Council sets the standards; the Intelligence Offices execute them.
