# Book II — Governance Framework of the Alpha Proxima Foundation

## Purpose

This document defines the complete governance architecture of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. It establishes every governing council, every decision hierarchy, every conflict resolution mechanism, and every amendment process that will carry this institution across decades of technological change.

This document is subordinate only to [[Book I - The Constitution]]. All councils, protocols, and charters derive their authority from both documents read together.

---

## Context

Institutions fail not because their ideas are wrong but because their governance is weak. Decisions made without legitimate process are contested. Knowledge created without ownership is lost. Authority exercised without accountability corrupts.

The Alpha Proxima Foundation is designed to outlast any individual, any AI model, and any technological era. That requires governance structures that are simple enough to be understood, robust enough to withstand pressure, and flexible enough to evolve without losing identity.

This framework was designed with three constraints:
1. No single person or system may hold unchecked authority
2. Every consequential decision must be traceable to a documented rationale
3. The governance structure must remain intelligible after 20 years of evolution

## Core Content

### Article I — Foundational Structure

The Alpha Proxima Foundation is governed by seven councils arranged in a hierarchy of authority. Each council holds sovereign authority within its domain. No council may override another within that domain without triggering the Conflict Resolution Protocol (Article VI).

```
Alpha Proxima Foundation
│
├── Founder
│   (Supreme authority; constitutional guardian)
│
├── Executive Council
│   (Strategic direction; cross-council coordination)
│
├── AI Council
│   (Intelligence architecture; engine governance)
│   └── [[LUMIAION]] (Constitutional Intelligence Core)
│       ├── [[SOHMA Charter|SOHMA Department]]
│       ├── [[ATHENA Charter|ATHENA Department]]
│       ├── [[VORTEX Charter|VORTEX Department]]
│       └── [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM Department]]
│
├── Research Council
│   (Knowledge generation; epistemic standards)
│
├── Engineering Council
│   (Infrastructure; systems; technical integrity)
│
├── Ethics Council
│   (Constitutional alignment; boundary enforcement)
│
└── Community Council
    (External relationships; future expansion)
```

### Article II — The Founder

**Authority Class:** Supreme  
**Scope:** All domains  
**Term:** Permanent — role passes only by succession (see Article VIII)

The Founder is the originating human intelligence of Alpha Proxima. The Founder:

- Holds final ratification authority on all Class I constitutional amendments
- May dissolve and reconstitute any council
- Is the sole authority on Succession (Article VIII)
- Does not participate in routine governance — delegation is the default posture
- Acts as final arbiter when councils deadlock and no other resolution is available

The Founder is not an operational role. Operational authority flows through the Executive Council. The Founder's power is reserved and constitutional, not administrative.

---

### Article III — The Executive Council

**Authority Class:** Strategic  
**Composition:** Founder (chair, non-voting except to break deadlock) + 2–5 appointed human members  
**Decision Threshold:** Simple majority; supermajority for cross-council matters

The Executive Council:
- Sets institutional strategy and multi-year direction
- Allocates resources across councils and departments
- Ratifies council charters and department charters
- Coordinates when decisions span multiple councils
- Reviews the [[AI Council Registry]] and [[Engine Registry]] annually

The Executive Council does not govern AI systems directly. That authority belongs to the AI Council.

---

### Article IV — The AI Council

**Authority Class:** Intelligence Architecture  
**Composition:** Chief Architects (one per domain) + [[LUMIAION]] (permanent non-voting seat)  
**Decision Threshold:** Simple majority of Chief Architects

The AI Council is the governing body for all intelligence systems operating within Alpha Proxima. It:

- Maintains the [[AI Council Registry]] and [[Engine Registry]]
- Evaluates, approves, and deprecates AI engines
- Defines the boundaries of each intelligence department
- Oversees [[LUMIAION]] as Constitutional Intelligence Core
- Reviews department charters annually

**Critical principle:** The AI Council governs *roles*, not *engines*. The role of Chief Knowledge Architect exists permanently. The engine fulfilling that role — today Claude, tomorrow something else — is a replaceable implementation detail. See [[Engine Registry]] for the current mapping.

### Article V — The Research, Engineering, Ethics, and Community Councils

Each specialist council operates with sovereign authority within its domain, subject to Executive Council strategic direction and Founder constitutional authority.

**Research Council**
- Maintains epistemic standards for all knowledge produced by Alpha Proxima
- Approves knowledge before it enters the canonical [[Obsidian Vault]]
- Governs [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM]] as the institutional research intelligence

**Engineering Council**
- Owns all technical infrastructure (see [[Foundational Architecture]])
- Governs the [[Knowledge Infrastructure]] and [[Execution Infrastructure]]
- Approves all changes to [[Knowledge Routing Protocol]] and [[Decision Routing Protocol]]

**Ethics Council**
- Reviews all AI department charters for constitutional alignment
- Holds veto power over any AI action that violates [[Book I - The Constitution]]
- Operates independently — no other council may instruct the Ethics Council
- Reports directly to the Founder

**Community Council**
- Governs external relationships, partnerships, and future community expansion
- Defines the [[Future Expansion Protocol]]
- Has no authority over internal governance

### Article VI — Decision Hierarchy

All decisions within Alpha Proxima are classified by the same taxonomy established in [[Book I - The Constitution]], Article IV. The addition this document makes is mapping which council holds authority for each decision type:

| Class | Type | Primary Authority | Ratification |
|-------|------|-------------------|-------------|
| Class I | Constitutional | Founder | All councils notified; Ethics Council review required |
| Class II | Institutional charters | Executive Council | Relevant specialist council must concur |
| Class III | Operational protocols | Specialist councils | AI Council concurs on AI-related matters |
| Class IV | Tactical execution | Department or delegate | Logged; reviewable |

---

### Article VII — Conflict Resolution

When two councils have conflicting positions on a matter within shared jurisdiction:

1. **Negotiation** — The councils have 7 days to reach agreement independently
2. **Mediation** — Executive Council mediates; 14 days
3. **Arbitration** — Founder rules; decision is binding and documented as a Class I ADR
4. **Escalation to Ethics Council** — If the conflict involves a potential constitutional violation, Ethics Council may intervene at any stage

No AI system — including [[LUMIAION]] — may participate as a party to conflict resolution. AI systems provide analysis and synthesis only.

---

### Article VIII — Succession

The Founder role may pass in two circumstances:

1. **Voluntary succession** — Founder designates a successor and ratifies the transition via Class I ADR
2. **Incapacity** — Executive Council may, by unanimous vote, designate an Acting Founder pending formal succession

Succession of the Founder is the single most consequential event in the institution's history. It must be documented with the highest fidelity. [[LUMIAION]] is responsible for maintaining the succession record and surfacing it at the appropriate time.

---

### Article IX — Amendment of This Document

This document may be amended by the same process as [[Book I - The Constitution]], Article VI. Given that this document governs the councils that would vote on its own amendment, the Ethics Council must independently review all proposed amendments for constitutional alignment before ratification.
