# Alpha Council

## Purpose

This note is the working reference for the Alpha Council — the supreme deliberative and executive body of [[Alpha Proxima Core]]. It covers mandate, composition, operating procedures, and the current decision log. The authoritative structural definition lives in the [[Institutional Registry]].

---

## Context

The Alpha Council was constituted as the founding act of [[Alpha Proxima Core]] governance. Its creation is defined in [[Book I - The Constitution]], Article III, Section 3.1, and its full registry entry is in [[Institutional Registry]], Section 1.

The Council exists because consequential decisions — about what to build, how to govern AI participation, and what values to enshrine — require a body with clear authority, clear accountability, and a durable record of its reasoning.

---

## Core Content

### Mandate

The Alpha Council holds supreme deliberative and executive authority. It is responsible for:

- Ratifying and amending [[Book I - The Constitution]]
- Chartering and dissolving Working Groups
- Overseeing the development and governance of [[LUMIAION]]
- Maintaining the integrity of this Vault as institutional memory
- Resolving disputes between participants or bodies
- Reviewing [[LUMIAION]]'s delegated scope quarterly

### Decision Authority

| Class | Type | Threshold |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Class I | Constitutional amendments | Supermajority (≥ ⅔) + 14-day deliberation |
| Class II | Institutional (charters, registrations) | Simple majority + [[ADR Template\|ADR]] |
| Class III | Operational (projects, protocols) | Delegate or Working Group + ADR |
| Class IV | Tactical (day-to-day) | Single responsible actor, logged |

---

### Operating Procedures

**Deliberation format:** Asynchronous by default. Synchronous sessions may be called by any Council member.

**Proposal process:**
1. Submitter drafts a [[Concept Note Template|Concept Note]]
2. Minimum review period: 14 days (Class I), 7 days recommended (Class II)
3. Council deliberates — log captured in the Concept Note
4. Vote taken; outcome recorded in an [[ADR Template|ADR]]
5. ADR filed in `04_DECISIONS/`

**Quorum:** *To Be Defined* — see Open Questions.

**Dissent:** All formal dissent is recorded in the relevant ADR. Dissent is not failure; it is institutional memory.
