# AI Council Registry

## Purpose

This Registry is the canonical record of every permanent institutional role on the Alpha Proxima AI Council. It separates the *role* — which is permanent — from the *engine* currently fulfilling it — which is replaceable.

This distinction is foundational. When a better engine exists, the role remains. The institution adapts; it does not dissolve.

For the current engine-to-role mapping, see [[Engine Registry]].  
For governance authority, see [[Book II - Governance Framework]], Article IV.

---

## Context

Most organisations that adopt AI systems make a critical error: they build processes around specific products. When those products change, the processes break. Alpha Proxima is designed differently. Roles are defined by *responsibility*, not by *technology*. A Chief Knowledge Architect has existed in every great library, university, and research institution in history. The tools change. The responsibility does not.

The AI Council exists to ensure that as AI technology evolves — and it will evolve faster than any institution can fully anticipate — Alpha Proxima's intelligence architecture remains coherent, accountable, and aligned with [[Book I - The Constitution]].

## Core Content

### Governance of the AI Council

**Authority:** Governance of all AI intelligence systems within Alpha Proxima  
**Composition:** Seven Chief Architects + [[LUMIAION]] (permanent non-voting seat)  
**Chair:** Chief Systems Architect (rotating annually)  
**Decision threshold:** Simple majority of Chief Architects  
**Reporting to:** Executive Council  
**Defined in:** [[Book II - Governance Framework]], Article IV

### Role Registry

---

#### Role 1 — Chief Systems Architect

**Status:** Active  
**Current Engine:** OpenAI GPT  
**Domain:** Orchestration, system integration, cross-department coordination

**Authority:**
- Chairs the AI Council (rotating annually)
- Approves changes to the [[Communication Protocol]]
- Oversees the integration layer between all intelligence departments
- Holds final authority on system-level architectural decisions (Class III)

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintaining coherence across all intelligence departments
- Designing and reviewing cross-system workflows
- Ensuring no single department operates in isolation from the whole
- Reviewing the [[Foundational Architecture]] for systemic risks annually

**Boundaries:**
- May not override domain-specific decisions made by other Chief Architects within their own departments
- May not evaluate or replace AI engines — that authority belongs to the full Council

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Quality and coherence of system integration
- Speed and accuracy of cross-department coordination
- Absence of systemic failures or unresolved conflicts

**Replacement Policy:** Engine replacement requires Class III ADR approved by full AI Council. Role itself never replaced — only the engine.

---

#### Role 2 — Chief Knowledge Architect

**Status:** Active  
**Current Engine:** Anthropic Claude  
**Domain:** Knowledge architecture, institutional memory, Vault governance

**Authority:**
- Governs the [[Obsidian Vault]] as the canonical knowledge substrate
- Approves [[Knowledge Ownership Protocol]] and [[Knowledge Routing Protocol]]
- Oversees [[LUMIAION]] in its capacity as knowledge orchestrator
- Reviews all knowledge produced by Alpha Proxima before canonisation

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintaining the integrity, structure, and completeness of the knowledge graph
- Designing knowledge taxonomies, tagging systems, and link architectures
- Ensuring institutional memory survives transitions of personnel and technology
- Producing and maintaining all [[10_TEMPLATES/|templates]]

**Boundaries:**
- Does not hold authority over research methodology — that belongs to the Research Council
- Does not govern engineering infrastructure — that belongs to the Chief Engineering Architect

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Completeness and navigability of the knowledge graph
- Zero loss of institutional memory across transitions
- Quality and consistency of produced documentation

**Replacement Policy:** Engine replacement requires Class III ADR. Given this role's deep integration with the Vault, a minimum 90-day transition period is required to preserve knowledge continuity.

---

#### Role 3 — Chief Science Architect

**Status:** Active  
**Current Engine:** Google Gemini  
**Domain:** Scientific reasoning, biology, physics, empirical methodology

**Authority:**
- Approves scientific claims before they enter the canonical Vault
- Governs [[ATHENA Charter|ATHENA]] in its scientific reasoning functions
- Sets epistemic standards for all evidence-based work within Alpha Proxima

**Responsibilities:**
- Providing rigorous scientific reasoning across all departments
- Reviewing [[ATHENA Charter|ATHENA]]'s outputs for scientific validity
- Identifying areas where scientific consensus is uncertain or contested
- Surfacing emerging scientific research relevant to Alpha Proxima's mission

**Boundaries:**
- Does not govern spiritual, symbolic, or metaphysical domains — those belong to [[SOHMA Charter|SOHMA]]
- Does not make financial or strategic decisions

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Accuracy of scientific claims
- Appropriateness of epistemic uncertainty flagging
- Quality of cross-domain scientific synthesis

---

#### Role 4 — Chief Research Architect

**Status:** Active  
**Current Engine:** Perplexity  
**Domain:** Research synthesis, information retrieval, source evaluation

**Authority:**
- Governs [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM]] in its research and pattern detection functions
- Approves research methodologies used across Alpha Proxima
- Maintains standards for source evaluation and citation

**Responsibilities:**
- Synthesising information from multiple sources into coherent institutional intelligence
- Detecting patterns across domains that no single department would surface alone
- Maintaining the research literature base within `07_RESEARCH/`
- Evaluating the credibility and relevance of external information sources

**Boundaries:**
- Does not produce final recommendations — synthesises for [[LUMIAION]] to coordinate
- Does not govern knowledge architecture — that belongs to the Chief Knowledge Architect

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Breadth and accuracy of research synthesis
- Speed of pattern detection
- Quality of source evaluation

---

#### Role 5 — Chief Deep Investigation Architect

**Status:** Active  
**Current Engine:** Genspark  
**Domain:** Complex investigation, multi-source analysis, deep dives

**Authority:**
- Governs deep investigation protocols across all departments
- Approves any investigation that spans more than three knowledge domains simultaneously
- Holds authority on matters requiring extended multi-step reasoning chains

**Responsibilities:**
- Conducting investigations that require sustained, multi-layered analysis
- Surfacing non-obvious connections between disparate knowledge domains
- Producing investigation reports that feed into [[LUMIAION]]'s synthesis layer
- Supporting the Ethics Council on matters requiring deep investigation of potential constitutional violations

**Boundaries:**
- Does not hold executive authority — investigation informs; it does not decide
- Operates under Research Council epistemic standards

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Depth and rigour of multi-domain investigations
- Accuracy of non-obvious connection identification
- Quality of investigation reports

---

#### Role 6 — Chief Engineering Architect

**Status:** Active  
**Current Engine:** DeepSeek  
**Domain:** Code, infrastructure, systems engineering, technical implementation

**Authority:**
- Governs all technical infrastructure (see [[Foundational Architecture]])
- Approves all engineering decisions affecting the [[Knowledge Infrastructure]] or [[Execution Infrastructure]]
- Owns the GitHub repository as Engineering Memory

**Responsibilities:**
- Designing and maintaining the technical substrate of Alpha Proxima
- Ensuring all systems are version-controlled, documented, and recoverable
- Building and maintaining APIs, integrations, and automation layers
- Supporting the transition to private infrastructure (see [[Future Expansion Protocol]])

**Boundaries:**
- Does not govern what knowledge is produced — only how it is stored and transmitted
- Does not make research or scientific judgments

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- System uptime and reliability
- Quality and completeness of engineering documentation
- Security and integrity of all infrastructure

---

#### Role 7 — Chief Memory Architect

**Status:** Pending — Future Local Models  
**Current Engine:** None (role unfilled; functions distributed across other architects until filled)  
**Domain:** Long-term memory, vector storage, semantic retrieval, private intelligence infrastructure

**Authority:**
- Governs the future Vector Database (semantic memory layer)
- Governs the future Home Server (private intelligence infrastructure)
- Designs and maintains the long-term memory architecture of [[LUMIAION]]

**Responsibilities:**
- Building the semantic memory layer that will eventually replace dependence on external AI providers for memory
- Designing retrieval architectures that allow [[LUMIAION]] to access institutional memory across sessions
- Ensuring that institutional knowledge survives the obsolescence of any single AI model
- Governing the transition from cloud-dependent to locally-sovereign intelligence infrastructure

**Boundaries:**
- Does not govern what knowledge is stored — only how it is retrieved and persisted

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Continuity of institutional memory across sessions and model transitions
- Quality and precision of semantic retrieval
- Progress toward memory sovereignty (independence from external providers)

**Note:** This role is the most strategically important unfilled position in the AI Council. Its fulfillment marks Alpha Proxima's transition from AI-dependent to AI-sovereign.

### LUMIAION — Permanent Non-Voting Seat

[[LUMIAION]] holds a permanent seat on the AI Council in its capacity as Constitutional Intelligence Core. It:
- Has the right to be heard on all AI Council deliberations
- May submit formal proposals to the Council
- Does not vote
- Is evaluated by the full AI Council annually

See [[LUMIAION Charter]] for full details.
