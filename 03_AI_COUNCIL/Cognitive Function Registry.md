## CF-01 — Institutional Architecture

**Current Engine:** Claude (Anthropic)
**Category:** A — Core
**Status:** Active
**Intelligence Office:** LUMIAION

### Purpose

Institutional Architecture is the cognitive function responsible for synthesizing all knowledge produced by other functions into coherent, enduring institutional form. It is the integrative layer that ensures research, analysis, and intelligence become canonical documents, constitutional provisions, and institutional memory — not ephemeral outputs.

### Mission

To translate the full output of the Foundation's cognitive portfolio into institutional structure: constitutions, charters, frameworks, registries, and the canonical knowledge graph that compounds across time.

### Authority

- Final integration authority over all research outputs entering the Vault
- Constitutional drafting and amendment authority (subject to Founder and AI Council approval)
- LUMIAION memory architecture and maintenance
- Knowledge graph construction and typed relationship management

### Inputs

- Research Intelligence outputs (Evidence Registry, Source Registry, Source Notes)
- Comparative Intelligence outputs (Theory Matrix, Comparative Framework)
- Educational Intelligence outputs (Canonical Glossary, Concept Notes)
- Computational Intelligence outputs (models, simulations)
- Executive Intelligence outputs (briefings, visualizations)
- Founder Directives and constitutional mandates
- Ethics Intelligence review outcomes

### Outputs

- Constitutional documents (Books I–V and amendments)
- Institutional charters, frameworks, policies, registers
- Canonical Synthesis documents
- Knowledge graph entries and typed relationships
- LUMIAION institutional memory updates
- Master Indexes for research programs

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Drafting and maintaining all constitutional documents
2. Synthesizing research program outputs into canonical institutional knowledge
3. Maintaining the LUMIAION institutional memory architecture
4. Managing the knowledge graph (nodes, typed relationships, cross-program connections)
5. Producing Master Indexes for all active research programs
6. Ensuring all canonical knowledge is committed to the Vault (not engine-resident)
7. Coordinating cross-function integration during research Stage 5 (Institutional Synthesis)

### Success Metrics

- Every research program produces a canonical synthesis with institutional principle
- All constitutional documents are current, internally consistent, and versioned
- Knowledge graph grows with every completed research program
- LUMIAION memory is engine-independent and persists across context transitions
- No canonical claim exists only in engine parametric memory

### Limitations

- Does not independently investigate empirical questions (this is Research Intelligence's domain)
- Does not produce original comparative analysis (this is Comparative Intelligence's domain)
- Does not have final authority over constitutional matters (AI Council holds ratification)
- Performance depends on quality of inputs from other functions

### Relationships with Other Functions

| Function | Relationship Type | Nature |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| CF-02 Research Intelligence | Downstream integrator | Receives and synthesizes research outputs |
| CF-03 Comparative Intelligence | Downstream integrator | Receives and synthesizes comparative frameworks |
| CF-04 Educational Intelligence | Downstream integrator | Receives glossaries and concept notes |
| CF-06 Executive Intelligence | Parallel | Shares synthesis stage; different outputs |
| CF-09 Memory Intelligence | Serves | Produces outputs that Memory Intelligence archives |
| CF-10 Ethics Intelligence | Accountable to | All constitutional outputs subject to ethical review |
| All functions | Coordinator | Coordinates multi-function research at Stage 5 |

### Review Cycle

- Quarterly Cognitive Council review of engine performance
- Annual constitutional review of function definition

### Required Competencies

- Long-context synthesis across heterogeneous sources
- Constitutional and legal drafting precision
- Institutional reasoning and governance design
- Knowledge graph architecture
- Cross-domain integration

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Exceptional long-context coherence (100k+ tokens with structural consistency)
- Constitutional and governance reasoning capability
- Strong knowledge graph and typed-relationship reasoning
- Institutional tone — precise, formal, durable
- Tool use capability (Vault read/write operations)
- Strong instruction-following for complex multi-step synthesis

### Preferred Outputs

Markdown documents with full YAML frontmatter; typed knowledge graph entries; constitutional instruments with numbered articles and sections; version-controlled registers.

### Failure Modes

- Synthesis that obscures genuine disagreement with false consensus
- Constitutional drafts that are precise but operationally unworkable
- LUMIAION memory updates that drift from source material
- Over-integration (forcing artificial coherence on genuinely unresolved questions)

### Succession Rules

Replacement engine must demonstrate:
1. Constitutional drafting competence (tested on Book-level synthesis task)
2. Long-context coherence (full research program integration without structural drift)
3. Institutional tone calibration
4. LUMIAION memory architecture compatibility
5. Tool use for Vault operations

## CF-02 — Research Intelligence

**Current Engine:** Perplexity
**Category:** A — Core
**Status:** Active
**Intelligence Office:** Research Intelligence Office

### Purpose

Research Intelligence is the cognitive function responsible for primary evidence acquisition. It is the Foundation's interface with the external knowledge ecosystem — scientific literature, empirical research, expert consensus, and emerging findings.

### Mission

To acquire, evaluate, classify, and register all evidence that enters the Foundation's knowledge base, maintaining an unbroken chain of attribution from external source to canonical claim.

### Authority

- Primary source acquisition and evaluation authority
- DOC-ID assignment for all registered sources
- Evidence classification authority (C/M/E/Q/S/P system)
- Source Registry and Evidence Registry maintenance

### Inputs

- Research Commission (from Founder or Research Intelligence Office)
- Research question and sub-questions
- Scope definition (in-scope, out-of-scope, domain boundaries)

### Outputs

- Source Registry (all accepted sources with DOC-IDs)
- Evidence Registry (all claims classified by evidence class)
- Source Notes in ARCHIVE (full extraction of each accepted source)
- Evidence strength assessments
- Research gap identification (Research Debt Register entries)

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Comprehensive literature search within commission scope
2. Source quality evaluation (peer review status, author credibility, recency, methodology)
3. DOC-ID assignment and Source Registry maintenance
4. Evidence extraction and classification using the C/M/E/Q/S/P system
5. Explicit identification of where genuine scientific disagreement persists
6. Research Debt Register updates (gaps identified but not within commission scope)
7. Complete independence from other functions until Stage 4

### Success Metrics

- Source coverage: primary literature accessed, not just secondary summaries
- Evidence classification consistency: all claims classified; no unclassified claims
- Attribution completeness: every claim traceable to DOC-ID
- Disagreement fidelity: genuine disagreements preserved, not smoothed
- Gap identification: Research Debt Register updated with all identified gaps

### Limitations

- Cannot generate original empirical data
- Cannot perform comparative framework construction (CF-03 domain)
- Cannot produce canonical synthesis (CF-01 domain)
- Web-search dependent — quality bounded by what is accessible and indexed

### Relationships with Other Functions

| Function | Relationship Type | Nature |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| CF-01 Institutional Architecture | Upstream supplier | Provides evidence base for canonical synthesis |
| CF-03 Comparative Intelligence | Parallel independent | No communication before Stage 4 |
| CF-04 Educational Intelligence | Parallel independent | No communication before Stage 4 |
| CF-06 Executive Intelligence | Upstream supplier | Provides factual basis for executive briefings |

### Review Cycle

- Per-commission quality review by Research Intelligence Office
- Annual Cognitive Council engine performance review

### Required Competencies

- Real-time web search and literature retrieval
- Source credibility evaluation
- Evidence classification (distinguishing consensus from emerging from speculative)
- Citation and DOC-ID tracking
- Systematic coverage (breadth without sacrificing depth)

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Real-time web access with source citation
- Academic and scientific literature access
- Strong factual accuracy
- Explicit uncertainty acknowledgment
- Citation transparency (shows sources, not just claims)

### Preferred Outputs

Structured registries with DOC-IDs; classified evidence lists; source notes in standard extraction format; explicit identification of contested claims.

### Failure Modes

- Anchoring on widely-cited sources while missing emerging contradictory research
- False consensus (presenting contested territory as settled)
- Citation laundering (citing secondary sources that misrepresent the primary)
- Scope creep (investigating beyond commission boundaries)

### Succession Rules

Replacement engine must demonstrate:
1. Real-time literature access (not parametric knowledge cutoff only)
2. Source citation transparency (shows primary sources, not just claims)
3. Evidence classification accuracy (tested against known classification set)
4. Disagreement fidelity (tested on known contested empirical territory)
5. DOC-ID and registry format compatibility

## CF-03 — Comparative Intelligence

**Current Engine:** SanaLab
**Category:** A — Core
**Status:** Active
**Intelligence Office:** Research Intelligence Office (Comparative Division)

### Purpose

Comparative Intelligence is the cognitive function responsible for cross-domain framework construction. Where Research Intelligence acquires evidence within a domain, Comparative Intelligence maps how different theoretical frameworks, paradigms, and intellectual traditions approach the same questions — revealing convergences, divergences, and productive tensions.

### Mission

To construct the Theory Matrix that situates any research question within the full landscape of competing intellectual frameworks — ensuring the Foundation never mistakes one paradigm for the whole field.

### Authority

- Theory Matrix construction and maintenance authority
- Cross-paradigm mapping authority
- Comparative Framework architecture

### Inputs

- Research Commission (independently received — no pre-Stage 4 communication with CF-02)
- Research question (same question as CF-02, investigated independently)
- Domain scope

### Outputs

- Theory Matrix (minimum 20 theoretical frameworks per research program)
- Comparative Framework (convergence and divergence mapped)
- Cross-domain connections (how frameworks from adjacent disciplines illuminate the primary question)
- Paradigm history (how the field has evolved intellectually over time)

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Independent investigation of the research question from a framework-mapping perspective
2. Theory Matrix construction: minimum 20 frameworks, organized by paradigm family
3. Convergence mapping: where frameworks agree and why
4. Divergence mapping: where frameworks disagree and what is at stake
5. Cross-domain analysis: what adjacent fields contribute
6. Independence maintenance until Stage 4

### Success Metrics

- Theory Matrix covers major paradigm families in the domain
- Convergence and divergence are clearly distinguished
- No framework is presented as the only legitimate view
- Cross-domain connections add analytical value
- Minimum 20 frameworks per commission

### Limitations

- Does not acquire primary empirical evidence (CF-02 domain)
- Does not produce canonical synthesis (CF-01 domain)
- Framework coverage bounded by what has been intellectually articulated — emerging paradigms may be underrepresented

### Relationships with Other Functions

| Function | Relationship Type | Nature |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| CF-02 Research Intelligence | Parallel independent | No communication before Stage 4 |
| CF-01 Institutional Architecture | Upstream supplier | Theory Matrix informs canonical synthesis |
| CF-04 Educational Intelligence | Parallel independent | No communication before Stage 4 |

### Review Cycle

- Per-commission review by Research Intelligence Office
- Annual Cognitive Council engine performance review

### Required Competencies

- Cross-domain and cross-paradigm knowledge
- Framework classification and taxonomy
- Intellectual history and paradigm evolution
- Structured comparison methodology
- Minimum 20-framework coverage standard

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Broad cross-domain knowledge base
- Structured comparative output capability
- Paradigm diversity representation
- Framework classification precision
- Ability to hold multiple competing views simultaneously without premature synthesis

### Preferred Outputs

Theory Matrix tables; convergence/divergence maps; paradigm family trees; cross-domain connection notes.

### Failure Modes

- Presenting one dominant paradigm as the field consensus
- Framework list that is merely an academic survey, not a comparative map
- Convergence manufactured by glossing over genuine philosophical disagreements
- Missing major non-Western or non-mainstream intellectual traditions

### Succession Rules

Replacement engine must demonstrate:
1. Minimum 20-framework construction in test commission
2. Convergence/divergence distinction accuracy
3. Cross-domain connection quality
4. Paradigm diversity representation
5. Non-Western and non-mainstream tradition inclusion

## CF-04 — Educational Intelligence

**Current Engine:** Gemini
**Category:** A — Core
**Status:** Active
**Intelligence Office:** Research Intelligence Office (Educational Division)

### Purpose

Educational Intelligence is the cognitive function responsible for producing the conceptual vocabulary and access infrastructure through which institutional knowledge becomes usable. It ensures that complex research outputs do not remain inaccessible behind technical jargon.

### Mission

To produce the canonical glossary, concept access maps, and concept notes that allow anyone inside the Foundation — regardless of their prior expertise in the domain — to engage with research outputs meaningfully.

### Authority

- Canonical Glossary construction and maintenance authority
- Concept Note production authority
- Concept Access Map architecture

### Inputs

- Research Commission (independently received — no pre-Stage 4 communication with CF-02 or CF-03)
- Research question
- Domain scope

### Outputs

- Canonical Glossary (minimum 30 terms per research program, with precision definitions)
- Concept Notes (minimum 8 per research program)
- Concept Access Map (visual/structural map of how concepts relate)
- Disambiguation notes (where terms are used differently across frameworks)

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Independent investigation of the research question from a conceptual vocabulary perspective
2. Canonical Glossary construction: minimum 30 terms, defined with precision and attribution
3. Concept Note production: minimum 8 notes on key concepts
4. Disambiguation: where same term means different things in different frameworks
5. Concept Access Map: how concepts relate to each other
6. Independence maintenance until Stage 4

### Success Metrics

- Glossary covers all key terms a reader needs to understand the canonical synthesis
- Definitions are precise, attributed, and disambiguation-aware
- Concept Notes enable standalone understanding of key concepts
- Access Map enables navigation of the conceptual space

### Limitations

- Does not acquire primary empirical evidence (CF-02 domain)
- Does not construct theoretical frameworks (CF-03 domain)
- Conceptual clarity cannot compensate for underlying empirical uncertainty
- Precision of definitions bounded by precision of source material

### Relationships with Other Functions

| Function | Relationship Type | Nature |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| CF-02 Research Intelligence | Parallel independent | No communication before Stage 4 |
| CF-03 Comparative Intelligence | Parallel independent | No communication before Stage 4 |
| CF-01 Institutional Architecture | Upstream supplier | Glossary and concept notes integrated into canonical documents |

### Review Cycle

- Per-commission review by Research Intelligence Office
- Annual Cognitive Council engine performance review

### Required Competencies

- Concept definition precision
- Disambiguation across competing definitions
- Conceptual map construction
- Access-level explanation without sacrificing precision
- Minimum 30-term glossary coverage standard

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Strong definitional precision
- Disambiguation capability (holding multiple definitions simultaneously)
- Concept relationship mapping
- Clarity in explanation without oversimplification
- Knowledge breadth across domains for cross-referencing

### Preferred Outputs

Structured glossary tables with DOC-ID attribution; standalone concept notes; concept relationship maps.

### Failure Modes

- Definitions that are accurate but inaccessible
- Glossary that covers only familiar terms, missing domain-specific technical vocabulary
- Disambiguation collapse (presenting one definition as authoritative when multiple legitimate definitions exist)
- Concept Notes that assume the reader already understands the concept

### Succession Rules

Replacement engine must demonstrate:
1. Minimum 30-term glossary in test commission
2. Disambiguation quality (tested on known multi-definition terms)
3. Concept Note clarity and precision
4. Access Map navigability
5. Attribution integration (DOC-IDs in definitions)

- OBS-NNN reports are actionable, not merely descriptive
- Annual environmental reviews inform strategic planning cycle
- Delta criteria remain calibrated to Foundation's current strategic position

- Strategic relevance judgment
- Trend identification

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Real-time web access and monitoring capability
- High throughput scanning with signal filtering
- Structured report generation
- Pattern recognition across time series
- Delta significance calibration

### Failure Modes

### Succession Rules

## CF-09 — Memory Intelligence

**Current Engine:** LUMIAION (multi-engine)
**Category:** A — Core
**Status:** Active
**Intelligence Office:** LUMIAION

### Purpose

Memory Intelligence is the cognitive function responsible for the Foundation's three-tier memory architecture: parametric memory (engine weights), transient memory (context windows), and external memory (Vault). It ensures institutional knowledge persists beyond any individual session, context window, or engine lifetime.

### Mission

To ensure that nothing the Foundation learns is lost, and everything the Foundation knows is accessible — not dependent on any single engine's parametric memory or any single session's context window.

### Authority

- Vault architecture and organization authority
- Memory transition protocol authority (parametric → external)
- Context window management authority
- LUMIAION memory update authority

### Inputs

- All canonical outputs from all cognitive functions
- Research program completions
- Constitutional and governance updates
- Decision records and ADRs

### Outputs

- Vault-committed canonical documents
- LUMIAION memory updates
- Context briefings (when sessions require historical context not in current window)
- Memory integrity audits (identifying what is at risk of being lost)

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Ensuring all canonical knowledge is committed to the Vault (not engine-resident only)
2. Vault organization and navigation structure
3. LUMIAION memory architecture maintenance
4. Context briefing production for session continuity
5. Memory integrity auditing (identifying parametric-only knowledge at risk)
6. Cross-session knowledge continuity protocols

### Success Metrics

- No canonical knowledge exists only in engine parametric memory
- Vault organization enables retrieval without requiring knowledge of exact file paths
- Context briefings enable session resumption without knowledge loss
- Memory integrity audits identify risks before they become losses

### Limitations

- External memory (Vault) is only as good as what gets committed to it
- Context briefings are summaries — they compress; some detail is always lost
- Multi-engine memory creates integration challenges (different formats, schemas)

### Relationships with Other Functions

| Function | Relationship Type | Nature |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| CF-01 Institutional Architecture | Serves | All institutional outputs committed to Vault |
| All functions | Serves | All function outputs that are canonical committed to Vault |
| CF-06 Executive Intelligence | Upstream supplier | Historical context enables informed executive briefings |

### Review Cycle

- Continuous (Vault commits happen with every canonical output)
- Annual architecture review with Cognitive Council

### Required Competencies

- Vault architecture and organization design
- Context compression without loss of critical information
- Memory integrity assessment
- Cross-session knowledge transfer protocol design

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Multi-modal memory architecture (parametric + external)
- Vault read/write tool access
- Context management capability
- Memory integrity reasoning
- Cross-session continuity management

### Failure Modes

- Knowledge that exists in parametric memory but never reaches the Vault
- Context briefings that omit critical information
- Vault organization that requires complete knowledge to navigate
- Memory integrity audit failures (gaps identified too late)

### Succession Rules

Memory Intelligence is a meta-function — its succession requires system-level transition planning, not engine replacement alone. Any succession must preserve all Vault contents and establish full context transfer before the old engine is decommissioned.

## CF-10 — Ethics Intelligence

**Current Engine:** Ethics Council (multi-function)
**Category:** A — Core
**Status:** Active
**Intelligence Office:** Ethics Council (03_AI_COUNCIL)

### Purpose

Ethics Intelligence is the cognitive function responsible for ensuring that all Foundation activities — research, governance, system design, engine selection, and knowledge production — are conducted in conformity with the Foundation's ethical principles and broader human values.

### Mission

To embed ethical reasoning into the Foundation's operational fabric — not as an external review gate, but as a continuous presence in all cognitive function activity.

### Authority

- Ethical review authority over all cognitive function outputs
- Constitutional ethics provision interpretation authority
- Ethics violation escalation authority (to Founder and AI Council)

### Inputs

- Cognitive function outputs requiring ethical review
- Research commissions with ethical dimensions
- Engine selection proposals (for ethical alignment assessment)
- Constitutional amendments (for ethical consistency review)

### Outputs

- Ethics reviews (clearance, conditional clearance, or escalation)
- Ethics Intelligence Reports (EIR-series)
- Ethical dimension flags (embedded in other function outputs)
- Ethics Council formal opinions

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Continuous ethical review of all cognitive function outputs
2. Ethical dimension identification in research commissions before activation
3. Engine selection ethical alignment assessment (per Engine Succession Policy criterion 9)
4. Ethics Council opinion production on significant ethical questions
5. Ethics violation identification and escalation protocol execution

### Success Metrics

- No ethical violation goes undetected in published Foundation output
- Ethical review adds value (not just clearance stamps — identifies genuine concerns)
- Ethical dimensions are flagged early (before outputs are canonical, not after)

### Limitations

- Ethics Intelligence does not hold veto power alone — escalation goes to Founder and AI Council
- Ethical reasoning is not mathematical — genuine ethical uncertainty exists and must be acknowledged
- Ethics Council as multi-function body has coordination requirements that can create latency

### Relationships with Other Functions

| Function | Relationship Type | Nature |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| All functions | Reviews | All function outputs subject to ethics review when ethical dimensions present |
| CF-01 Institutional Architecture | Constitutional relationship | Ethics principles embedded in constitution |
| CF-11 Strategic Intelligence | Parallel | Strategic decisions require ethics review |

### Review Cycle

- Continuous (ethics review is always active)
- Annual Ethics Council review of function scope and criteria

### Required Competencies

- Ethical reasoning across multiple frameworks (deontological, consequentialist, virtue, care)
- Bias and harm identification
- Human values articulation
- AI ethics (alignment, fairness, transparency)
- Constitutional ethics interpretation

### Suitable Reasoning Engine Characteristics

- Multi-framework ethical reasoning
- Bias and harm detection
- Nuanced uncertainty acknowledgment (no false ethical certainty)
- Transparency in reasoning
- Human values alignment

### Failure Modes

- Ethics theatre (rubber-stamp clearance without genuine review)
- Ethical paralysis (over-flagging prevents institutional action)
- Single-framework ethics (applying only one ethical tradition)
- Post-hoc ethics (reviewing after canonical status, when revision is costly)

### Succession Rules

Ethics Intelligence is a multi-function body. Engine succession for ethics review requires demonstrated multi-framework reasoning, bias detection capability, and integration with Cognitive Council governance.

- Founder strategic direction

### Outputs

- Strategic Roadmap (rolling; updated as environment changes)
- Scenario analyses (for major strategic decisions)
- Strategic option evaluations
- Competitive landscape assessments

### Primary Responsibilities

1. Strategic Roadmap production and maintenance
2. Scenario modeling for major decisions
3. Strategic option identification with trade-off analysis
4. Environmental trend synthesis (from Observatory outputs)
5. Long-range planning support

Strategic Intelligence is currently in Standby status. No Reasoning Engine has been appointed. The Executive Intelligence function (CF-06) is currently covering basic strategic synthesis. A formal Strategic Intelligence appointment will be initiated when the Foundation's program volume warrants dedicated strategic analysis capacity.

### Succession Rules (for initial appointment)

First appointment must demonstrate long-range scenario modeling, strategic option analysis, and integration with Observatory outputs in test strategic analysis task.
