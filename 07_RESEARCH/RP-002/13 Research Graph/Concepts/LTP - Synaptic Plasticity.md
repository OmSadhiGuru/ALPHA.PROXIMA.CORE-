# Concept Note — LTP and Synaptic Plasticity

---

## Canonical Definition

**Long-Term Potentiation (LTP)** is the activity-dependent strengthening of synaptic connections that forms the primary cellular substrate of memory formation [C-002]. First described by Bliss and Lømo (1973) in the rabbit perforant path. LTP is the biological implementation of Hebb's postulate (1949): neurons that fire together wire together.

**Synaptic plasticity** is the broader category: the capacity of synapses to change their efficacy in response to activity. LTP (strengthening) and LTD (long-term depression, weakening) are the bidirectional forms.

---

## Molecular Cascade

```
1. Coincident pre- and postsynaptic activity
        ↓
2. Glutamate release → NMDA receptor activation
   (NMDA requires: glutamate + postsynaptic depolarization — "coincidence detector")
        ↓
3. Ca²⁺ influx through NMDA receptor
        ↓
4. CaMKII activation (calcium/calmodulin-dependent kinase II)
        ↓
5. AMPA receptor phosphorylation → insertion into postsynaptic membrane
   (immediate early LTP — E-LTP)
        ↓
6. mTOR pathway → local protein synthesis at active synapses
        ↓
7. Dendritic spine remodeling (actin cytoskeleton restructuring)
   (late LTP — L-LTP; requires new protein synthesis)
        ↓
8. PKMζ constitutively active → maintains synaptic potentiation
   (the "memory maintenance" protein; ZIP injection erases established LTM)
```

## Four Plasticity Types (DOC-A)

| Type | Mechanism | Timescale | Function |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Hebbian LTP/LTD | NMDA, AMPA, CaMKII, PKMζ | Minutes-hours | Synapse-specific encoding |
| Synaptic Homeostasis | AMPA receptor scaling (all synapses) | Hours-days | Maintains dynamic range; prevents saturation |
| Structural Plasticity | Actin remodeling; spine morphogenesis | Days-weeks | Physical rewiring |
| Adult Neurogenesis | New DG granule cells (SGZ) | Weeks-months | Pattern separation capacity |

## The Protein Turnover Paradox [M-003, S-003]

Individual synaptic proteins (AMPA receptors, PSD-95, PKMζ) turn over on timescales of days to weeks. Memory persists for decades. If synaptic weights are the sole storage medium, this is paradoxical.

Proposed resolutions:
- Continuous re-synthesis of proteins at potentiated synapses (PKMζ loop)
- Epigenetic mechanisms (DNA methylation, histone modifications) as supplementary storage
- Perineuronal nets (PNN) as structural scaffolding preserving synaptic configuration

This paradox is unresolved [M-003].
