# RP-001 — Research Question

---

## Primary Research Question

**What is the current scientific and philosophical understanding of human consciousness — and what does that understanding imply for the design of artificial cognitive systems?**

---

## Decomposition

### 1. Empirical Sub-Question
What neural mechanisms, anatomical structures, and dynamic processes are reliably associated with conscious experience in biological systems?

### 2. Theoretical Sub-Question
Which theoretical frameworks best explain the available empirical evidence — and where do they agree, conflict, or operate at different explanatory levels?

### 3. Translational Sub-Question
Which insights from consciousness science are tractable for implementation in artificial cognitive architectures — and what conditions would be necessary and sufficient for machine consciousness?

### 4. Institutional Sub-Question
What is Alpha Proxima's research posture on consciousness — and how should the Foundation position itself relative to ongoing adversarial collaborations (COGITATE, INTREPID)?

---

## Scope Boundaries

**In scope:**
- Neuroscientific theories of consciousness (GNWT, IIT, PP, RPT, HOT, AST, Active Inference)
- Empirical evidence base for each theory
- Philosophical frameworks relevant to the hard problem
- Implications for AI system design

**Out of scope (for Phase 1):**
- Contemplative and phenomenological traditions (Advaita, Buddhist phenomenology, Yogic frameworks) — reserved for Phase 2 or a separate sub-program
- Clinical applications beyond consciousness level assessment
- Social or collective consciousness
