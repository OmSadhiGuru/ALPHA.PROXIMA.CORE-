# RP-001 — Governing Provisions and Operational Laws

---

## Purpose

This document records the specific institutional laws, protocols, and operational provisions that directly govern RP-001's conduct. It is the compliance interface between this research program and the Alpha Proxima legal and operational framework.

---

## Primary Governing Provisions

### Research Program Naming and Numbering
RP-001 is the first Alpha Proxima Research Program. All programs are named and numbered sequentially: RP-001 through RP-006 (current generation). New programs require Research Council authorization.

### Source Quality Requirements
All external sources must receive a source quality assessment before claims from those sources enter the Canonical Synthesis. Quality criteria: independence, methodology documentation, replication, peer review, recency, potential bias.

### Evidence Classification Mandate
Every claim in institutional documents must carry an evidence class (C/M/Q/E/S/P). No claim may enter the Canonical Synthesis without its evidence class and source citation. This is non-negotiable per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. II.

### ARCHIVE Immutability Mandate
Original research artifacts entering the ARCHIVE are immutable. No editing, compression, or replacement is permitted. If an artifact is found to be erroneous, an Amendment Note is appended to the registration note — the original artifact is not modified. Per [[ARCHIVE/ARCHIVE Philosophy]].

### Competing Theory Prohibition
Alpha Proxima does not force artificial consensus between competing theories. Where genuine competition exists, it is recorded as such with both positions documented. Per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. I, Sec. 1.4.

### Revision Trigger Protocol
When adversarial collaboration results (COGITATE, INTREPID) are published, the Canonical Synthesis version number is incremented and the Evidence Registry is updated. Prior versions are preserved in the Version History. Per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. IV.
