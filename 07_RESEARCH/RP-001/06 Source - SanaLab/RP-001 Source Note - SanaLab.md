## Role in RP-001

SanaLab is the primary substantive contributor to RP-001 Phase 1. Its two artifacts provided:

**DOC-003 (21 pages):** The broadest theoretical coverage — seven theories of consciousness analyzed across nine dimensions each. Established the comparative framework that underpins the Theory Matrix. Introduced the evidence classification system that was mapped to Alpha Proxima's own classification.

**DOC-004 (3 pages):** Focused architectural comparison between GNWT and IIT through the lens of the Triple Network Model. Produced the most institutionally significant insight of Phase 1: the complementary-layers thesis — that IIT and GNWT operate at different explanatory levels (structural substrate vs. functional routing) rather than competing for the same explanatory ground.

---

## The Complementary-Layers Thesis (Critical Institutional Insight)

> *"GNWT accurately models the access and maintenance of conscious content during high-level cognitive operations (the processing timeline). Conversely, IIT accurately models the presence and intensity of experience itself within structural neural fields (the background state). Our systems model must treat them not as mutually exclusive truths, but as complementary architectural layers."* — DOC-004, p.3

This insight is foundational to [[09 Canonical Synthesis/RP-001 Canonical Synthesis]], Section 3.
