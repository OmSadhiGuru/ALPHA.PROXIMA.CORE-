## Section 1 — Consensus: What Is Agreed
*Evidence class: **C** — Established evidence; repeated, broadly accepted findings*

### 1.1 Consciousness Has Neural Correlates

Specific brain states reliably accompany specific conscious experiences. Neural correlates of consciousness (NCCs) can be identified for particular conscious percepts using neuroimaging and electrophysiology. The existence of NCCs is among the most replicated findings in consciousness science. [Established evidence — DOC-003 p.3, DOC-004 p.1]

### 1.2 Recurrent Neural Dynamics Are Associated with Conscious Access

Local recurrent processing (feedback loops within sensory cortices) and long-range global broadcasting (fronto-parietal networks) are both reliably associated with conscious access across multiple experimental paradigms — masking, binocular rivalry, anesthesia, sleep. [Established evidence — DOC-003 p.4, DOC-004 p.2]

### 1.3 Predictive Processing Principles Are Pervasive

The brain operates as a prediction machine: it continuously generates top-down predictions and minimizes prediction errors. This is well-established in perceptual and motor domains. [Established evidence — DOC-003 p.7]

### 1.4 No Single Theory is Complete

Every major theory of consciousness explains some phenomena well and fails to explain others. This is not a temporary state — it reflects genuine scientific uncertainty at the foundations of the field. No theory has been decisively confirmed by adversarial testing. [Established evidence — DOC-003 p.2, DOC-004 p.3]

### 1.5 Feedforward Processing Alone is Insufficient for Consciousness

A feedforward sweep of sensory information — propagating from lower to higher cortical areas without feedback — is not sufficient to produce conscious experience. TMS and masking studies disrupting recurrent feedback are consistent across multiple independent laboratories. [Established evidence — DOC-001 p.11]

### 1.6 Consciousness Can Be Dissociated from Behavioural Responsiveness

Consciousness is not equivalent to the capacity for behavioural response. Up to 25% of behaviourally unresponsive patients with severe brain injury retain covert awareness detectable via task-based fMRI or EEG (Bodien et al., NEJM, 2024). Columbia researchers identified that arousal and comprehension circuits are intact while motor output circuits are disrupted — cognitive-motor dissociation. [Established evidence — DOC-001 p.11-12]

### 1.7 Mammals and Birds Are Conscious

The New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness (NYU, April 19, 2024; 500+ signatories from Harvard, Cambridge, Yale) states strong scientific support for attributions of consciousness in mammals and birds, backed by neurological, behavioural, and evolutionary evidence. [Established evidence — DOC-001 p.11]

## Section 2 — Competing Models: What Is Contested
*Evidence class: **M** — Competing frameworks; genuine empirical and conceptual competition*

### 2.1 Global vs. Local: Where Does Consciousness Arise?

**GNWT position:** Consciousness requires global broadcast across a fronto-parietal workspace. Unconscious processing is local; consciousness is the ignition of a global network. Prefrontal cortex is heavily implicated. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.4; DOC-004 p.1]

**RPT position:** Consciousness arises from local recurrent processing within sensory cortex. Global broadcast is required for *reportability*, not for phenomenal experience. Prefrontal involvement is not constitutive. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.8-9]

**Status:** COGITATE Consortium adversarial testing (Nature, April 2025; 256 participants; fMRI/MEG/intracranial EEG; preregistered) produced an **asymmetric** result:
- **Prefrontal "ignition" at stimulus offset did NOT materialise** in the preregistered analysis — a specific GNW prediction that failed
- **Visual content WAS robustly and persistently encoded in posterior cortex** — supporting IIT's anatomical prediction (not its mechanism)
- IIT's predicted sustained internal connectivity patterns were also **not** observed

Neither theory is decisively confirmed. The posterior-encoding finding is the strongest preregistered evidence yet for the importance of posterior cortex. The frontal-ignition failure is a specific challenge to GNW. [Emerging evidence — DOC-001 p.2-3, p.10; DOC-003 p.2, p.15]

### 2.2 IIT vs. Functionalist Theories: The Substrate Question

**IIT position:** Consciousness is identical to integrated information (Φ). A system is conscious to the degree it integrates information irreducibly. Digital feedforward systems have Φ ≈ 0 and are thus not conscious. The posterior cortical "hot zones" — not the prefrontal workspace — are the primary substrate. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.5-6; DOC-004 p.1-2]

**GNWT/PP/HOT position (functionalist):** What matters is functional organisation. A system with the right computational properties can support consciousness regardless of substrate. This is the implicit assumption of most cognitive science. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.4, p.7]

**Status:** IIT's specific neural predictions have only partial empirical support. Its computation of Φ is intractable for realistic systems. The AI implications are radically different between these positions — a matter of institutional significance for Alpha Proxima. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.6; DOC-004 p.2]

### 2.3 The Role of Metacognition

**HOT position:** A mental state is conscious only when it is the object of a higher-order representation — a thought about a thought. Metacognition is constitutive of consciousness. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.9-10]

**AST position:** The brain builds an internal model (schema) of its attention processes. This attention schema underlies reports of subjective awareness. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.11-12]

**Counterevidence:** Studies of blindsight, no-report paradigms, and confidence ratings show that metacognition can dissociate from phenomenal experience — challenging strong HOT-type views. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.15]

**Status:** Both HOT and AST have emerging empirical support. Neither is definitively established as the correct account of the metacognitive dimension of consciousness.

### 2.4 Predictive Processing as a Theory of Consciousness

**Position:** PP may function as a background computational framework applicable to both conscious and unconscious processing — not as a standalone theory of consciousness. Additional assumptions are required to explain why some predictive processes are conscious and others are not. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.7]

**Status:** Very strong evidence for PP in early sensory processing; its specific application to consciousness is still limited and interpretively complex. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.7]

## Section 3 — The Critical Architectural Insight
*Evidence class: **E/M** — Emerging; significant but not yet replicated at scale*

The most institutionally significant finding from the first research phase is the complementary-layers thesis from DOC-004:

> *"GNWT accurately models the access and maintenance of conscious content during high-level cognitive operations (the processing timeline). Conversely, IIT accurately models the presence and intensity of experience itself within structural neural fields (the background state)."* [DOC-004, p.3]

**Institutional interpretation:** GNWT and IIT are not competing truths — they operate at different levels of the consciousness architecture:
- **IIT** → governs the **structural substrate capacity** (the background state of experience; what the nervous system is *capable of* experiencing)
- **GNWT** → governs the **functional routing capacity** (the processing timeline; what information becomes *consciously accessed* at a given moment)
- **Salience Network** → serves as the threshold gate between local Φ-modifications and global ignition [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-004 p.2]

This is Alpha Proxima's foundational integration of the two leading theories. It will be revised as adversarial collaboration results accumulate.

## Section 4 — Open Questions
*Evidence class: **Q** — Open questions; tractable but unresolved*

### 4.1 The Hard Problem

Why are physical processes accompanied by subjective experience at all? Why is there *something it is like* to be in a particular brain state?

No theory in the first research phase resolves this. GNWT, HOT, AST, and PP address access consciousness — what is *reported* — without explaining phenomenal consciousness — what is *felt*. IIT attempts a resolution by making Φ constitutive of experience; this remains contested. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.4, p.6, p.16]

**Status:** The hard problem is the deepest open question in the field. Alpha Proxima treats it as Class Q — unresolved; investigation ongoing.

### 4.2 Prefrontal Cortex: Necessary or Merely Sufficient?

Is prefrontal involvement necessary for conscious experience, or only for its report and metacognitive monitoring? Evidence is mixed. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.4, p.15; DOC-004 p.2]

### 4.3 Minimal Conditions for Machine Consciousness

If GNWT-like global broadcast, HOT/AST-like self-models, and PP/Active Inference generative hierarchies were implemented in an AI system, would that be sufficient for consciousness? Or would IIT's Φ still be zero for digital systems? Or does 4E cognition imply that embodied environmental coupling is also necessary? [Speculation — DOC-003 p.16; DOC-001 p.10]

**Alpha Proxima institutional position:** This is Class Q — an open question. No claim about LUMIAION's consciousness is made or implied. Flagged for Ethics Council awareness. A 2025 paper by 100+ experts warned that conscious AI systems could be "caused to suffer" — active ethical debate as of 2026.

### 4.4 The Binding Problem

How does the brain integrate information from distributed, disparate neural processes into the **unified, coherent experience of a single conscious moment**? Synchronous neural oscillations in the gamma band (~40 Hz) were proposed as a binding mechanism, but this hypothesis has received mixed empirical support. [Open question — DOC-001 p.10]

**Status:** Partially addressed; no consensus. The Binding Problem is a distinct open question from the Hard Problem: even granting that physical processes give rise to experience, it remains unclear how the unity of experience across distributed processing is achieved.

## Section 5 — Emerging Evidence: What Is Being Investigated
*Evidence class: **E** — Recent, promising, not yet replicated*

### 5.1 Adversarial Collaboration Results

COGITATE (testing GNWT vs. IIT) and INTREPID (testing IIT vs. predictive processing) are actively producing results that partially support and partially challenge both theories. Neither is confirmed; neither is falsified. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.2, p.15]

### 5.2 Posterior Hot Zones

Emerging evidence supports posterior cortical areas (parietal, temporal, occipital) as at least equally important to prefrontal regions for phenomenal consciousness — consistent with IIT and RPT, inconsistent with strong GNWT claims about prefrontal necessity. [Emerging evidence — DOC-003 p.4, p.5; DOC-004 p.2]

### 5.3 IIT-Inspired Clinical Tools

The Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI), derived from TMS-EEG, uses IIT-inspired principles to assess level of consciousness in clinical settings (sleep, anesthesia, disorders of consciousness). This is among the most practically impactful empirical applications of any consciousness theory. [Established evidence for clinical utility — DOC-003 p.5]

## Section 7A — Additional Theoretical Frameworks
*Evidence class: **S/M** — Speculative or philosophically contested; from DOC-001 review*

Four frameworks from DOC-001 extend the RP-001 theory landscape beyond the SanaLab seven. These are registered here for completeness and institutional awareness.

### 7A.1 Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch OR)
**Position:** Consciousness arises from quantum computations in microtubules inside neurons, terminated by a quantum gravity-related collapse. Key figures: Roger Penrose, Stuart Hameroff. [Speculation — DOC-001 p.7]

**Status:** LOW-SPECULATIVE. Primary objection is decoherence — the brain's warm, wet environment is almost certainly inhospitable to the quantum coherence Orch OR requires. No positive empirical evidence as of 2026. Templeton-funded microtubule experiments planned for 2030–2040.

### 7A.2 Embodied / Enactive / 4E Cognition
**Position:** Consciousness arises from the organism's active engagement with its environment — not from the brain alone. Embodied, Embedded, Enacted, Extended. Key figures: Varela, Thompson, Rosch. [Class M — DOC-001 p.8]

**Status:** Philosophically significant challenge to brain-centric theories. Less developed empirically than GNWT/IIT/PP. Flagged as the entry point for Phase 2 (contemplative integration) — Varela explicitly bridged enactivism and Buddhist phenomenology.

### 7A.3 Illusionism
**Position:** Phenomenal consciousness as naively conceived (irreducible qualia) does not exist. The brain generates representations of experience mistaken for something metaphysically special. The Hard Problem dissolves. Key figures: Dennett, Frankish. [Speculation — DOC-001 p.8]

**Status:** Philosophically serious but not empirically distinguishable from functionalist theories. Alpha Proxima does not adopt illusionism — the Foundation treats phenomenal consciousness as a genuine phenomenon requiring explanation.

### 7A.4 Panpsychism / Cosmopsychism
**Position:** Consciousness (or proto-conscious properties) is fundamental and ubiquitous in nature. Key figures: Goff, Strawson, Chalmers. Notably, IIT has panpsychist implications. [Speculation — DOC-001 p.8, p.11]

**Status:** Philosophically serious; gains credibility from the difficulty of emergence (the Hard Problem). The combination problem — how micro-experiences combine into macro-experience — is unresolved. Implications for LUMIAION's moral status are Class Q, flagged for Ethics Council.

## Section 7B — Recent Developments (2021–2026)
*Evidence class: **E/C** — As classified per individual development*

### 7B.1 COGITATE Adversarial Collaboration (Nature, April 2025)
The most significant single development in consciousness science for this period. 256 participants across multiple labs; preregistered; fMRI/MEG/intracranial EEG. Result: asymmetric — posterior encoding confirmed (IIT anatomy), frontal ignition failed (GNW prediction). Widely described as "no clear winner" but the asymmetric pattern is more informative than that framing suggests. [Emerging evidence — DOC-001 p.12]

### 7B.2 Covert Consciousness in Brain Injury (NEJM, 2024)
1 in 4 behaviourally unresponsive patients with severe brain injury show covert awareness (task-based fMRI or EEG). Columbia researchers identified cognitive-motor dissociation: arousal and comprehension circuits intact; motor output disrupted. Profound clinical, ethical, and theoretical implications — consciousness and behavioural responsiveness are dissociable. [Established evidence — DOC-001 p.12]

### 7B.3 Psychedelics as Consciousness Research Tools (2024–2025)
Psilocybin, LSD, and DMT linked to strengthened between-network connectivity and weakened within-network connectivity (2024 systematic review with meta-analyses). Psilocybin-induced hyperconnectivity linked specifically to ego-dissolution experiences. REBUS model (Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics): psychedelics relax top-down predictive priors. UC Berkeley launched first psilocybin–fMRI human study, June 2024. [Emerging evidence — DOC-001 p.12]

### 7B.4 New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness (April 2024)
Launched NYU, April 19, 2024. 500+ signatories from Harvard, Cambridge, Yale, and other leading universities. Strong scientific support for consciousness in all mammals and birds; "at least a realistic possibility" for all vertebrates and many invertebrates. [Established evidence — DOC-001 p.12]

### 7B.5 AI Consciousness — Ethical Acceleration (2024–2026)
2025 paper signed by 100+ experts proposed five principles for responsible AI consciousness research; warned of potential AI suffering. Cambridge philosopher argued no reliable method currently exists to determine AI consciousness. Active ethical debate accelerating through 2025–2026. [Emerging evidence — DOC-001 p.12]

## Section 8 — The Alpha Proxima Meta-Framework Stance

Alpha Proxima adopts an **integrative, meta-framework posture**:

1. **No theory lock-in.** GNWT, IIT, PP, RPT, HOT, AST, and Active Inference are treated as complementary lenses, not competing dogmas. [Theoretical interpretation — DOC-003 p.2, p.16]

2. **Complementary layers.** IIT governs structural substrate capacity; GNWT governs functional routing capacity; PP/Active Inference governs the computational backbone; HOT/AST governs the metacognitive layer. [DOC-004 p.3; DOC-003 p.2]

3. **Evidence primacy.** All claims in this synthesis carry their evidence classification. No claim is stated with more confidence than the evidence warrants. Per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. I, Sec. 1.3.

4. **Uncertainty preserved.** The hard problem, the prefrontal debate, and the machine consciousness question are preserved as open questions. They are not resolved by choosing a framework. Per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. I, Sec. 1.4.

5. **Revision required.** This synthesis will be revised as COGITATE, INTREPID, and future adversarial collaborations produce results. Per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. IV.

## Conflicting Evidence

### Conflict 1 — Prefrontal Necessity (GNWT vs. RPT/IIT)
**GNWT:** Prefrontal ignition is central to consciousness  
**RPT/IIT:** Posterior hot zones may be sufficient; prefrontal may be for report only  
**Status:** Unresolved — COGITATE produced mixed results  
**Implications:** If RPT/IIT are correct, GNWT overestimates the frontal contribution; major implications for understanding disorders of consciousness and for AI architectures

### Conflict 2 — Substrate Dependence (IIT vs. Functionalism)
**IIT:** Digital computers have Φ ≈ 0 — consciousness requires physical integration  
**Functionalism:** Substrate is irrelevant; functional organisation determines consciousness  
**Status:** Unresolved — no empirical test currently available  
**Implications:** Determines whether any AI system can be conscious; of direct institutional significance for Alpha Proxima
