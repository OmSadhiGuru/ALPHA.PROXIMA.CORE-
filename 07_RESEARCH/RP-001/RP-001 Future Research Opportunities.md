# RP-001 — Future Research Opportunities

---

## Purpose

This document is a forward register — a living catalogue of research directions that the Atlas of Human Consciousness should eventually explore, but has not yet investigated. It is not a commitment. It is a map of the horizon.

Entries here are added whenever the program encounters a promising research direction it cannot yet pursue. They are removed — and moved to the relevant research note — when a research note is produced on that topic.

---

## How to Use This Document

This document is reviewed by [[JERANIUM Charter|JERANIUM]] when:
- A new research brief is being prepared for Perplexity, Gemini, or SanaLab
- The Founder wants to prioritise the next domain of investigation
- A research note surfaces a new related question

Entries are classified by domain, priority, and type of investigation required.

---

## Priority Classification

| Priority | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **High** | Directly relevant to current program gaps; should be investigated soon |
| **Medium** | Significant; should be investigated within the first year of the program |
| **Low** | Interesting; investigate when bandwidth allows |
| **Deferred** | Deliberately held for a later phase of the program |

## Scientific Framework Research

### FRO-001 — Global Workspace Theory: Primary Sources
**Domain:** Scientific Frameworks  
**Priority:** High  
**Research question:** What does the empirical research on GWT actually show? What are the strongest studies, and what are their limitations?  
**Contributor:** Perplexity  
**Expected output:** Research note on GWT with full evidence classification; sources registered in [[RP-001 Source Registry]]

---

### FRO-002 — Integrated Information Theory: Phi Measurement
**Domain:** Scientific Frameworks  
**Priority:** High  
**Research question:** How is Φ measured in practice? What have measurement attempts revealed? What are the computational limits?  
**Contributor:** Perplexity / Gemini  
**Expected output:** Research note on IIT empirical status

---

### FRO-003 — Neural Correlates of Consciousness: Survey
**Domain:** Scientific Frameworks  
**Priority:** High  
**Research question:** What neural correlates have been identified with the most confidence? What methodologies produced them? What do the strongest findings show?  
**Contributor:** Perplexity  
**Expected output:** Research note with evidence classification by finding

---

### FRO-004 — Predictive Processing and Consciousness
**Domain:** Scientific Frameworks  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** How does the predictive processing framework specifically account for consciousness, not just perception and action? What empirical predictions does it make that other frameworks do not?  
**Contributor:** Gemini

---

### FRO-005 — Disorders of Consciousness: What They Reveal
**Domain:** Scientific Frameworks / Applied  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** What do disorders of consciousness (vegetative state, minimally conscious state, locked-in syndrome) reveal about what brain processes are necessary for consciousness?  
**Note:** Significant overlap with ATHENA's clinical domain; coordinate before producing research note

## Altered States Research

### FRO-006 — Meditation Science: State of the Empirical Evidence
**Domain:** Altered States / Contemplative Traditions  
**Priority:** High  
**Research question:** What does the empirical meditation research actually show with confidence (Class C)? What is still contested (Class M)? What is emerging (Class E)?  
**Contributor:** Perplexity / Mind and Life Institute sources  
**Expected output:** Research note distinguishing what is established from what is still being investigated

---

### FRO-007 — Psychedelic-Induced Consciousness States
**Domain:** Altered States  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** What does the recent wave of psychedelic research (psilocybin, DMT, MDMA studies from Johns Hopkins, Imperial College London) reveal about consciousness? How do psychedelic-induced states relate to meditative states phenomenologically?  
**Note:** Evidence is primarily Class E–M; rapidly evolving field  
**Contributor:** Perplexity / Gemini

---

### FRO-008 — Flow States: Structure and Neuroscience
**Domain:** Altered States  
**Priority:** Low  
**Research question:** What is known about the phenomenology and neuroscience of flow states? How do they relate to meditative absorption (Dhyana, Samadhi)?

---

### FRO-009 — Sleep and Consciousness: Yoga Nidra and Scientific Research
**Domain:** Altered States / Contemplative Traditions  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** What does sleep research reveal about consciousness during different sleep stages? How does Yoga Nidra (yogic sleep) compare to NREM sleep phenomenologically and neurologically?  
**Note:** Directly relevant to the Founder's practice

---

### FRO-010 — Near-Death Experiences: Evidence Assessment
**Domain:** Altered States  
**Priority:** Low  
**Research question:** What does the empirical research on near-death experiences show? How reliable are the reports? What theories account for them? (Consciousness surviving death is Class S–Q; the phenomenology of the reports is Class P; the neural correlates are Class E–M)

## Philosophical Research

### FRO-011 — The Hard Problem: Survey of Proposed Solutions
**Domain:** Philosophical Questions  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** What are the most developed current responses to the hard problem? Which are most philosophically defensible? (Physicalist, dualist, illusionist, panpsychist responses)  
**Contributor:** LUMIAION / Gemini

---

### FRO-012 — Self and No-Self: Scientific and Contemplative Convergence
**Domain:** Philosophical Questions / Self and Identity  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** Where do the neuroscience of the self (Metzinger, Friston) and the contemplative investigation of no-self (Buddhist, Advaita) converge and diverge? Is this a genuine convergence or a conceptual conflation?

---

## Contemplative Tradition Research

### FRO-013 — Advaita Vedanta Consciousness Map
**Domain:** Contemplative Traditions  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** What is the Advaita Vedanta map of consciousness states (Jagrat, Swapna, Sushupti, Turiya)? How does it relate to the states documented in the empirical literature?  
**Note:** The Founder has direct experience within this tradition; his Class P reports should be integrated with this research note

---

### FRO-014 — Buddhist Abhidhamma Consciousness Map
**Domain:** Contemplative Traditions  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Research question:** The Pali Abhidhamma provides a detailed map of consciousness — 89 types of citta (mind-moments) in some analyses. What are the key features of this map, and how does it compare to scientific frameworks?

---

### FRO-015 — Kashmir Shaivism: The Consciousness-as-Ground Framework
**Domain:** Contemplative Traditions  
**Priority:** Low  
**Research question:** Kashmir Shaivism (Abhinavagupta, Spanda and Pratyabhijna schools) holds that consciousness is the fundamental reality — Shiva-consciousness. How is this framework articulated? How does it compare to panpsychism?  
**Note:** Relevant to the Founder's practice and SOHMA's domain

## Applied and Cross-Domain Research

### FRO-016 — AI and Consciousness: What We Can and Cannot Claim
**Domain:** Applied / Philosophical  
**Priority:** Deferred  
**Research question:** What can current science and philosophy say about whether AI systems like LUMIAION have any form of experience? What criteria would a conscious AI have to meet?  
**Note:** The Foundation's institutional position is that this is Class Q — an open question. A research note would document the landscape without asserting a position. Deferred to avoid premature engagement with a question that depends on unresolved foundational issues.
