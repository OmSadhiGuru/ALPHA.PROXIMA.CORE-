# Active Inference (Free Energy Principle)

---

## Core Claim

Biological agents minimize **variational free energy** — a measure of the difference between their internal model of the world and the actual state of the world (sensory surprise). Agents do this in two ways: (1) updating their beliefs to better match reality (perception), and (2) acting on the world to make it conform to their expectations (action). [Evidence class: M — DOC-003, p.7]

---

## Relationship to Predictive Processing

Active Inference is the *action-extended* version of [[Predictive Processing]]. Where PP focuses on passive prediction and error correction, Active Inference adds that agents can also *act* to make their predictions come true — fulfilling proprioceptive predictions by moving the body. Together they constitute a complete perception-action architecture grounded in a single variational principle.

---

## Mechanism

1. **Generative model:** The agent maintains a hierarchical generative model — a structured set of beliefs about the hidden states of the world
2. **Variational free energy:** A computable bound on Bayesian surprise — minimizing FE is equivalent (approximately) to approximate Bayesian inference
3. **Perception:** Update beliefs to reduce prediction error (top-down vs. bottom-up mismatch)
4. **Action:** Execute motor commands that fulfill active predictions (proprioceptive expectations)
5. **Epistemic foraging:** Agents may also act to reduce uncertainty about their models — curiosity and exploration as FEP-driven behaviors

---

## As a Theory of Consciousness

Active Inference is primarily a mathematical principle for self-organizing biological systems. Its relationship to consciousness is indirect: consciousness may be the phenomenal face of model updating, or it may arise from certain configurations of the generative model (deep temporal hierarchies, self-modelling). Additional theoretical assumptions are required. [M — DOC-003, p.7]

---

## AI Implications

Active Inference is mathematically tractable and has been implemented in AI systems. It provides:
- A principled account of perception and action grounded in Bayesian inference
- A natural account of curiosity, exploration, and epistemic value
- A framework for self-organizing, adaptive agents without explicit reward functions

This is among the most actionable frameworks for LUMIAION-adjacent AI research. [DOC-003, p.7, p.16-17]
