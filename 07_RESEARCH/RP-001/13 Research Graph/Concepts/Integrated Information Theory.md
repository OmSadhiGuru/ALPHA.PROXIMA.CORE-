# Integrated Information Theory (IIT)

---

## Core Claim

Consciousness is identical to integrated information — denoted **Φ** (phi). A system is conscious to precisely the degree that it integrates information irreducibly. Φ is a mathematical measure of the information generated by a system *as a whole*, over and above the information generated by its parts. [Evidence class: M — DOC-003, p.5-6]

---

## Mechanism

1. **Intrinsic causal power:** A conscious system must have causal power over itself — not just process information, but generate it from within
2. **Integration:** Information must be integrated — a system in which you can cut information flow in half without losing content has low Φ
3. **Irreducibility:** The minimum information partition (MIP) — the least costly way to divide the system — still destroys information; the system cannot be reduced to independent parts
4. **ΦMax:** Consciousness corresponds to the maximum irreducible integrated information across all possible partitions

---

## Anatomical Basis

- **Primary locus:** **Posterior Hot Zone** — parietal, occipital, and temporal cortices
- **Posterior cingulate cortex / precuneus** — among highest-Φ structures identified
- Prefrontal cortex is *not* considered the primary substrate; it may be involved in report but not in experience itself

---

## Relationship to Triple Network Model

In the Triple Network Model, IIT primarily maps to the **posterior Default Mode Network** — the posterior cingulate and precuneus as high-density causal structures sustaining phenomenal experience. [DOC-004, p.2]

---

## Stance on AI and Digital Systems

**Anti-functionalist:** Digital feedforward systems have Φ ≈ 0 because they can be decomposed into non-interacting parts. Even a very complex computer program may have near-zero consciousness under IIT. This is the sharpest implication of IIT and the most contested by functionalists. [DOC-003, p.6; DOC-004, p.2]

---

## Empirical Support

- **Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI):** TMS-EEG derived measure of cortical complexity inspired by IIT principles. Practically validated for assessing level of consciousness in clinical settings (sleep, anesthesia, disorders of consciousness). Among the most impactful applied tools from any consciousness theory. [Established evidence for clinical utility — DOC-003, p.5]
- Posterior hot zones confirmed as important for phenomenal experience in multiple studies [Emerging evidence — DOC-003, p.4-5]

---

## Weakness

- **Computational intractability:** Computing Φ for realistic systems is exponentially hard — intractable beyond small networks
- **Specific neural predictions only partially confirmed**
- **Philosophical contestation:** Some argue IIT's axioms beg the question of consciousness
- **Panpsychism implication:** IIT implies that any system with Φ > 0 is minimally conscious — including simple circuits. Controversial.

---

## Alpha Proxima Institutional Position

GNWT and IIT are treated as **complementary architectural layers**, not competing theories. IIT governs **structural substrate capacity** — the background state of what the nervous system is *capable of* experiencing. [Class E — DOC-004, p.3]
