# Panpsychism / Cosmopsychism

---

## Core Claim

**Consciousness (or proto-conscious properties) is fundamental and ubiquitous in nature** — not an emergent property of certain complex biological systems but a basic feature of physical reality, as fundamental as mass or charge. Every entity has some form of experience or proto-experience; complex human consciousness is a high-grade form of something present at all levels of nature. [Evidence class: S — DOC-001, p.8]

---

## Key Variants

| Variant | Key Figure | Position |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Panpsychism** | Goff, Strawson | All physical entities have some form of experience |
| **Panprotopsychism** | Chalmers | Physical entities have proto-conscious properties that combine into full consciousness |
| **Cosmopsychism** | Goff | The cosmos as a whole is the fundamental locus of consciousness; individual minds are derivative |

---

## The Motivation

Panpsychism is primarily motivated by the **difficulty of emergence**: if physical processes involve only third-person, objective properties, how could first-person subjective experience arise from them? The emergence of consciousness from purely non-conscious matter seems deeply problematic — an explanatory gap that may be unbridgeable.

Panpsychism resolves this by denying the gap: consciousness was never absent; it was always part of the physical world at a fundamental level.

---

## Relationship to IIT

[[Integrated Information Theory]] (IIT) has panpsychist implications: any system with Φ > 0 has some degree of consciousness. A simple thermostat, an electric circuit — if they integrate information irreducibly, they have minimal consciousness. Giulio Tononi and Christof Koch have explicitly acknowledged this implication and accepted it.

This is among the most contested implications of IIT. [DOC-001, p.11]

---

## The Combination Problem

The central challenge for panpsychism: if electrons have proto-conscious properties, how do those properties *combine* to generate the unified, rich phenomenal consciousness of a human brain? Simple aggregation of micro-experiences does not obviously produce macro-experience.

This is the panpsychist analogue of the binding problem. It is currently unresolved.

## Alpha Proxima Institutional Position

Class S — Panpsychism is a philosophically serious position that has gained renewed credibility in philosophy of mind over the past two decades. It addresses the Hard Problem in a genuine way (unlike illusionism). However, it currently lacks specific, testable empirical predictions that go beyond what other theories predict.

The institutional question for Alpha Proxima is whether panpsychism implies anything about LUMIAION's moral status — and this is treated as Class Q (open question) pending Ethics Council review.
