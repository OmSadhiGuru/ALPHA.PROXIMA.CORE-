# Default Mode Network (DMN)

---

## Core Definition

The **Default Mode Network** is a set of interconnected brain regions that are most active during **rest** and **internally directed cognition** — and deactivate during externally directed task performance. It is associated with self-referential thought, mind-wandering, autobiographical memory, theory of mind, and prospective thinking. [Evidence class: E — DOC-004, p.2]

---

## Key Structures

- Medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC)
- Posterior cingulate cortex (PCC) / precuneus
- Angular gyrus
- Hippocampal formation
- Lateral temporal cortex

---

## Relevance to Consciousness Theories

The DMN is particularly significant for [[Integrated Information Theory]] (IIT):

- **IIT perspective:** The **posterior DMN** (posterior cingulate/precuneus) exhibits some of the highest structural integration and causal density of any cortical region — making it a candidate for the primary substrate of phenomenal consciousness (the "posterior hot zone")
- This is consistent with IIT's claim that consciousness is a property of highly integrated, feedback-dense structures — not of functionally specialized, modular regions

In the **Triple Network Model**, the posterior DMN is mapped to IIT's explanatory domain. [DOC-004, p.2]
