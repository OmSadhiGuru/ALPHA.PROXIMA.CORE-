# Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT)

---

## Core Claim

Consciousness arises from **local recurrent processing** within sensory cortex — feedback loops that create a rich, self-sustaining representation within a sensory area. Global broadcast (GNWT) is required for *reportability*, not for *phenomenal experience*. Prefrontal involvement is not constitutive of consciousness. [Evidence class: M — DOC-003, p.8-9]

---

## Mechanism

1. **Feedforward sweep:** Initial sensory processing propagates rapidly from lower to higher cortical areas (unconscious)
2. **Local recurrence:** Within a sensory cortical area (e.g., V1-V4 for vision), feedback connections create recurrent loops
3. **Rich representation:** This recurrence creates a detailed, stable representation that constitutes phenomenal experience
4. **No frontal requirement:** Experience occurs at this stage — frontal involvement adds only metacognitive access and reportability

---

## Key Distinction from GNWT

| Dimension | RPT | GNWT |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Locus of consciousness | Sensory cortex (posterior) | Fronto-parietal workspace |
| Prefrontal role | Report only; not constitutive | Central; ignition necessary |
| What is explained | Phenomenal experience | Access consciousness / report |

---

## Empirical Support

- No-report paradigms: when subjects cannot report, conscious percepts still appear to occur — suggesting prefrontal is not necessary
- Blindsight: subjects with V1 lesions cannot report visual information but show residual behavioral sensitivity — but may lack recurrent loops
- COGITATE results partially challenged GNWT's prefrontal-necessity claim, consistent with RPT [Emerging evidence — DOC-003, p.2, p.15]

---

## Weakness

- Difficult to test definitively whether recurrence *is* sufficient for experience or merely correlates with it
- The dissociation between experience and report is hard to measure without report

---

## Alpha Proxima Institutional Position

RPT is treated as a competing perspective on the GNWT claim about prefrontal necessity. The prefrontal debate (GNWT vs. RPT/IIT) is an **unresolved conflict** — Conflict 1 in the Canonical Synthesis. [Class M]
