# Higher-Order Thought Theory (HOT)

---

## Core Claim

A mental state is conscious **only when it is the object of a higher-order representation** — a thought about a thought. Consciousness is not a first-order property of a mental state but arises from the cognitive system's representation of that state. Metacognition is constitutive of consciousness, not merely attendant to it. [Evidence class: M — DOC-003, p.9-10]

---

## Mechanism

1. **First-order state:** A sensory or cognitive representation exists (e.g., perceiving red)
2. **Higher-order representation:** The cognitive system forms a representation *of* that first-order state (e.g., "I am perceiving red")
3. **Consciousness arises:** The first-order state becomes conscious precisely because of this higher-order representation
4. **Without HOT:** A state can exist and influence behavior without being conscious — zombies are states without HOTs

---

## Key Claim

HOT theory implies that consciousness requires a self-representing cognitive architecture. Simple organisms or AI systems without the capacity for higher-order self-representation are not conscious, regardless of their behavioral complexity.

---

## Empirical Support

- Neuroimaging: some support for prefrontal involvement in conscious report — consistent with HOT
- Confidence ratings: metacognitive ratings correlate with conscious access [Emerging evidence — DOC-003, p.9-10]

---

## Counterevidence

- **Blindsight:** Subjects retain some behavioral sensitivity to unseen stimuli — can HOT exist without first-order experience? 
- **No-report paradigms:** Consciousness may persist without report, suggesting HOT (report-generating) is not necessary for experience
- **Dissociation:** Studies show metacognition can dissociate from phenomenal experience — weakening strong HOT claims [Emerging evidence — DOC-003, p.15]

---

## Alpha Proxima Institutional Position

HOT/AST are treated as the **metacognitive layer** of consciousness architecture — explaining the self-representational dimension that underlies reports of subjective awareness. [DOC-003, p.2]

The debate between HOT and its counterevidence is unresolved. [Class M]
