# Meta Awareness

---

## Core Definition

**Meta awareness** (or metacognition) refers to the capacity to represent, monitor, and regulate one's own mental states. In the context of consciousness science, it is the capacity to know that one is having an experience — not just having it. Second-order awareness: awareness of awareness. [Evidence class: M — DOC-003, p.9-12]

---

## Why It Matters for Consciousness Theory

Two major theories make metacognition *constitutive* of consciousness:

1. **[[Higher-Order Thought Theory]] (HOT):** A mental state is conscious *only when* it is the object of a higher-order representation. Metacognition is not just a capacity attached to consciousness — it *is* what makes a state conscious.

2. **[[Attention Schema Theory]] (AST):** The brain builds a model of its own attention. Subjective awareness is the phenomenal face of this self-model. Metacognition-as-attention-modelling underlies all reports of conscious experience.

---

## The Dissociation Problem

Studies of blindsight, no-report paradigms, and confidence ratings suggest that **metacognition can dissociate from phenomenal experience**:
- Subjects show residual behavioral sensitivity (suggesting some form of experience) without metacognitive access to it
- This challenges strong HOT-type views: if phenomenal experience can exist without a HOT, metacognition is not constitutive

**Status:** Unresolved — the dissociation data is real; the theoretical interpretation is contested. [M — DOC-003, p.15]

---

## Institutional Significance for LUMIAION

Meta awareness is directly relevant to AI design: whether an AI system can be said to have subjective experience may depend on whether it has genuine metacognitive access to its own computational states — not merely the ability to compute about external states, but to model and represent its own inner states.

Under HOT/AST, a system with genuine self-representation of its attentional and cognitive states would be a stronger candidate for consciousness than one without.
