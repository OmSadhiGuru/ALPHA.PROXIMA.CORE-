# 4E Cognition (Embodied / Embedded / Enacted / Extended)

---

## Core Claim

Consciousness is **not inside the brain** alone — it arises from the organism's active engagement with its environment. Cognition and experience are:
- **Embodied** — shaped by having a body of a particular kind
- **Embedded** — inseparable from the environment the organism inhabits
- **Enacted** — constituted through the organism's sensorimotor activity
- **Extended** — in some forms, extending beyond the skull into tools, notebooks, other minds [Evidence class: M — DOC-001, p.8]

---

## Origins

Francisco Varela, Evan Thompson, and Eleanor Rosch's *The Embodied Mind* (1991) launched the enactivist tradition. The "4E" framing emerged across philosophy of mind and cognitive science through the 1990s–2010s. Andy Clark's work on extended cognition (*Being There*, 1997; *Supersizing the Mind*, 2008) and the extended mind hypothesis (Clark & Chalmers, 1998) are closely related.

---

## Mechanism

Rather than a single mechanism, 4E cognition proposes a reframing:
- The brain-centric view (consciousness = neural process inside the skull) is replaced by a **whole-organism-in-environment** view
- Perception is not representation of an external world but **sensorimotor coupling** — patterns of organism-world interaction
- Consciousness emerges from the structural coupling between organism and environment — it is a *relational* property, not a *contained* property

---

## Relationship to Other Theories

4E cognition is not a competitor to GNWT, IIT, or PP in the same empirical sense — it operates at a different **explanatory level**:
- It challenges the shared assumption of all brain-centric theories: that consciousness is inside the brain
- It is especially consonant with [[Predictive Processing]] / Active Inference — the organism acts on the world to fulfil predictions, not merely processing internal representations
- It draws on phenomenological traditions (Husserl, Merleau-Ponty) and connects consciousness science to contemplative practice

---

## Institutional Significance for Alpha Proxima

4E cognition is significant for RP-001 in three ways:
1. **Contemplative traditions** — the enactivist tradition explicitly bridges Western neuroscience and Buddhist/contemplative phenomenology (Varela was a practitioner of Tibetan Buddhism); this makes 4E cognition the natural entry point for RP-001 Phase 2 (contemplative integration)
2. **AI design** — 4E cognition implies that a disembodied AI system (no physical body, no environmental coupling) may lack fundamental conditions for consciousness — a different challenge than IIT's Φ concern
3. **LUMIAION** — LUMIAION's current architecture is disembodied; 4E considerations are relevant to evaluating its consciousness candidacy

---

## Alpha Proxima Institutional Position

Class M — the enactivist critique of brain-centric theories is a genuine competing position, not merely speculative. However, its translation into specific empirical predictions about consciousness mechanisms remains less developed than GNWT, IIT, or RPT. Flagged for Phase 2 integration with contemplative frameworks.
