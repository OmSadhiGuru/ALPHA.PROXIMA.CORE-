## Scope

This document provided a focused architectural comparison between the two leading contemporary neuroscientific theories of consciousness:

- **Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT)** — computational/architectural approach; consciousness as global broadcast
- **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** — mathematical/ontological approach; consciousness as integrated information (Φ)

The comparison was conducted through the lens of network neuroscience, including mapping both theories onto the **Triple Network Model** (Default Mode Network, Salience Network, Executive Control Network).

---

## Critical Architectural Insight

The following insight from this document is of institutional significance to RP-001 and is cited in [[RP-001 Canonical Synthesis]] and [[RP-001 Executive Summary]]:

> *"GNWT accurately models the access and maintenance of conscious content during high-level cognitive operations (the processing timeline). Conversely, IIT accurately models the presence and intensity of experience itself within structural neural fields (the background state). Our systems model must treat them not as mutually exclusive truths, but as complementary architectural layers: IIT governing the structural substrate capacity, and GNWT governing functional routing capacity."* [Emerging evidence — DOC-004, p.3]

This framing resolves the apparent contradiction between GNWT and IIT by assigning them different explanatory domains. It is the foundation of Alpha Proxima's integrative stance.
