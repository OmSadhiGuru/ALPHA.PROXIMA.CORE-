## Key Contributions to Institutional Record

### 1. Historical Periodisation

The most complete historical account in the RP-001 source set:
- Descartes' mind-body dualism (17th c.) → Nagel's "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" (1974)
- Bernard Baars: GWT (1988); Crick & Koch: NCC programme (1990); Tononi: IIT (late 1990s)
- Chalmers 1995: Hard Problem / easy problems distinction
- ARC-COGITATE: $20M adversarial collaboration (Templeton World Charity Foundation, 2018); results published *Nature*, April 2025 (256 participants, fMRI/MEG/intracranial EEG); neither theory's core predictions fully supported

### 2. Additional Theoretical Frameworks (Not in DOC-003/DOC-004)

Four frameworks absent from the SanaLab sources:

| Framework | Key Figure(s) | Core Claim | Evidence Class |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|----------------|
| Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch OR) | Penrose & Hameroff | Consciousness arises from quantum computations in microtubules | S |
| 4E / Enactive Cognition | Varela, Thompson, Rosch | Consciousness arises from organism-environment engagement, not only from brain | M/S |
| Illusionism | Dennett, Frankish | Phenomenal consciousness as naively conceived does not exist; the Hard Problem dissolves | S |
| Panpsychism / Cosmopsychism | Goff, Strawson, Chalmers | Consciousness (or proto-conscious properties) is fundamental and ubiquitous | S |

### 3. Refined COGITATE Results (Nature, 2025)

DOC-001 provides the most precise account of COGITATE findings across all three sources:
- Prefrontal "ignition" at stimulus offset **did NOT materialise** in preregistered analysis → specific challenge to GNW
- Visual content **was** robustly and persistently encoded in **posterior** areas → supports IIT's anatomical prediction
- IIT's **mechanism** (sustained internal connectivity) was **not** observed → IIT mechanism challenged even where its anatomy was confirmed

This asymmetric result — posterior encoding confirmed, frontal ignition not — is more precise than the "mixed results" framing in DOC-003 and DOC-004.

### 4. New Consensus Claims (Part V)

Three established-evidence claims not previously in the Canonical Synthesis:
- **Feedforward processing alone is insufficient for consciousness** — TMS and masking studies consistent across labs [Established evidence — DOC-001, p.11]
- **Consciousness can be dissociated from behavioural responsiveness** — up to 25% of behaviourally unresponsive patients retain covert awareness (Bodien et al., NEJM, 2024) [Established evidence — DOC-001, p.11-12]
- **Mammals and birds are conscious** — New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness, April 2024, 500+ signatories [Established evidence — DOC-001, p.11]

### 5. The Binding Problem (New Open Question)

Not present in DOC-003/004: how does the brain integrate information from distributed, disparate neural processes into the unified, coherent experience of a single moment? Gamma oscillations (~40 Hz) proposed as binding mechanism — mixed empirical support. [Class Q — DOC-001, p.10]

### 6. Recent Developments (Part VII)

- **Covert Consciousness, NEJM 2024** — 1 in 4 unresponsive brain-injured patients show covert awareness; Columbia researchers identified cognitive-motor dissociation mechanism
- **Psychedelics as research tools (2024–2025)** — psilocybin/LSD/DMT linked to strengthened between-network connectivity and weakened within-network connectivity; psilocybin hyperconnectivity linked to ego dissolution (REBUS model); UC Berkeley psilocybin fMRI study launched June 2024
- **New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness (April 2024)** — launched NYU April 19, 2024; 500+ signatories from Harvard, Cambridge, Yale; attributes consciousness to all vertebrates and many invertebrates
- **AI Consciousness — Ethical Acceleration (2025)** — 100+ expert paper proposing five principles for responsible AI consciousness research; warning of potential AI suffering; Cambridge philosopher argued no reliable method exists to determine AI consciousness
