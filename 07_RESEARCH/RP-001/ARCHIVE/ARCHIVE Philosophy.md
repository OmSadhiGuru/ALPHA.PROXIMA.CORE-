# ARCHIVE — Philosophy and Governance

---

## Purpose

This document defines the philosophy, rules, and operating procedures for the RP-001 ARCHIVE. It governs what enters the ARCHIVE, how it is stored, and why it must never be modified.

---

## The Core Principle

**The ARCHIVE is immutable historical memory.**

Original research artifacts enter the ARCHIVE exactly as they were produced. Nothing inside the ARCHIVE is ever edited, compressed, updated, or replaced. The ARCHIVE does not contain institutional synthesis — it contains the raw material from which synthesis is built.

This is not bureaucracy. It is epistemology.

When a research program builds on sources, those sources must be traceable. If a canonical synthesis is later revised — because better evidence emerges, or because the original was found to be flawed — the Foundation must be able to return to the original artifacts and re-evaluate. An ARCHIVE that has been modified destroys this capacity. It makes the research record unreliable.

The ARCHIVE is the proof that the Foundation knows where its knowledge came from.

## What Belongs in the ARCHIVE

The following materials are archived permanently upon delivery:

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Original PDFs** | Research documents exactly as delivered by external contributors | Architecture of Human Consciousness (Perplexity, 2026-07-01) |
| **Original prompts** | The exact prompts used to commission research from each contributor | The brief sent to Perplexity for DOC-001 |
| **Original AI outputs** | Raw text outputs before any processing or integration | SanaLab Comparative Framework raw text |
| **Original notes** | Handwritten or informal notes from the Founder or researchers during intake | Founder notes on first review of DOC-003 |
| **Original metadata** | Source information as received (dates, contributor names, version numbers) | Document headers exactly as produced |

---

## What Does NOT Belong in the ARCHIVE

- Institutional synthesis documents (those belong in `09 Canonical Synthesis/`)
- Revised or updated versions of original documents (revisions are a new document; the original is untouched)
- Notes that were produced during the integration process (those belong in the relevant working sections)
- Placeholder or empty files

---

## ARCHIVE Rules

**Rule 1 — Never Modify**
No file inside ARCHIVE may be edited after initial deposit. Not for corrections. Not for updates. Not for cleanup. If a correction is needed, a note is added to the ARCHIVE Registry (below) documenting the error — but the original file is unchanged.

**Rule 2 — Timestamp and Register**
Every file that enters the ARCHIVE is registered in the ARCHIVE Registry with: deposit date, original creation date, contributor, document ID, and the reason for archiving.

**Rule 3 — Preserve Format**
Original files are stored in their original format. PDFs remain PDFs. Plain text remains plain text. They are not converted to Markdown. The Markdown notes in this ARCHIVE are registration notes only — they describe the artifact, not replace it.

**Rule 4 — Link Outward, Not Inward**
ARCHIVE notes link to the institutional documents that were built from them. The institutional documents link back to the ARCHIVE. No other section of the Vault links internally within the ARCHIVE.

**Rule 5 — Immutability Declaration**
Each ARCHIVE entry must include this declaration in its frontmatter: `immutable: true`
