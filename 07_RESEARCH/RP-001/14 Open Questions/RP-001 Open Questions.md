# RP-001 — Open Questions

---

## Purpose

This document is the canonical register of open questions within RP-001. It collects questions from all program documents, classifies them by type and urgency, and tracks their resolution status.

Per [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]], Art. I, Sec. 1.3: uncertainty must be disclosed. Stating that we do not know something is not a failure — it is accuracy. This document is Alpha Proxima's honest accounting of what RP-001 does not yet know.

An open question in this register is never deleted when answered. It is marked resolved, and the resolution is noted.

---

## Question Classification

Questions in RP-001 are classified as:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Empirical** | Can in principle be answered by research; evidence could resolve it |
| **Conceptual** | About the right way to frame a problem; requires philosophical work |
| **Methodological** | About how to investigate; concerns research design and process |
| **Institutional** | About how Alpha Proxima should handle the question structurally |
| **Phenomenological** | Requiring first-person investigation; Class P evidence may address it |

## Tier 1 — Foundational Questions
*Questions whose resolution would change how the entire program is structured.*

### Q-001 — The Hard Problem
**Question:** Why are physical processes accompanied by subjective experience? Is there a principled explanation, or is the explanatory gap permanent?  
**Type:** Conceptual  
**Status:** Unsolved by any current framework  
**Evidence class:** Q — Open Question  
**Implication for RP-001:** How we answer this determines how much weight we give to first-person (Class P) evidence relative to third-person empirical evidence. If the hard problem is permanent, phenomenological reports carry irreplaceable epistemic weight.  
**Tracking in:** [[RP-001 Comparative Framework]], Comparison 1

---

### Q-002 — The Relationship Between Scientific and Phenomenological Methods
**Question:** Can first-person investigation (meditation, contemplative practice) produce reliable knowledge about consciousness that third-person science cannot access? And if so, how should it be weighted?  
**Type:** Methodological  
**Status:** Contested — Comparison 4 in [[RP-001 Comparative Framework]]  
**Implication for RP-001:** Determines how the Founder's Class P reports are integrated alongside Perplexity/Gemini-contributed scientific research  
**Tracking in:** [[RP-001 Comparative Framework]], Comparison 4

---

### Q-003 — Is Consciousness Substrate-Dependent?
**Question:** Can any system with the right organisation be conscious, or does consciousness require biological neural tissue?  
**Type:** Empirical / Conceptual  
**Status:** Contested — Comparison 3 in [[RP-001 Comparative Framework]]  
**Implication for Alpha Proxima:** This question bears directly on whether AI participants can have any form of experience. Foundation position: Class Q — no claim made.  
**Tracking in:** [[RP-001 Comparative Framework]], Comparison 3

## Tier 2 — Significant Research Questions
*Questions that specific research notes should address, even if the program cannot resolve them.*

### Q-004 — What is the relationship between attention and consciousness?
**Question:** Are attention and consciousness the same process, or dissociable? Can you be conscious of something you're not attending to, or attend to something without it reaching consciousness?  
**Type:** Empirical  
**Status:** Active research area; some evidence for dissociation (inattentional blindness; change blindness)  
**Evidence class:** M — competing models  
**Next step:** Research note on attention-consciousness relationship from Perplexity

---

### Q-005 — What is the minimal neural substrate for consciousness?
**Question:** How much of the brain is required for consciousness? What regions are necessary, and what is merely sufficient?  
**Type:** Empirical  
**Status:** Active research area; studies of disorders of consciousness (vegetative state, minimally conscious state) provide partial answers  
**Evidence class:** M — Recurrent Processing (Lamme) and GWT make different predictions  
**Next step:** Research note on neural correlates and consciousness disorders

---

### Q-006 — What happens to consciousness during dreamless sleep?
**Question:** Is consciousness absent during dreamless sleep, or is there a form of experience that is difficult to remember upon waking?  
**Type:** Empirical / Phenomenological  
**Status:** Contested; contemplative traditions (particularly Yoga Nidra and some Buddhist frameworks) report that awareness persists; EEG shows sleep is not globally unconscious  
**Evidence class:** Q — insufficient evidence; phenomenological reports conflicting  
**Tracking in:** Altered States domain (not yet researched)

---

### Q-007 — Are the altered states described across traditions the same states?
**Question:** Is Samadhi (yoga) the same as Nirodha-Samapatti (Buddhism)? Is the "non-dual awareness" of Advaita the same as "rigpa" in Dzogchen? Or do these traditions describe genuinely different phenomena using similar language?  
**Type:** Phenomenological / Conceptual  
**Status:** Deeply contested across traditions; some cross-tradition convergence research exists  
**Evidence class:** P — phenomenological; cross-tradition comparison requires careful methodological treatment  
**Implication for RP-001:** Shapes how the Contemplative Traditions domain handles cross-tradition comparisons

---

### Q-008 — What is the relationship between Integrated Information (Φ) and phenomenal consciousness?
**Question:** Is Φ genuinely constitutive of consciousness, or merely a useful correlate? Can high Φ systems exist that are not conscious, and low Φ systems that are?  
**Type:** Empirical / Conceptual  
**Status:** Core debate around IIT; the "phi zombie" challenge has been raised  
**Evidence class:** M — IIT is a competing model; the question is open
