# Office Map

## Purpose

Define jurisdiction for all fourteen Constitutional Offices. This is the consolidated definition; an individual Office may later receive its own full charter (`../TEMPLATES/office-charter.template.md`) as a separate document — none has been split out yet, so this map is authoritative until that happens.

## Reading This Map

Companion names never appear in an Office's own name (per instruction) — they appear only in each Office's **Related Companions** field. Where an Office has no assigned Companion, that is stated explicitly, not left blank.

| Field | Value |

| Jurisdiction | Ecological literacy, plant knowledge, gardening, food growing, fungi, mushroom identification, crystals/minerals, phytotherapy, naturopathic knowledge, traditional formulas, survival knowledge, preservation of practical nature knowledge. |

| Current Status | Jurisdiction ratified with explicit safety requirements; no dedicated folder yet. |
| Future Scope | Formal identification-content review protocol with the Ethics & Evidence Council. |

**Safety Rules — Office of Nature Systems (binding, not advisory):**
- Every plant, fungi, or mineral identification claim states its confidence level and the risk of misidentification explicitly.
- Poisonous plants and toxic mushrooms are never described without an explicit, prominent toxicity warning adjacent to the description.
- Any phytotherapy or naturopathic content that could interact with medication states drug-herb interaction risk explicitly, or states that this has not been assessed.
- No naturopathic formula or traditional remedy is presented as a substitute for medical treatment.
- Dosage information, where given at all, is flagged with explicit uncertainty — this Office does not issue dosing guidance with clinical confidence.
- Protected species and ecological-ethics constraints (harvesting limits, endangered status) are stated wherever foraging or collection is discussed.
