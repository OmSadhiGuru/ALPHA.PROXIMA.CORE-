# Canonical Terminology Register

---

## Purpose

This Register is the authoritative vocabulary of the Alpha Proxima Foundation. Every term defined here has exactly one canonical meaning within institutional documentation. Deprecated synonyms are listed so that existing documents can be updated over time. New documents must use canonical terms from their creation date.

This Register does not impose retroactive rewriting obligations. It does impose prospective usage obligations: from the ratification date of this document, all new institutional documents shall use canonical terminology.

---

## Institutional Principle

**One term. One meaning. One location.**

When the same concept appears in multiple documents under different names, institutional memory fractures. This Register prevents that fracture by establishing canonical terms in advance of ambiguity.

When a new document uses a non-canonical term for an established concept, that is a documentation debt — not an error — and should be registered for correction at the next scheduled review.

---

## Term Register

Each entry follows this structure:

> **Canonical Term** | *Scope* | Definition | Relationships | Preferred Usage | Deprecated Synonyms | Constitutional Location

### ALPHA PROXIMA FOUNDATION

**Scope:** Institutional identity

**Definition:** The institution itself — the permanent, named entity with a mission, constitutional authority, governance structures, and an accumulated body of knowledge. The Foundation is not defined by any technology it uses, any individual who leads it, or any program it operates. It persists across all of these.

**Relationships:** Contains all Institutions, Offices, Programs, Workflows, Artifacts, and Reasoning Engines. Reports to no external authority.

**Preferred Usage:** Use "the Foundation" in most internal documents; "Alpha Proxima Foundation" in formal or external-facing documents; "Alpha Proxima" in casual references.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "Alpha Proxima Core" (used in earliest documents; preserved in historical records); "the ecosystem"; "the project"

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book I - The Constitution]], Article I.1

---

### INSTITUTION

**Scope:** Organizational identity layer

**Definition:** An organized, governed, purpose-driven entity within or equivalent to the Foundation. An Institution has a mission, a governance structure, an enduring identity, and a defined relationship to the Foundation. Alpha Proxima Foundation is itself an institution. Future affiliated bodies — if constituted with their own charters — would also be institutions.

**Relationships:** The Foundation is the primary institution. Future satellite institutions or affiliated bodies would be subordinate institutions.

**Preferred Usage:** Use when describing the Foundation as a whole in its organized, governed sense. "We are building an institution" = we are building something with durable identity, governance, and memory.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "organization," "project," "initiative" (when used to mean the Foundation itself)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book I - The Constitution]], Article I.1

---

### INSTITUTE

**Scope:** Disciplinary organizational unit (future)

**Definition:** A named, chartered, domain-specific organizational unit within the Foundation that operates with significant autonomy, its own research agenda, and a defined relationship to the Cognitive Architecture. An Institute is more autonomous than an Office and more specific than the Foundation as a whole.

**Relationships:** Subordinate to the Foundation; may have its own charter, programs, and governance.

**Preferred Usage:** Reserved for future use — when the Foundation constitutes domain-specific bodies (e.g., "The Alpha Proxima Institute for Cognitive Systems"). Currently no Institutes are active.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** None — term is being introduced, not replacing an earlier term

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book II - Governance Framework]] (Working Groups provision is the current analog; Institutes would require Class I constitutional addition)

---

### OFFICE

**Scope:** Permanent institutional organizational unit

**Definition:** A permanent institutional unit with a defined mission, authority, inputs, outputs, and review cycle. An Office is the organizational expression of one or more cognitive functions. Offices are permanent — they outlast the programs they run and the reasoning engines that serve them.

**Relationships:** Each Office serves one or more Cognitive Functions; runs Programs; produces Artifacts; may use Reasoning Engines.

**Preferred Usage:** Use "Office" for permanent institutional units with their own charters. Examples: Executive Office, Research Intelligence Office, Engineering Office, Institutional Observatory.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "Department" (the term used in the founding documents for SOHMA, ATHENA, VORTEX, JERANIUM — these are Cognitive Functions / Domain Interfaces, not fully independent Offices; the terminology is being clarified over time); "team"; "unit"

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article VII

**Note:** The existing LUMIAION "departments" (SOHMA, ATHENA, VORTEX, JERANIUM) are better understood as Cognitive Functions or Domain Interfaces than as institutional Offices. Their charters remain valid; the terminology is being refined, not invalidated.

---

### EXECUTIVE OFFICE

**Scope:** Strategic translation function

**Definition:** The specific institutional office responsible for translating the Founder's strategic direction into institutional priorities, cross-office coordination, and operational execution. The Executive Office is the operational apex of the Foundation — not a governance body (that is the Executive Council) but an operational coordinator.

**Relationships:** Reports to Founder; coordinates all Offices; owns the institutional roadmap.

**Preferred Usage:** "Executive Office" — always with this precise name to distinguish from the Executive Council.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** None — this is a new canonical term introduced in Epoch III

**Constitutional Location:** [[Executive Office Charter]]; [[Book II - Governance Framework]], Article III (Executive Council — the governance counterpart)

---

### COGNITIVE FUNCTION

**Scope:** Permanent institutional capability

**Definition:** A permanent, named institutional capability that the Foundation requires to operate. Cognitive functions are defined by their responsibility, not by the technology serving them. They exist as long as the Foundation requires them. Examples: Research Intelligence, Institutional Architecture, Knowledge Architecture, Environmental Observation.

**Relationships:** Each Cognitive Function is served by one or more Reasoning Engines; is operationalized by one or more Offices; is governed by the Cognitive Council.

**Preferred Usage:** "The Research Intelligence cognitive function" or "LUMIAION serves the Institutional Architecture cognitive function."

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "AI role," "Chief Architect role" (these are the same concept; terminology is being updated to emphasize the institutional function over the AI execution), "capability"

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article II and V

---

### DOMAIN INTERFACE

**Scope:** Specialized cognitive function boundary

**Definition:** The point at which a Cognitive Function interfaces with a specific knowledge domain. Domain Interfaces are where general institutional intelligence meets specialized domain expertise. Examples: SOHMA as the Domain Interface for consciousness, symbolism, and metaphysical inquiry; ATHENA as the Domain Interface for biology, health, and empirical science.

**Relationships:** Domain Interfaces are a subset of Cognitive Functions; they are served by specialized Reasoning Engines optimized for their domain.

**Preferred Usage:** Use "Domain Interface" when describing how LUMIAION connects to specialized domains through its departments. "SOHMA is the Domain Interface for consciousness and symbolic reasoning."

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "Department" (partially); "specialist department" — the existing SOHMA/ATHENA/VORTEX/JERANIUM charters use "Department" which remains valid; "Domain Interface" is the preferred term in new documentation

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article V; [[LUMIAION Charter]]

---

### REASONING ENGINE

**Scope:** Technology serving a Cognitive Function

**Definition:** The AI system, model, or technology currently performing a Cognitive Function. A Reasoning Engine is provisional — chosen because it is currently the best available tool for the function, evaluated continuously, and replaced when a better option exists. Examples: Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT, Google Gemini, Perplexity, Genspark, Comet, DeepSeek.

**Relationships:** Serves one or more Cognitive Functions; is governed by the Cognitive Council and AI Council; is tracked in the Engine Registry.

**Preferred Usage:** "Claude is currently the preferred reasoning engine serving the Institutional Architecture cognitive function." Never: "Claude is the Architect."

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "AI model," "the AI," "provider" (providers supply engines; they are not the engines themselves), "tool," "engine" (acceptable shorthand in informal usage only)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article I and VI; [[Engine Registry]]

---

### IMPLEMENTATION

**Scope:** Specific deployment of a Reasoning Engine

**Definition:** A specific configuration, version, deployment context, or integration of a Reasoning Engine. An Implementation is transient — it reflects a particular moment in the engine's lifecycle. Examples: "Claude Sonnet 4.6 via Anthropic API, July 2026" or "Perplexity Pro via web interface, Q3 2026."

**Relationships:** An Implementation is the most specific level of the cognitive architecture hierarchy. Multiple Implementations may serve one Reasoning Engine. Implementations change frequently and are not tracked in constitutional documents.

**Preferred Usage:** Use in technical documentation and Engineering Office records when specific version or deployment context matters. Avoid in constitutional documents — reference the Reasoning Engine instead.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "model version," "specific model," "deployment"

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article II (hierarchy)

---

### ARCHITECTURE

**Scope:** Structural design

**Definition:** The set of structural decisions that define how the Foundation operates, how its components relate to each other, and how it will evolve. Architecture is the most expensive layer to change — therefore the most carefully considered. Examples: Cognitive Architecture (how intelligence is organized), Knowledge Architecture (how knowledge is structured), Foundational Architecture (how technical systems are arranged).

**Relationships:** Architecture precedes Implementation; it governs at a higher level of abstraction than any individual program or workflow.

**Preferred Usage:** "Cognitive Architecture," "Knowledge Architecture," "Foundational Architecture" — always with a qualifying adjective to specify which architecture domain.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "design," "structure," "framework" (when used to mean architecture — "framework" is acceptable for governance frameworks but not for structural designs)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]]; [[Foundational Architecture]]

---

### GOVERNANCE

**Scope:** Rule-making and authority structures

**Definition:** The system of rules, authority structures, decision processes, and accountability mechanisms through which the Foundation makes consequential decisions. Governance includes the Constitution, the Governance Framework, charters, protocols, and ADRs. Governance is distinct from operations — governance creates the rules; operations follow them.

**Relationships:** Governance is the second layer of the Foundation's architecture, after mission and values. All Offices, Programs, and Reasoning Engines operate within the governance framework.

**Preferred Usage:** "Governance framework," "governance document," "governance obligation." Avoid conflating governance with management or administration.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "rules," "policies," "administration" (when used to mean governance)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book II - Governance Framework]]

---

### MEMORY

**Scope:** Institutional knowledge that persists

**Definition:** The structured accumulation of knowledge that the Foundation retains across sessions, personnel transitions, and technology transitions. Memory is institutional — it lives in the Vault, not in any individual session. The Foundation distinguishes three memory tiers: parametric memory (encoded in AI model weights — cannot be updated without retraining), transient memory (the active context window — lost at session end), and external memory (the Vault — persistent, searchable, maintained).

**Relationships:** Memory is the substrate for Knowledge; Knowledge is structured memory.

**Preferred Usage:** "Institutional memory," "the Vault as external memory," "memory tier." In AI contexts: "parametric / transient / external memory."

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "information," "data" (when used to mean structured institutional memory — data is raw; memory is structured and contextualized)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]]; [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article III; [[LUMIAION Charter]]

---

### KNOWLEDGE

**Scope:** Structured, evaluated, and institutionalized understanding

**Definition:** Information that has been evaluated against epistemic standards, attributed to its sources, classified by its confidence level, and incorporated into the Foundation's memory. Knowledge is not raw information — it is information that has passed through the Foundation's epistemic process. The distinction matters because only Knowledge is cited, built upon, and trusted for institutional decisions.

**Relationships:** Knowledge is produced from information; stored in Memory; governed by Knowledge Integrity standards; used in Programs and Workflows.

**Preferred Usage:** "Institutional knowledge," "canonical knowledge," "the knowledge graph." Distinguish from "information" (raw, unevaluated) and "data" (quantitative, unstructured).

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "information" (when used to mean evaluated institutional knowledge); "content"

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]]

---

### OPERATIONS

**Scope:** Day-to-day execution

**Definition:** The day-to-day execution of programs, workflows, and tasks within the governance framework. Operations follow the rules that governance creates. Operations are managed by Offices; executed through Programs and Workflows; recorded as Artifacts.

**Relationships:** Operations are subordinate to Governance; they produce Artifacts; they use Reasoning Engines.

**Preferred Usage:** "Operational execution," "operational layer," "day-to-day operations." The Operations Layer is the Engineering Office's infrastructure responsibility.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "administration," "management," "execution" (acceptable synonym for "operations" in context)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Alpha Proxima Operating Model v1.0]]

---

### STRATEGY

**Scope:** Long-term directional decisions

**Definition:** The decisions that determine what the Foundation will prioritize across a multi-year horizon — which programs to activate, which capabilities to develop, which cognitive functions to add, how to allocate attention. Strategy is distinct from operations (which executes strategy) and governance (which constrains it).

**Relationships:** Strategy is set by the Executive Council and translated into institutional priorities by the Executive Office; strategy drives Program activation and Cognitive Function development.

**Preferred Usage:** "Strategic direction," "strategic priority," "strategic portfolio." Owned by the Executive Council; translated by the Executive Office.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "plan," "roadmap" (roadmaps are tools of strategy, not strategy itself), "direction" (acceptable informal synonym)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book II - Governance Framework]], Article III; [[Executive Office Charter]]

---

### RESEARCH

**Scope:** Knowledge generation activity

**Definition:** The structured process of generating new institutional knowledge — identifying questions, reviewing sources, evaluating evidence, classifying claims, and producing Canonical Syntheses that become part of the Foundation's knowledge base. Research is governed by the Research Intelligence Office under the Research Methodology constitutional standard.

**Relationships:** Research generates Knowledge; Knowledge feeds Architecture; Architecture governs Research direction.

**Preferred Usage:** "Research program," "research intelligence," "research methodology." Governed by [[Alpha Proxima Research Methodology v1.0]].

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "study," "analysis" (analysis is a step within research, not equivalent to it), "investigation" (investigation is the deep-dive function of the Deep Investigation cognitive function, not research in general)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Alpha Proxima Research Methodology v1.0]]; [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]]

---

### ENGINEERING

**Scope:** Technical building activity

**Definition:** The structured process of designing, building, testing, and maintaining the technical infrastructure through which the Foundation operates. Engineering produces systems, not knowledge — though it builds the systems that produce and preserve knowledge.

**Relationships:** Engineering is governed by the Engineering Office; uses the Engineering Intelligence cognitive function; produces technical Artifacts; maintains the Foundational Architecture.

**Preferred Usage:** "Engineering program," "engineering infrastructure," "the Engineering Office." Distinct from Research (which generates knowledge) and Operations (which uses infrastructure to execute programs).

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "building," "development" (acceptable synonym), "coding" (a subset of engineering)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Foundational Architecture]]; [[Book II - Governance Framework]], Article V

---

### OBSERVATORY

**Scope:** External monitoring function

**Definition:** The permanent institutional function responsible for monitoring meaningful change in the external environment and reporting only when that change is significant enough to warrant the Foundation's attention. The Observatory protects the Foundation's attention from noise. Silence is a valid report when no meaningful delta exists.

**Relationships:** The Observatory is an Institutional Office; it serves the Environmental Observation cognitive function; its current Reasoning Engine is Comet; it reports to the Executive Office and Founder.

**Preferred Usage:** "Institutional Observatory," "the Observatory function," "Observatory report." Governed by [[Institutional Observatory Charter]] and Observatory Law 001.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "monitoring," "scanning," "intelligence gathering" (acceptable descriptors but not institutional terms)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Institutional Observatory Charter]]

---

### PROGRAM

**Scope:** Bounded, governed work initiative

**Definition:** A defined, governed body of work within an Office that produces a specific set of Artifacts. Programs are bounded — they have a defined scope, a research question or engineering objective, and a completion criterion. Examples: RP-001, RP-002 (Research Programs); RC-001, RC-002 (Research Compendiums); EP-001 (Engineering Programs).

**Relationships:** Programs are run by Offices; governed by Program Methodology standards; produce Artifacts; may use Reasoning Engines.

**Preferred Usage:** "Research Program," "Engineering Program," "Strategic Program." Use program codes (RP-001, EP-001) for precise reference.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "project" (projects are temporary; Programs are governed and registered); "initiative" (an initiative may become a Program once properly chartered); "study"

**Constitutional Location:** [[Alpha Proxima Research Methodology v1.0]]; [[Book II - Governance Framework]]

---

### WORKFLOW

**Scope:** Repeatable execution process

**Definition:** A defined, documented process for accomplishing a recurring task within the Foundation. Workflows are the operational layer of the governance framework — they specify how Programs execute, how Artifacts are produced, how Reasoning Engines are invoked. Workflows are documented in protocols and methodologies.

**Relationships:** Workflows execute Programs; follow governance standards; produce Artifacts.

**Preferred Usage:** "Research workflow," "governance workflow," "the Source Verification workflow." Documented as Protocols or as sections of methodology documents.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "process," "procedure," "method" (acceptable descriptors; "workflow" is the preferred institutional term)

**Constitutional Location:** [[Alpha Proxima Research Methodology v1.0]]; [[08_SYSTEMS/Protocols/]]

---

### ARTIFACT

**Scope:** Institutional output

**Definition:** Any durable output produced by the Foundation — a document, a code system, a registry, a report, a visual, a dataset. Artifacts are the tangible record of the Foundation's work. All Artifacts must be owned, versioned, and stored in the appropriate layer (Vault for knowledge artifacts; GitHub for engineering artifacts).

**Relationships:** Artifacts are produced by Workflows; owned by Offices; stored in Memory; governed by [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]].

**Preferred Usage:** "Research artifact," "governance artifact," "engineering artifact." Never used loosely to mean "a thing" — in Alpha Proxima, Artifact has a precise meaning.

**Deprecated Synonyms:** "document," "output," "deliverable" (acceptable in operational contexts but less precise than "artifact")

**Constitutional Location:** [[Book III - Knowledge Integrity]]; [[Book IV - Cognitive Architecture]], Article II

## Deprecated Terms — Migration Notes

The following terms appear in founding documents and remain valid in their original context. New documents should use the canonical terms above. Existing documents will be updated over time through the normal review cycle — there is no mandate for immediate retroactive correction.

| Deprecated Term | Canonical Replacement | Documents Affected | Priority |
|----------------|----------------------|-------------------|---------|
| "Alpha Proxima Core" (as institutional name) | "Alpha Proxima Foundation" | Root MOC, Book I | Low — historical; both acceptable |
| "Department" (for SOHMA/ATHENA/VORTEX/JERANIUM) | "Cognitive Function" or "Domain Interface" | Department charters | Low — charters remain valid |
| "Claude is the Architect" formulations | "Claude is currently the preferred reasoning engine for [function]" | Book II, Engine Registry | Medium — update in next version |
| "AI model" / "the AI" | "Reasoning Engine" | Various | Low — informal usage acceptable |
| "Chief Architect" (as identity) | "Chief [Function] Architect role" (role, not identity) | AI Council Registry | Low — conceptual clarification only |
| "project" (for bounded work) | "Program" | Various | Low |
