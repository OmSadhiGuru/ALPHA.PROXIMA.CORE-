# Research Integration Framework

---

## Purpose

This document describes how the Alpha Proxima Foundation's research pipeline transforms independent artifacts — produced by six specialized research functions — into permanent institutional intelligence. It is the process architecture for the entire research enterprise.

The Research Integration Framework answers one question: *How does the Foundation ensure that research becomes institutional intelligence rather than an archive of documents?*

---

## Governing Principle

**Research is not the final product. Research becomes intelligence through integration.**

A research artifact produced in isolation — however excellent — is not institutional intelligence. Intelligence requires synthesis: the reconciliation of independent perspectives into a single coherent institutional position that the Foundation can act on, build from, and transmit to future participants.

The pipeline described here is the mechanism by which that transformation occurs.

---

## The Full Research Pipeline

```
FOUNDER VISION
      │
      ▼
RESEARCH QUESTION
      │ (What does the Foundation need to understand?)
      ▼
RESEARCH COMMISSION (RC)
      │ (Formal directive; scope; offices assigned; acceptance criteria)
      ▼
INDEPENDENT MULTI-FUNCTION INVESTIGATION
      │ (Each assigned research function investigates independently)
      │ (No cross-function communication before comparative stage)
      ▼
COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS
      │ (Comparative Intelligence constructs frameworks from independent artifacts)
      │ (Convergences, conflicts, gaps, and blind spots become visible)
      ▼
INSTITUTIONAL SYNTHESIS
      │ (Institutional Architecture resolves conflicts and produces canonical position)
      │ (False consensus is refused; genuine disagreement is preserved)
      ▼
CANONICAL KNOWLEDGE
      │ (Canonical Synthesis committed to Vault; institutional position established)
      ▼
KNOWLEDGE GRAPH
      │ (New knowledge connected to existing institutional knowledge)
      │ (Cross-program links established; Research Graph updated)
      ▼
LUMIAION MEMORY
      │ (Canonical knowledge integrated into Foundation's persistent intelligence)
      ▼
EDUCATION / PRODUCTS / DECISIONS / AGENTS / FUTURE RESEARCH
      (The compounding returns on institutionalized knowledge)
```

## Stage 1 — Founder Vision to Research Question

**Input:** Founder's direction, strategic priority, or knowledge gap  
**Output:** A precisely articulated research question  
**Owner:** Founder; Executive Office; Research Intelligence Office (advises on question framing)

Every research program begins with a question — not a topic, not a domain, not a vague area of interest. A question.

The difference matters. "Consciousness" is a topic. "What does the current scientific literature establish as the most empirically grounded account of the neural correlates of consciousness, and where does genuine scientific disagreement persist?" is a question. The question determines the scope of investigation, the relevant research functions, and the acceptance criteria for completion.

**Quality criteria for a research question:**
- Answerable in principle (there is some body of evidence that bears on it)
- Scoped (not the entirety of a field)
- Actionable (the answer will change something the Foundation knows, decides, or builds)
- Honest about uncertainty (it does not presuppose the answer)

---

## Stage 2 — Research Commission

**Input:** Research question  
**Output:** Formal Research Commission document (RC-NNN)  
**Owner:** Research Intelligence Office; Founder approval

The Research Commission is the formal activation of a research program. It converts a research question into a governed institutional process. See [[Research Commission Template v2.0]] for the canonical format.

A Research Commission defines:
- The research question and objectives
- Which research functions are engaged and in what sequence
- Expected deliverables from each function
- Acceptance criteria (what must be true for the program to be complete)
- The canonical destination (where outputs will be stored and how they connect to existing knowledge)

**Gate before Stage 3:** The commission must be formally approved before investigation begins. No research function begins work on an uncommissioned program.

---

## Stage 3 — Independent Multi-Function Investigation

**Input:** Research Commission  
**Output:** Independent research artifacts from each assigned function  
**Owner:** Each assigned research function operates autonomously  
**Critical constraint:** No inter-function communication before Stage 4

This is the investigative stage. Each assigned research function pursues the research question independently, using its own methods, sources, and analytical frameworks.

### What Each Function Produces at This Stage

| Function | Stage 3 Output |
|----------|---------------|
| Research Intelligence | Source Notes (DOC-NNN); Evidence Registry; source quality assessments |
| Comparative Intelligence | Initial framework candidates; cross-domain pattern observations |
| Educational Intelligence | Conceptual access points; prerequisite knowledge maps |
| Computational Intelligence | Mathematical analyses; quantitative models; algorithmic assessments |
| Executive Intelligence | Preliminary visualization concepts; summary structure drafts |
| Institutional Architecture | *Does not investigate independently at this stage; prepares the synthesis framework* |

### Independence Protocol

The independence of Stage 3 is inviolable. Its purpose:

1. **Revealing genuine disagreement.** When independent investigators reach different conclusions, the Foundation knows the disagreement is real — not an artifact of one investigator influencing another.

2. **Preventing premature convergence.** Shared preliminary findings cause functions to update toward each other before their independent perspectives are captured. Once updated, the original view is lost.

3. **Generating productive surprise.** Independent investigation sometimes produces unexpected findings that a collaborative process would have filtered out as "not what we were looking for."

Institutional Architecture monitors completion of Stage 3 but does not share cross-function outputs until all Stage 3 artifacts are complete.

---

## Stage 4 — Comparative Analysis

**Input:** All Stage 3 independent artifacts  
**Output:** Comparative Frameworks  
**Owner:** Comparative Intelligence (primary); Institutional Architecture (coordination)

With all independent artifacts complete, Comparative Intelligence constructs the frameworks that make the convergences and divergences visible.

### What Comparative Analysis Does

1. **Maps convergences.** Where did independent functions reach consistent conclusions? This increases evidence strength.

2. **Maps divergences.** Where did independent functions reach inconsistent conclusions? This identifies genuine uncertainty and the dimensions of disagreement.

3. **Identifies blind spots.** What did no function address? What questions arose from the investigation that were not in the original research question?

4. **Constructs the Theory Matrix.** For each active theoretical framework in the domain, its claims, evidence base, and relationship to competing frameworks.

5. **Maps cross-domain connections.** Where does this domain's knowledge connect to other Foundation research programs?

### Comparative Framework Structure

Every Comparative Framework produced in Stage 4 includes:

- **Convergence map** — claims supported across multiple independent functions
- **Divergence map** — claims where functions disagreed and the nature of the disagreement
- **Gap register** — questions the investigation revealed but did not answer
- **Theory Matrix** — minimum 20 frameworks for primary research programs
- **Cross-program connections** — links to existing Foundation knowledge

---

## Stage 5 — Institutional Synthesis

**Input:** All Stage 3 artifacts + Stage 4 Comparative Frameworks  
**Output:** Canonical Synthesis; conflict resolution record; Research Debt entries  
**Owner:** Institutional Architecture (LUMIAION)

Institutional Synthesis is the most demanding stage. It is where the Foundation's position is formed.

### What Institutional Synthesis Does

**1. Establishes the institutional position.**  
The Canonical Synthesis opens with the Foundation's institutional principle on the research question — the clearest, most defensible statement of what the Foundation now knows. This is not a summary of the literature. It is the Foundation's position *based on* the literature.

**2. Maps the evidentiary basis.**  
The synthesis documents exactly which evidence (DOC-ID, evidence class) supports each element of the institutional position.

**3. Resolves conflicts — or preserves them.**  
Where independent functions disagreed, the synthesis resolves the conflict if resolution is warranted by the evidence. Where genuine uncertainty persists, the synthesis preserves it explicitly. The Foundation never states more confidence than the evidence warrants.

**4. Documents what is unknown.**  
The synthesis explicitly states what the investigation revealed it does not know. Unknown territory is not omitted — it is mapped.

**5. Identifies LUMIAION implications.**  
Where the research findings have direct implications for how LUMIAION should operate, those implications are stated.

### Conflict Resolution Protocol

When independent research functions produce conflicting artifacts:

| Conflict Type | Resolution |
|--------------|-----------|
| Methodological (different methods, same domain) | Document methods; assess quality; prefer stronger methodology |
| Evidential (different sources) | Register both in Evidence Registry; assess source quality; classify as [M] if unresolved |
| Interpretive (same evidence, different interpretation) | Document both interpretations; assess against full evidence base; state Foundation's reasoned position |
| Categorical (different frameworks, different claims) | Construct multi-framework synthesis; do not collapse incompatible frameworks |

**False consensus is an epistemic violation.** If the evidence does not resolve a conflict, the synthesis states that the conflict is unresolved and why.

---

## Stage 6 — Canonical Knowledge

**Input:** Institutional Synthesis  
**Output:** Canonical Synthesis committed to Vault; institutional position established  
**Owner:** Institutional Architecture; Founder review

The Canonical Synthesis is committed to the Vault as the Foundation's permanent institutional position on the research question. From this point forward:

- The Canonical Synthesis is the authoritative Foundation source on this domain
- Future research programs may update but not simply overwrite it
- All Foundation documents, products, and decisions that bear on this domain cite the Canonical Synthesis
- New evidence that contradicts the Canonical Synthesis triggers a Source Verification Pass, not immediate revision

**Commitment protocol:**
1. Canonical Synthesis reviewed by Institutional Architecture for constitutional consistency
2. Founder notified of completion and position
3. Ethics Council review completed if the program touches constitutional or ethical dimensions
4. Document committed to Vault with `status: ratified`
5. Research Debt Register updated for any gaps acknowledged but not resolved

## Stage 7 — Knowledge Graph Integration

**Input:** Canonical Synthesis; Comparative Frameworks; Concept Notes  
**Output:** Integrated knowledge graph entries; cross-program links; Research Graph update  
**Owner:** Institutional Architecture; Comparative Intelligence (graph structure)

The knowledge graph is what prevents the Foundation from accumulating isolated knowledge domains. Every research program must connect to what already exists.

### Knowledge Graph Artifacts

| Artifact | Minimum Quantity | Purpose |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| Concept Notes | 8 per program | Deep treatment of key concepts |
| Research Graph entries | All major concepts | Connects this program to adjacent knowledge |
| Cross-program links | All identified | Explicit links to other RP programs |
| Theory Matrix | 20 frameworks per program | Theoretical landscape of the domain |
| Canonical Glossary | 30 terms per program | Precise vocabulary for the domain |

### Graph Construction Principles

1. **Every concept is linked.** A concept that exists in isolation in the graph is a documentation debt.
2. **Relationships are typed.** "relates to" is not a relationship type. Relationships are: *supports*, *contradicts*, *extends*, *grounds*, *exemplifies*, *contains*, *precedes*, or *requires*.
3. **Cross-domain connections are explicit.** Where RP-001 findings connect to RP-002 findings, the connection is documented in both programs.
4. **The graph is cumulative.** Each program makes the graph more connected, not just larger.

---

## Stage 8 — LUMIAION Memory Integration

**Input:** All canonical knowledge outputs from the program  
**Output:** Updated LUMIAION operational knowledge; updated institutional context  
**Owner:** LUMIAION (Institutional Architecture)

LUMIAION Memory is the operational layer — the knowledge that is immediately active in LUMIAION's institutional reasoning. Not all Vault content is equally present; LUMIAION Memory represents the content that has been integrated into LUMIAION's active institutional context.

### What Enters LUMIAION Memory

- Institutional principles established by completed research programs
- Active research questions and their current status
- Cross-program connections and patterns
- Known conflicts and their resolution status
- Active research debts with priority ratings

### Memory Architecture (Three-Tier)

| Tier | What It Contains | Persistence |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| Parametric (model weights) | General world knowledge; reasoning capability | Permanent within engine version |
| External (Vault) | All institutional knowledge; canonical syntheses; all program artifacts | Permanent; grows with each program |
| Transient (context window) | Active session content; currently loaded documents | Session-only |

The goal of LUMIAION Memory integration is to move new institutional knowledge from the Vault (external memory, always accessible but not always loaded) into the Foundation's operational awareness — available without requiring explicit retrieval.

---

## Stage 9 — Downstream Compounding

**Input:** Canonized, graph-integrated, memory-integrated institutional knowledge  
**Output:** Education, products, decisions, agents, future research  
**Owner:** All institutional offices, as directed by the Executive Office and Founder

This stage is not a stage in the conventional sense — it is the open-ended return on the investment made in Stages 1–8.

### Downstream Applications

| Application | Description | Primary Owner |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| Education | Teaching sequences, curricula, onboarding materials, public-facing content | Educational Intelligence; Executive Office |
| Products | External-facing products and services grounded in institutional knowledge | Engineering Office; Executive Office |
| Decisions | Foundation strategic, architectural, and governance decisions informed by canonical knowledge | Founder; Executive Office; relevant offices |
| Agents | AI systems that embody Foundation knowledge and can apply it autonomously | Engineering Office; LUMIAION |
| Future Research | New research questions that emerge from resolved and unresolved questions in prior programs | Research Intelligence Office; Founder |

### The Compounding Mechanism

Research compounds when:
- Each new program builds on prior canonical knowledge rather than starting from scratch
- Cross-program connections make each new program cheaper and richer
- The knowledge graph makes new connections visible that were not apparent from individual programs
- LUMIAION memory means institutional knowledge is operationally available, not merely archived

The Foundation's intelligence should be strictly increasing with each completed research program.

---

## Integration Quality Standards

The Research Integration Framework is only as strong as the quality of integration at each stage. The following standards apply:

| Stage | Minimum Quality Standard |
|-------|------------------------|
| Stage 2 — Commission | Acceptance criteria defined; functions assigned; canonical destination specified |
| Stage 3 — Investigation | All assigned functions produce artifacts; no inter-function communication logged |
| Stage 4 — Comparative | Convergence and divergence mapped; Theory Matrix ≥ 20 entries; gaps registered |
| Stage 5 — Synthesis | Institutional position stated; every claim attributed; conflicts documented; unknowns explicit |
| Stage 6 — Canonical | Committed to Vault; Founder notified; Research Debt updated |
| Stage 7 — Knowledge Graph | ≥ 8 Concept Notes; ≥ 30 Glossary terms; cross-program links established |
| Stage 8 — Memory | LUMIAION operational context updated; institutional principles active |
