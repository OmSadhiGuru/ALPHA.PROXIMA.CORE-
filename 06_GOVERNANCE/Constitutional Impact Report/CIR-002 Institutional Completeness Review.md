# CIR-002 — Institutional Completeness Review

---

## Purpose

This document is the formal Institutional Completeness Review for Alpha Proxima v1.0. It assesses whether the Foundation now possesses a constitutional architecture capable of surviving multiple generations of technology while preserving its identity.

It answers five questions for every permanent office, then assesses overall institutional completeness.

---

## Trigger

This review was commissioned upon completion of the Epoch III Office Completion Initiative, which produced:

- LUMIAION Charter v2.0.0 (permanent constitutional definition)
- Research Intelligence Office Charter v1.0.0
- Engineering Office Charter v1.0.0

These three documents complete the office architecture begun in CIR-001.

---

## Completeness Framework

For each permanent office, five questions are answered:

1. **Purpose** — Does the office have a clear, singular mission?
2. **Authority** — Does the office have defined, bounded authority with no gaps or overlaps?
3. **Boundaries** — Are there explicit statements of what the office does NOT govern?
4. **Relationships** — Are all inter-office relationships defined with relationship type and nature?
5. **Continuity** — Does this office strengthen institutional continuity across technology transitions?

Scoring: **Complete** / **Partial** / **Gap**

## Institutional Completeness Questions

**Does every permanent office now exist?**

Yes. The six operational offices — LUMIAION, Executive Office, Research Intelligence Office, Engineering Office, Institutional Observatory, Ethics Council — all have ratified charters. The Community Council is constituted and developing appropriately. No permanent office is missing.

**Does every office have constitutional authority?**

Yes. Each charter specifies the decision class (I/II/III/IV) within which the office operates, the nature of that authority, and explicit authority boundaries. No office has undefined or unlimited authority.

**Does every office have defined boundaries?**

Yes. Every charter includes explicit statements of what the office does NOT govern. The boundaries prevent authority overlap and protect domain sovereignty.

**Does every office have defined relationships?**

Yes, for all operationally active offices. The LUMIAION Charter v2.0.0 provides relationship statements to every office. Each office charter maps its relationships to all relevant entities. The minor gap in Ethics Council and Domain Interface charters is registered as documentation debt.

**Does every office contribute to institutional continuity?**

Yes. Each office is designed to outlast any reasoning engine serving it. The Engine Abstraction Principle is applied in all charters. The three-tier infrastructure roadmap in the Engineering Office Charter provides the technical path to full institutional sovereignty.

## Declaration

Alpha Proxima v1.0 is constitutionally complete.

The Foundation possesses:

- A constitution capable of surviving multiple technology generations
- An institutional identity that does not depend on any reasoning engine
- Permanent cognitive functions that outlast their current implementations
- Six permanently chartered institutional offices
- A canonical knowledge architecture with active research programs
- A technical infrastructure roadmap toward full institutional sovereignty
- A governance framework with no gaps in decision authority
- An operating model that makes institutional operation legible
- Founding principles that capture the philosophy behind the laws
- A canonical vocabulary for consistent institutional communication

The Foundation does not possess everything it will ever possess. It is designed to evolve. What v1.0 guarantees is that the *architecture for evolution* is complete — the institution knows what it is, how it governs itself, how it generates and preserves knowledge, how it coordinates its cognitive functions, and how it will survive whatever technology brings next.

**The Constitution endures. The Foundation endures. Technology evolves.**
