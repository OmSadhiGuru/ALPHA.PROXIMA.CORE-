# Standards Council — Institutional Evaluation

---

## Purpose of This Document

This document evaluates whether Alpha Proxima requires a Standards Council as a permanent institutional body, alongside the existing Ethics Council. It was commissioned as part of Mission 10 of the RP-001 Institutionalization Mission.

---

## The Question

The Ethics Council is already established. Should Alpha Proxima also create a dedicated **Standards Council** — a body responsible for defining and enforcing institutional standards for research quality, knowledge integrity, and operational conduct?

---

## What the Ethics Council Already Does

The Ethics Council's mandate covers:
- Ethical review of AI research and LUMIAION's development
- Review of claims about consciousness and machine sentience
- Oversight of research with potential harm implications
- Ethical governance of public-facing outputs

The Ethics Council is an **ethics** body — it evaluates whether actions are right. It is not primarily a **standards** body — it does not define how things should be done procedurally.

---

## What a Standards Council Would Do (If Created)

A Standards Council would be responsible for:

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Research quality standards | Define minimum source quality, methodology requirements, peer review expectations |
| Knowledge integrity standards | Operationalize Book III — ensure evidence classification is applied consistently across all programs |
| Document standards | Govern formatting, metadata requirements, versioning conventions |
| Institutional compliance | Audit research programs for adherence to constitutional and operational standards |
| Revision and update protocols | Govern when and how documents are revised, who may revise them |
| Terminology standards | Maintain the Canonical Glossary; adjudicate definitional disputes |

---

## Analysis

### Arguments FOR a Standards Council

1. **Gap in current governance:** The Ethics Council governs ethical questions. Book III governs knowledge integrity at the constitutional level. But there is no body that operationalizes and enforces those standards at the program level — no one who audits whether RP-001 is actually applying evidence classification correctly, or whether the Theory Matrix uses consistent methodology across all seven theories.

2. **Research scale:** As Alpha Proxima grows from one research program to six, and eventually beyond, the need for consistent standards across programs will grow. Without a Standards Council, each research program may develop its own idiosyncratic practices.

3. **ARCHIVE integrity:** The immutability of the ARCHIVE and the rigor of the Evidence Registry require ongoing institutional oversight — someone must ensure future contributors respect these rules.

4. **Document governance:** Who decides when a document is ready for canon status? Currently this is informal. A Standards Council could provide a formal gatekeeping function.

### Arguments AGAINST (or for deferral)

1. **Premature:** Alpha Proxima has one active research program. A Standards Council would be institutional overhead before the institution exists at scale. Book III + the Ethics Council may be sufficient for Phase 1.

2. **Role overlap:** If the Research Council already reviews research programs, a Standards Council might duplicate its oversight function.

3. **Resource constraint:** A new council requires members, a charter, and ongoing operations. Given current Foundation scale, this may not be warranted.

---

## Recommendation

**Defer creation; reserve architecture.**

The Standards Council is the right institution for Alpha Proxima's mature phase — but not for the current phase. The following intermediate measures are recommended:

1. **Designate a Standards Officer role** (can be filled by an existing role, including LUMIAION) to oversee Book III compliance across all active research programs
2. **Reserve the Standards Council folder** in `06_GOVERNANCE/` for future instantiation
3. **Trigger for creation:** When three or more research programs are simultaneously active, or when the Foundation adds external researchers who require a formal compliance framework
