# Experience Modes

## Purpose

Define the three interface modes required by Constitution Article IX, and the rule governing when each applies.

## The Three Modes

### 1. Symbolic Mode
The Axiom Nexus body/silhouette interface (`axiom-nexus-symbolic-interface.md`). Optional — a user may choose it or leave it. Routes through Companion portals (`companion-portal-system.md`) — never a direct route to an Office.

### 2. Neural Mode
A direct, structured interface presenting the same underlying content without symbolic/metaphor framing — list-, card-, or graph-based rather than silhouette-based. **Constitutionally required** as an always-available alternative to Symbolic Mode (Constitution Art. IX, §4). May access **Offices, the Knowledge Graph, and Companions directly** — it is not required to route through a Companion portal the way Symbolic Mode does. Serves users who prefer direct interaction, and is the accessibility fallback required by `../DESIGN/accessibility-standard.md`, §3.

### 3. Professional Mode
An interface oriented to coaching, clinical-adjacent, or business contexts (Office of Health, Office of Enterprise) where symbolic framing would be inappropriate — e.g., a practitioner reviewing a client's Office of Health content, or a partner reviewing Office of Enterprise dashboards. **Constitutionally required** alongside Neural Mode (Constitution Art. IX, §4). May access **Offices, dashboards, records, and analytics directly**.

## Mode Selection Rule — Ratified 2026-07-09

1. A user may switch modes at any time; no content is mode-exclusive except the silhouette/chakra visualization itself, which exists only in Symbolic Mode.
2. **Symbolic Mode routes through Companion portals.** Neural and Professional Mode do not require this routing and may reach institutional substance directly, per each mode's definition above.
3. Every Companion, Office, and knowledge object reachable in Symbolic Mode must also be reachable in Neural Mode. Professional Mode's scope may be narrower (business/health-oriented) by design, not by omission.
4. Medical Safety Note and Ethics Review Required callouts (`../DESIGN/callout-standard.md`) apply identically in all three modes — mode does not change what governance requires, only how it is presented.
